              Teaching Strategies is a dynamic early childhood education company that provides the most innovative and effective curriculum, assessment, professional development, and family connection resources to programs serving children from birth through kindergarten. With ground-breaking solutions and a strong belief that the most powerful way to impact child outcomes is to improve teacher effectiveness, Teaching Strategies has been supporting the critical work of early childhood educators for over 25 years.
              As a company, our philosophy is that the best outcomes result from working together as a team. Located in downtown Bethesda, MD, our company's headquarters are within steps from Bethesda Row and the Bethesda Metro stop. Candidates who fit with our values, vision, and mission will find a casual and fun work environment that fosters creativity, innovation and career growth. Along with great benefits and competitive compensation packages, we at Teaching Strategies pride ourselves on the impact we have on the early childhood field through supporting teachers who are doing the most important work there is - teaching children to become creative, confident thinkers.
              Position overview:
              WANTED: Serious data geeks. Teaching Strategies is looking for a highly talented, innovative Data Scientist to join its technology development team. We need someone with extensive experience aggregating data across multiple systems and transforming the vast amount of data into valuable information for our customers. This team member will be instrumental in defining and realizing future data products for Teaching Strategies. We're looking for technologists that want to make a real impact in early childhood education.
              Specific roles and responsibilities:
               Designs and develops large datastores of aggregated data from disparate systems
               Discovers meaningful patterns in the data
               Applies advanced technologies including machine learning and map reduce to quickly process vast amounts of data
               Defines best practices for secure data management
               Works with business stakeholders to define and execute on business plans based on the data
              Qualifications:
               Bachelor's or Master's degree in Math, Computer Science, or a related field
               Minimum of five (5) years of hands-on experience in data science and database architecture
               Experience with SQL databases, NoSQL databases and big data systems
               Experience working with API-based data sources
               Experience with programming languages commonly used for data analytics (R, Python, C)
               Patience, rigor, and the ability to communicate clearly
               Highly collaborative work style
              Teaching Strategies, LLC is an equal opportunity employer of Minorities, Females, Protected Veterans and Individual with Disabilities

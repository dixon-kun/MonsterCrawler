        AM Pierce &amp; Associates, Inc. is a woman-owned small business providing Engineering &amp; Research, Cyber, C5ISR, Program &amp; Acquisition Management services and solutions to the Department of Defense, Department of Homeland Security and other federal agencies.
        AM Pierce &amp; Associates is in search for a candidate who has data fusion and related technical skills to support military and homeland security needs. This position is based out of Patuxent River, MD.&#160; Background in artificial intelligence and machine learning would be especially helpful. The work is characterized by problems for which technical precedents are lacking and/or insufficient. The problems are highly complex with divergent requirements. Solutions to these problems require innovative approaches drawing from a range of technical disciplines. The candidate will work with leaders and engineers throughout government and industry to develop new applications and products.
         Duties/Responsibilities
          Critique technical design decisions to provide constructive recommendations to development efforts
          Provide analysis of design using engineering tools such as MATLAB and Excel
          Understands the purpose of data fusion algorithms and system and how to evaluate their performance
          Can relate data fusion algorithms and systems to mission need
          Understand the SETR (systems engineering technical review) process
          Strong verbal and writing skills to clearly and concisely explain status, findings and recommendations
          Ability and willingness to stay current on technical progress and trends in data fusion and related technologies, including machine learning, artificial intelligence, and computer science
          Able to work well with teams of engineers
          Can conduct research and development of novel data fusion capabilities
         The successful candidate will have the education and experience below:
          B.S. degree in math, computer science, physics, or engineering. (M.S. degree preferred)
          Strong computer science skills
          Strong math skills (e.g., linear algebra and calculus)
          Strong applied math skills (e.g., probability and statistics)
         An Active DoD Secret clearance is required; TS/SCI desired
         Working Conditions
        The physical environment requires the employee to work on-site at the government program office, sitting in a cubicle or at a desk for the majority of the workday.

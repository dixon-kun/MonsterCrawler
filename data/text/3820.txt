        A9.com, an Amazon company, creates powerful, customer-focused search and advertising solutions and technologies. Whenever a customer visits an Amazon site worldwide and types in a query or browses through product categories, A9 Product Search services go to work. We design, develop, and deploy high performance, fault-tolerant distributed search systems used by millions of Amazon customers every day.
        As a Graduate Data Science Intern in Product Search, your work will range from experimenting with new ideas and exploring new techniques, to implementing features used by millions of Amazon customers every day. In this internship you will have the ability to apply information retrieval, machine learning and/or natural language processing techniques to one of the following areas of our search engine: understanding query intent, retrieving relevant results, providing optimal ranking, or analyzing Amazon user search activity to develop algorithms and create reports to drive key decisions. You will have the opportunity to work on one of the following teams:
        Search Relevance: Our Search Relevance team works to maximize the quality and effectiveness of the search experience for visitors to Amazon websites worldwide across all Amazon categories (Fashion, Digital Music, Amazon Instant Video, etc). The search solutions you influence will allow customers to quickly discover products in our vast catalog, but also support exploration of our growing selection.
        Search Insights: Our Search Insights team is responsible for analyzing Amazon user search activity to develop algorithms and create reports to drive key decisions. Areas of work include spam detection, anomaly detection, identification of underperforming experiences, and attribution of purchase events to prior search activity.
        Query Understanding: Our Query Understanding team extracts semantic information from queries and documents, and makes it available to the relevance and user interface teams. Our goal is to ensure that the customer's search experience (whether it's on the web, on a mobile device, or via voice) is perfectly customized to their intent. Within each query we recognize named entities (e.g. brands, authors, artists, titles), identify attributes (eg. color, material, price, and gender), classify queries into categories, and recognize structural dependencies between parts of the query.

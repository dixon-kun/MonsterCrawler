          General Summary
         The Chief Data Officer (CDO) has the primary role and responsibility for RIC&#8217;s data management as well as to develop and ensure the organization&#8217;s data is used as a strategic business asset. The role is one of data strategist and adviser, steward for improving data quality and driver for data sharing. The CDO is also responsible for scanning the organizational need and external environment to ensure the organization has the most appropriate tools and technology to enable cutting edge research and clinical care. The CDO will assess technology investments and provide recommendations to the Executive team.
         The CDO will work closely with RIC executives to align data vision with strategic organizational vision for the organization. He/she will establish and operationalize the vision for managing organization-wide data as an asset to support RIC&#8217;s data-driven culture and decision-making including identifying and managing resource requirements driving the value of the data held by the enterprise. Specifically, the CDO will improve how the enterprise collects, uses, manages and publishes data and will lead the enterprise efforts to track data collections, data purchases, databases, physical data models and linkages between datasets. The CDO will improve data quality and how data quality is measured. Moreover, the CDO shall oversee the management and development of RIC&#8217;s Enterprise Data Warehouse (EDW), as well as be responsible for building and managing this asset The CDO will provide leadership to the EDW team to support the mission and vision of the organization while ensuring that enterprise data is available, reliable, consistent, accessible, secure, and timely.
         The Chief Data Officer will consistently demonstrate support of the RIC statement of Vision, Mission and Core Values by striving for excellence, contributing to the team efforts and showing respect and compassion for patients and their families, fellow employees, and all others with whom there is contact at or in the interest of the institute.
         The Chief Data Officer will demonstrate RIC Core Attributes: Communication, Accountability, Flexibility/Adaptability, Judgment/Problem Solving, Customer Service and RIC Values (Hope, Compassion, Discovery, Collaboration, &amp; Commitment to Excellence) while fulfilling job duties.
          Principal Responsibilities
         The CDO works closely with the executive leadership to address the ever-growing data assets and needs of the enterprise, toward the goal of reducing cost and increasing value. The role of the CDO has these responsibilities:
          Provide vision and strategy for all data management activities within RIC.
          Provide executive-level oversight of enterprise information/data management team, budgeting and initiatives.
          Serve as the overall liaison with business sponsors and executives.
          Organize and implement policies, procedures, standards, structures, roles and responsibilities that outline and enforce rules of engagement, decision rights, and the accountabilities for the effective management of information assets.
          Maintain a central inventory of the enterprise data collections and datasets as well as standardize and automate all future data collections.
          Establish standards (e.g., data naming, abbreviations, acronyms, etc.) to ensure data consistency across systems and to maximize data quality.
          Oversee establishment and evolution of EDW / Business Intelligence technical architecture.
          Establish Enterprise Data Model to depict core business objectives and their data relationships.
          Establish Metadata Management standards and practices.
          Mine the data for new features and streamline uses.
          Establish Business Performance Metrics and dashboards, ensuring alignment with Executive Leadership Team&#8217;s strategy, enabling employees to improve decisions, optimize processes and plans, and work proactively.
          Maintain data security and privacy, enforcing specific security and privacy requirements for each piece of data as it related to corporate compliance mandates, HIPAA and other regulatory requirements.
          Decrease the costs of collecting, managing, and sharing data while increasing the value of the data to the enterprise.
          Develop a team of data practitioners to enable and support a culture of data sharing and repurposing, including data scientists, data quality experts and data architects.
          Provide expertise and consult on all new major data-related or data-intensive initiatives.
          Work to improve search and search experience.
          Work with stakeholders to plan for future data needs and develop implementation plans.
          Ensure necessary systems support for accomplishment of RIC strategic, tactical and operational goals including identification of required technology.
          Collaborate with clinical and administrative leadership to widen perspective of the role data can play in providing clinical, operational and administrative services and ensure strategy and processes are consistent with the development of state-of-art practices.
          Works closely with VP of Technology for delivery of data acquisition systems and solutions needed to capture data to be incorporated into the EDW.
          Administrative Responsibilities
          Plan and oversee the data organization budget, according to the approved Strategic Plan and operational budgetary guidelines
            Develops annual expense, capital and FTE budgets
            Monitors and manages data budget
            Identifies and prioritizes capital investments
          Keeps abreast of emerging technology and effectively deploys leading-edge applications:
            Encourages development of new applications at the enterprise utilizing state-of-the-art systems approaches and advances;
            Collaborates and networks with management throughout the enterprise to enhance services offered through systems approaches.
          Develops cooperative relationships with external organizations
            Evaluates opportunities to work with external providers of data systems expertise and applications;
          Conducts human resource functions as required:
            Reviews and revises functional organizational structure to meet changing internal needs;
            Directs the hiring, orientation, technical skill development, and management of all staff members within the department;
            Conducts performance reviews.
          Perform all other duties that may be assigned in the best interest of RIC.

The Department of Applied Science at the College of William and Mary seeks applicants for a lecturer position to support coursework within the new Data Science Program. We are interested in individuals with teaching expertise in machine learning, data visualization, the use of data in decision-making, and the philosophy of data. The successful applicant will have a proven record of teaching courses encompassing both the computational and communications aspects of data science; having such experience in an interdisciplinary context is preferred. The ideal applicant will be interested in the training and mentoring of undergraduate students in capstone research projects as a part of the Data Science Program, as well as contribute his or her expertise to assist in growing the program. This position is a 9-month, non-tenure eligible appointment. It carries a teaching load of 3 courses during the fall and 3 during the spring semester. The expected teaching load will include introductory courses in the morals and ethics of data science and sophomore/junior-level applied machine learning.

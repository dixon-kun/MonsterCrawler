Located on the center of Long Island, New York, Brookhaven Lab brings world-class facilities and expertise to the most exciting and important questions in basic and applied science&#8212;from the birth of our universe to the sustainable energy technology of tomorrow.         Organizational Overview
        Building on its capabilities in computational science and data management, the Computational Science Initiative (CSI - https://www.bnl.gov/compsci/ ) at the U.S. Department of Energy's (DOE) Brookhaven National Laboratory was established to integrate and leverage computer science, computational science and applied mathematics expertise and investments across multiple programs at the Laboratory. In providing leading computational and data science solutions for BNL's flagship facilities &#8211; RHIC, LHC Atlas US Tier One Center, NSLS II, CFN &#8212;CSI is confirming Brookhaven as a leader in tackling the "big data" challenges at the frontiers of scientific discovery. CSI's mission is to provide 'Leadership in Data Driven Discovery'. We are pursuing this mission by engaging in research into novel approaches and methods throughout the software stack to create high performance analysis and interpretation capabilities for experimental, observational and computational data, the provision of data management and analysis facilities, and the practical application of these resources to critical domain science challenges.
        Position Description
        The research assistant will perform computational research on plasma liners within the PLX-Alpha collaboration. The PLX-Alpha team works on the development of spherically imploding plasma liners as a standoff driver for a plasma-jet-driven magneto-inertial-fusion (PJMIF) concept, in which merging supersonic plasma jets form a plasma liner that compresses a magnetized plasma target to fusion conditions. The BNL team works on the modeling and simulation of the propagation and merger of plasma jets, formation and implosion of liners, and compression of plasma targets. Code comparison, validation using available experimental data, and prediction / guidance of future experiments will be performed.
        Essential Duties and Responsibilities:
        * Develop, implement and test new numerical algorithms for hydrodynamic simulations in the presence of radiation and atomic physics processes
        * Perform supercomputer simulations of plasma liner related processes
        * Analyze simulation data, perform verification and validation studies
        * Organize results for presentations and scientific publications
        Position Requirements
        Required Knowledge, Skills, and Abilities:
        * Knowledge of numerical methods for computational fluid dynamics
        * Skills in C / C++ programming and MPI
        * Experience in performing simulations on supercomputers and large scale data visualization
        Preferred Knowledge, Skills, and Abilities:
        * Knowledge of numerical models for equations of state with atomic processes and radiation transport
        * Knowledge of magneto-hydrodynamics
        OTHER INFORMATION:
        The position will require one travel per year to the PLX collaboration meeting usually held at the Annual Meeting of the Division of Plasma Physics

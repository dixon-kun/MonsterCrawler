          PROGRAM DESCRIPTION
           CBIIT:
         The mission of the Data Science and Information Technology Program (DSITP) is to develop an enterprise-level, consolidated information technology infrastructure that provides exceptional IT capabilities to the Frederick National Labs for Cancer Research (NCI-Frederick/FNLCR) in support of basic, translational, and clinical cancer and AIDS research.&#160; The CBIIT Technical Operations Support (CTOS) Group is part of the DSITP within Leidos Biomed.&#160; &#160;The CTOS team is primarily involved in the operations and execution of the projects sponsored by NCI-CBIIT. Please see
         for details about CBIIT&#8217;s mission.
          JOB DESCRIPTION/BASIC FUNCTION
          Ability to provide expert level knowledge with Biomarker abstractions on clinical trials and will be responsible for developing/growing the Clinical Trial Operations Scientific Data Analyst (SDA) team&#8217;s knowledge of Biomarker abstractions in support of the Clinical Trials Operations Program (CTRP)
          Provides knowledge of structured eligibility criteria and will be responsible for developing the SDA team&#8217;s knowledge of structured eligibility criteria
          Organizes and leads the implementation of key process improvement initiatives
          Assists with Scientific abstractions submitted to the Clinical Trials Reporting Program (CTRP,) ensuring consistent abstraction, curation and coding of scientific data
          Performing Quality Control (QC) reviews on the team&#8217;s trial abstractions, as necessary
          Works closely with the Director, Clinical Trials Operations to provide consistent data quality and to immediately address issues as they arise
          Provides overall guidance to the Scientific Data Analyst team on daily work assignments
          Supervises staff
          Qualifications:
          BASIC QUALIFICATIONS
          To be considered for this position, you must minimally meet the knowledge, skills, and abilities listed below:
          Possession of a Bachelor&#8217;s degree from an accredited college or university according to the Council for Higher Education Accreditation (CHEA) in a clinical, biological/biomedical science or healthcare related field and a minimum of four (4) years related experience, including personnel management experience
          Foreign degrees must be evaluated for U.S. equivalency
          A minimum of six (6) years of progressively responsible relevant experience in clinical research.
          Demonstrated clinical experience and/or in-depth knowledge of clinical trials and clinical trial abstractions
          Works under minimal supervision; requires ability to exercise good judgement.
          Able to problem solve and prioritize assignments/tasks
          Work/projects successfully completed in a timely manner
          Familiarity with area of clinical trials, both interventional and non-interventional, preferably in the field of Oncology
          Exceptional written and oral communication skills
          Ability to work in a busy setting, both independently and within a team
          Ability to communicate effectively with high-level personnel
          Ability to attend meetings as a program liaison
          Willingness to perform tasks that may fall outside of the normal daily responsibilities
          Must be able to obtain and maintain a Security Clearance

         POSITION SUMMARY:
          This position for a Designer IV (Lead Designer equiv.) sits in ADP's corporate UX Center of Excellence, based in Roseland NJ. Reporting to the UX Director this role has three specific areas of focus:
           Advocate for and promote the adoption of ADP's Visual Design Language (VDL) - a fundamental collective of style guides, interaction patterns, heuristics and philosophies.
           Continue the development and refinement of the VDL standard through innovation and convergence of design across different product silos
           Act as an approachable, pragmatic thought-leader for the UX community
          The position requires experienced design and production skills, a knowledge of emerging and prevalent design trends in the interactive industry, and the ability to collaborate around goals and self-manage.           RESPONSIBILITIES:
          As a design innovator you will work with peers and leaders across a dozen or so business groups to assess product adoption of the ADP design standards. It's fun work, and your impact will be broad.
          When you find two different products making similar designs you'll work with them to come up with one solution, when appropriate. This will require hands-on designing and, yes, some production work. You should be able to analyze, study and understand applications, business domain data requirements, user task flows and objectives, and create pixel perfect designs in a fast-paced environment.
          You will be working closely with UX Research/Strategy/Design, Developers, Product Owners and other project stakeholders, act as user-advocate during the development process, conceptualizing ideas that bring simplicity and user friendliness to complex, enterprise design challenges. You will advocate aggressively for user experience quality within the company.
          You will be a member of ADP's UX Center of Excellence, so you'll be a thought leader. The means cultivating a balance between getting stuff done tactically and thinking more broadly about the whole organization. You'll need to be able to shift on a dime, and you should be a savvy communicator - good at reaching out to colleagues and interested in building your network inside the company.
          We'll want you to acquire a deep understanding of our strategic products, of customer and user needs, of our different business environments and of the competitive landscape. You will use this foundation to make good strategic decisions and create appropriate solutions.
          This isn't a technology role, but you will need to understand that different teams use different technology stacks and that this can sometimes impact design adoption of our design standard. You'll inevitably get into conversations about JavaScript frameworks, data sources, stylesheets, etc. so you should be able to speak intelligently to this.
          As a key member of a small team you will promote a fun, supportive, collaborative environment to bring out the best work in others. You should be able to mentor and guide Junior designers
          You will participate in and facilitate UX reviews with product teams, size up this work to our existing standards, and innovate around extensions or evolutions.
          You will gather information and contribute to regular reporting for senior management and product teams to track progress against these standards.

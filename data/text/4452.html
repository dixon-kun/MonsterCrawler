POSITION SUMMARY

The Fraud Prevention Alert Vetting Team (FPAVT) is committed to keeping our customers' assets safe and strengthening our ability to prevent, detect and deter fraud across MMUS through the development and execution of a unified, comprehensive fraud prevention strategy.

The Fraud Prevention Analyst acts as the first line of defense in fighting fraud by researching and assessing suspicious activity to determine whether a matter should be escalated for formal investigation. Fraud Prevention Analysts conduct in-depth research, interview customers and advisors to determine if fraudulent activity has occurred and escalate for formal investigation as appropriate. The Fraud Prevention Alert Vetting Team works closely with its business partners, the Special Investigation Unit (SIU), Compliance and Enterprise Information Risk Management (EIRM) to prevent and\or detect fraud

RESPONSIBILITIES

* Gather and analyze information associated with suspicious activity in order to independently determine if escalation and formal investigation is warranted.

* Provide SIU and Compliance with sufficient information to enable a quick and thorough investigation.

* Developing communication and training materials to help business partners better detect and prevent fraud.

* Partner with the SIU, Compliance, EIRM and business partners to continually assess MMUS fraud prevention capabilities in order to identify and drive improvements to strengthen processes and controls.

* Partner with EIRM and Data Sciences to develop reporting, monitoring and predictive analytical capabilities to proactively identify suspicious activity.

QUALIFICATIONS

* A positive can do attitude and the ability to overcome challenges and resolve conflict independently.

* Well organized with strong analytical skills able to research, analyze and present findings and recommendations in a professional and thoughtful manner.

* Self-motivated individual who can be successful in a fast-paced, high volume and rapidly changing environment, with minimal supervision.

* Seasoned professional able to operate and make decisions independently and under pressure.

* Capable with having challenging interactions with customers or advisors to solicit information in the course of researching potentially suspicious or fraudulent activity.

* Strong interpersonal skills with proven ability to work with and build relationships at all levels in the organization

* Ability to write reports, business correspondence, and procedures

* Experience defining problems, collecting data, establishing facts, and drawing valid conclusions

* Must have experience with Word, Excel, PowerPoint, and other Microsoft Office applications

* Ability to learn new systems and applications

* Authorized to work in the US without sponsorship

* Bachelor's degree required

PREFERRED REQUIREMENTS

* MBA/Master's degree preferred

* Certified Fraud Examiner (CFE) certification or other related certification

* 5+years of related fraud experience in a financial institution preferred

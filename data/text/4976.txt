         The Georgia Department of Public Health is the lead agency entrusted by the people of the state of Georgia with the ultimate responsibility for the health of communities and the entire population.
         The agency is seeking a highly qualified candidate for the position of Enterprise Solutions Management (ESM) Program Director within the Division of Information Technology.
         The incumbent will be responsible for providing direction and oversight of program management work on numerous highly complex, enterprise-wide information technology programs consisting of multiple projects, which includes managing the planning, implementation, evaluation, and support/maintenance phases of the program management life cycle.
         Program Leadership
           Maintain effective relationships with business partners and stakeholders at all levels of the organization to effectively manage the program and ensure the program objectives are being met.
           Work with the CIO and program governance bodies to facilitate project investment decision-making, including project prioritization and selection for funding.
           Manage and leverage the entire life cycle of project investments in the program.
           Define and develop enterprise-wide IT project/program management practices, governance standards, processes and metrics.
           Analyze lessons learned and recommend implementation of new policies, methodologies and state of the art technology to continuously improve the project management program and to increase the successfulness of future projects.
           Manage all levels of projects up to and including large, complex IT projects.&#160; Projects use cross-functional teams, are complex, large, time sensitive and/or mission critical.&#160; This includes the initiation, planning, execution, monitoring and controlling and closure project processes.
           Partner with enterprise stakeholders to ensure effective and efficient project management processes, tools and practices are established and continuously improved.
         Unit Oversight and Direction
           Supervise the work of assigned staff including the full scope of human resources functions such as but not limited to, employment, training, conducting performance evaluations and recommending disciplinary action(s).
           Be accountable for the creation of key project artifacts such as:&#160; detailed requirements, comprehensive scope definition, staffing and work plans, task estimates, project schedule and budget, risk plans, quality plan, procurement plan and communication plan.
           Confirm work was performed to requirements, perform scope verification, document final team performance, update and archive records, conduct lessons learned, facilitate hand-off to program support and release resources.
         Project Portfolio Monitoring and Management
           Measure project performance against baselines, performing forecasting to predict the actual completion date of a project, verify scope and quality, manage change requests, resolve issues, facilitate conflict resolution, measure team member performance, manage financial reserves, track the project budget, authorize expenditures, conduct risk audits, and administer contracts.
           Negotiate changes in project resources as necessary to achieve objectives and timelines.
           Use reporting tools to monitor portfolio activities, and regularly brief senior IT and business management on portfolio performance.
           Help monitor and report on the status of project investments within IT portfolio/programs, ensuring projects:
             Achieve strategic objectives as established by leadership,
             Meet targets and expectations, and
             Adhere to established operating parameters.

        USDA provides leadership on food, agriculture, natural resources, rural development, nutrition, and related issues based on sound public policy, the best available science, and efficient management. Our vision is to expand economic opportunity through innovation, helping rural America to thrive; to promote agriculture production sustainability that better nourishes Americans while also helping feed others throughout the world; and to preserve and conserve our Nation's natural resources through restored forests, improved watersheds, and healthy private working lands.
        The Office of Inspector General (OIG) is an independent office within the Department of Agriculture whose mission is to promote excellence, integrity, and accountability throughout the USDA. The OIG conducts investigations, audits, and inspections in order to enhance effectiveness and efficiency and to prevent and detect waste, fraud, and mismanagement in USDA programs and operations.
        This position serves as an entry-level Statistician for the Assistant Inspector General (AIG), Office of Data Sciences, USDA, OIG. The AIGA is responsible for developing, coordinating, conducting, and overseeing data mining and analytical research efforts of USDA's programs and operations in the prevention of fraud, waste, and abuse.
        Recent Graduate appointments are intended to last for 1 year. This is an excepted service appointment. This Pathways Recent Graduate Program offers candidates the opportunity to gain valuable on-the-job training and experience related to the Data Analytics career field. Recent Graduates who satisfactorily complete the USDA Recent Graduates Program and meet all eligibility requirements for conversion may be non-competitively converted to a term or permanent appointment in the competitive service. Recent Graduates converted to term appointments may later be non-competitively converted to a permanent competitive service position.
        Duties
        The Statistician will perform the following duties:
        * Performs a variety of technical assignments that are structured to provide the incumbent with experience in the application of conducting data analysis using mathematical theory and statistical sampling principles, procedures, and techniques;
        * Conducts data mining, gathers large volumes of data and performs routine, scientific, or technical tasks, projects, and/or calculations while performing work related to developing and executing policies and programs;
        * Identifies conditions affecting procedures, methods, and changes, as well as their application and use in audit, inspection, and investigative inquiries, and changes in OIG policy and procedures;
        * Participates in developing and reporting on the economy and efficiency of agency and program operations and ensures that information is clear, concise, and logically organized; and
        * Participates in the formulation and drafting of a report and presents findings to supervisors, team leads, and agency officials.

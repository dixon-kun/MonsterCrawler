        Client &amp; Clinical Care Office
        The Client and Clinical Care Office provides clinical guidance on policy development and quality measurement for the Department and advice regarding Department services, programs, policy, client and provider relations, and performance. The Office is comprised of the Chief Nursing Officer, the Pharmacy Section, the Data Analysis Section, and the Quality and Health Improvement Section. Description of Job
        This position exists to utilize business intelligence software such as COGNOS or TOAD for Data Analysts to write complex queries to extract data from relational databases. The position writes complex coding to manipulate data to provide actionable information to answer very specific questions around the operations and evaluation of the Medicaid program. The position summarizes the data into understandable reports and communicates findings to internal and external customers. Position will potentially perform work for any unit within the Department, but will have expertise in Medicaid Management Information System (MMIS) data extraction and analysis Position also works with contractors, auditors federal and state agencies.
        Position is familiar with databases including, but are not limited to MMIS (Medicaid management information system), BUS (Benefits utilization system) and CCMS (Community contract management system). The position has an expert level understanding of these databases. The position uses statistical software such as SPSS or SAS to complete research and analysis on Medicaid data. Documents all analysis in format required by Section standard operating procedure. The position gathers project requirements and plans out projects independently. This position will work closely and regularly with many different Department projects and their project managers.
        The position works independently determining the analytical process by selecting statistical procedures or principles to be applied in developing a model or process. Position uses judgment and resourcefulness to tailor existing analytical guidelines so they can be applied to particular programs and will use creativity to combine several measures to produce valid, meaningful results. Position will educate others on unfamiliar and complex concepts and theories used in database software and analytical processes and models. The position is unique as it works primarily with waiver data including claims and service plan, acuity data. The position completes complex claims and quality measure reporting. The position supports Medicaid program staff including, but not limited to staff in the Intellectual and Developmental Disabilities Division in all of their reporting needs.
        Minimum Qualifications, Substitutions, Conditions of Employment &amp; Appeal Rights
        Minimum Qualifications
        Graduation from an accredited college or university with a bachelor's degree in statistics, statistical analysis, mathematics, economics, or in a field that includes at least six (6) credit hours of statistics coursework, AND three years of professional statistical analysis experience in data science, research, quantitative program evaluation or business analytics experience.
        Preferred Qualification
        * Masters or Doctoral degree in a quantitative field
        * Professional work experience in health care analytics.
        * Working knowledge of Medicaid or the health insurance industry.
        * Advanced knowledge of relational databases, querying complex data, SQL or other programming language.
        * Experience with advanced business intelligence reporting including dashboards.
        * Experience with TOAD for Data Analysts, COGNOS, Microsoft Access and Excel SAS, SPSS or Tableau.
        * Experience working with, negotiating and influencing executive-level staff and other high-profile stakeholders.
        * Experience designing analytical tools from the ground up including experience incorporating feedback or research from multiple resources/stakeholders.
        * Highly developed interpersonal skills.
        * Ability to negotiate sensitive situations collaboratively with internal and external partners.
        Substitutions
        A combination of work experience in the occupational field or specialized subject area of the work assigned to the job, which provided the same kind, amount, and level of knowledge acquired in the required education, may be substituted on a year-for-year basis for the bachelor's degree.
        A master's or doctorate degree from an accredited college or university with a statistics, data science or quantitative research methods component may be substituted for the bachelor's degree and, at the agency's discretion, for one or two years of the required general statistical analysis experience respectively.

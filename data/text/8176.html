Enterprise Data Quality Lead

Apply
Description

The Lead of Enterprise Data Quality is responsible for developing a data quality strategy and leading a team through profiling and data quality projects. This person will be responsible for data quality testing within the Master Data Solution, perform data reconciliation to identify data anomalies, and prioritize data issues needing process re-engineering. The Lead of Enterprise Data Quality will partner with the Governance program to deliver high quality solutions across CHRISTUS Health.

The Lead supports the business goals and objectives for the Data Management division, the Information Management organization, and the enterprise as a whole.

  • Lead data quality efforts across the CHRISTUS Health enterprise; Work with business functions, enterprise service delivery office, and IT partners to remediate identified gaps using both manual and automated solutions.
  • Manage and grow a technology-driven team of data quality analysts and developers capable of improving, monitoring, developing, and auditing data quality within the business operations, reporting, and analytic environments.
  • Develop a data quality strategy and quality scorecard metrics to improve and monitor quality across the enterprise.
  • Partner with ongoing data architecture processes and governance programs to deliver high quality, high impact business solutions.
  • Determine and oversee audit functions to ensure data integrity and quality.


Requirements

A. Skills:

  • Team leadership
  • Interacts professionally and effectively with multiple functional areas in designing, developing, and implementing process improvements related to Data Quality activities.
  • Create a roadmap of projects aligning with the vision and strategic direction to enable business success
  • Deliver recommendations to business leadership regarding steps to alleviate deficiencies (such as training needs, monitoring processes, and formalized procedures)
  • Profile source data and determine all source data and metadata characteristics; Design and execute a Data Quality Audit/Assessment; Design, develop, and execute the data quality mappings that will cleanse, de-duplicate, and otherwise prepare the project data
  • Strong attention to detail
  • Demonstrate flexibility and willingness to adjust to changing work environments
  • Partner with Stewards and Business users to resolve Data Quality issues
  • Prepare auditing analysis to identify trends, patterns, and/or system issues that may contribute to coding and documentation deficiencies and risk areas
  • Deep technical skills in Informatica Data Quality tools and SQL (Microsoft and Teradata).

B. Education/Experience

  • Bachelor's Degree in Computer Science, Business, Information Management, Information Systems, Engineering, or Data Science
  • 5+ years in data profiling and implementation developer roles; Ability to profiles sources, design and audit/assessment, cleanse, and de-duplicate
  • 3+ years managing and developing a team
  • 3+years of experience leading a Data Quality Program
  • Hands on development experience with Informatica Data Quality Tools and Metadata Management
  • Experience implementing and sustaining a master data management discipline (meta data, data governance, data quality, processes, technologies) in a large organization
  • Deep understanding of SQL (Microsoft and Teradata preferred)
  • Ability to communicate technical concepts, possibilities, and limitations to a non-technical audience (product managers, business stakeholders)
  • Knowledge of data, and more specifically a data domain, utilized within Healthcare
  • High attention to detail and constructive attitude in reviewing data

About CHRISTUS

In 1999, two historic Catholic charities became one, forming CHRISTUS Health and creating a unique purpose in the modern health care market - to take better care of people.

To extend the healing ministry of Jesus Christ, the mission that the Sisters of Charity Health Care system and Incarnate Word Health system shared for more than a century, is now also the mission of CHRISTUS Health.

Ranked among the top 10 Catholic health systems in the United States by size, the CHRISTUS Health system includes more than 40 hospitals and facilities in seven U.S. states, Chile and six states in Mexico, with assets of more than $4.6 billion.

Whether seeking care in Alexandria Louisiana, or Coahuila, Mexico, patients discover that the healing spirit is alive at CHRISTUS Health.

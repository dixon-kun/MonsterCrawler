        The Johns Hopkins University Applied Physics Laboratory (APL), a national leader in scientific research and development, is seeking a Health Technologies, Program Manager located midway between Baltimore and Washington, DC.
        Job Summary:
        Lead a high-impact Health Technologies business portfolio focused on delivering value-based healthcare, innovative medical solutions, and next-generation functional restoration, including:
        * Assistive robotics
        * Medical imaging
        * Biomaterials
        * Physiological/ Biomarker sensors (Wearables)
        * Telemedicine
        Integrate these solutions in a systems-based context and provide thought leadership in the application of science and engineering to Health Technologies. Pursue, develop, and grow new sponsor relationships, to include MRMC, ONR, DARPA, CDMRP, VA, NIH, FDA, private industry, and others.
        Duties:
        1. Create and pursue new business initiatives to support the National Health Mission Area through regular interaction with existing and potential sponsors. Develop and execute a vision that meets current and emerging government sponsor needs in national health, and leverages APL's diverse expertise across both technology-based and systems-integration based solutions. Understand the sponsor's funding profile, major milestones and future needs. Educate and inform the sponsor of APL's capabilities, as well as appropriate roles for and limitations of a University Affiliated Research Center. Develop and execute proposals, task assignments, and related documentation to address sponsor needs and comply with APL policies and processes. (50%)
        2. Lead and mentor a highly competent team of Project Managers and Task Leaders to execute the work of the Program. Coordinate Program Team efforts to ensure on time and within budget execution of Program tasking. (20%)
        3. Work proactively and collaboratively within the Research and Exploratory Development Department to ensure adequate staffing, capital, and facility resources are in place to properly execute the Program. Develop and monitor program plans and budgets for both APL-internal activities and Project as a whole to support Government objectives. (20%)
        4. Invent the future. Conceptualization and pursue new research initiatives, vision, and strategy to support the Health Technologies Program. Seek innovative new ideas and opportunities that seek to revolutionize health through science and engineering. (10%)
        Note: This job summary and listing of duties is for the purpose of describing the position and its essential functions at time of hire and may change over time.
        Required Qualifications:
        Master's degree or equivalent in biomedical, healthcare systems or related engineering or scientific discipline. 10 years of relevant technical work experience and 5 years of team leadership and development experience. Experience in developing systems solutions and leading projects at multiple levels or experience leading complex projects with significant impact on the organization. Experience in sponsor engagement/business development. Excellent interpersonal skills, oral, and written communication skills.
        Desired Qualifications:
        MBA with experience in business development. Exceptional track record of maturing technology in data science and/or healthcare applications. Experience collaborating with external healthcare subject matter experts including Johns Hopkins Medicine and others.
        Desired Qualifications:
        MBA or MS in an engineering or scientific discipline. Solid technical background in health technologies. Experience collaborating with external healthcare subject matter experts including Johns Hopkins Medicine and others.

The Buying team is part of Amazon’s global Inventory Planning and Control organization, and is responsible for deciding how much inventory to carry of every single item offered by Amazon Retail on all of Amazon’s websites worldwide. Consuming inputs including demand forecasts, placement options, and vendor capability data, our buying system automatically computes the optimal inventory levels and re-order quantities for every single item in our Retail catalog every single day, submitting POs to our vendors requesting delivery into our fulfillment network. As a Data Scientist in Buying, you will operate at the apex of multiple advanced Amazon systems (like Forecasting, Sourcing, Placement), Amazon’s worldwide business teams and fulfillment operations teams, getting global visibility of how Amazon functions and serves our customers. You will drive ever-better customer experiences in shopping and delivery. Your curious mind will help create products that drive ever-greater automation, scalability and optimization of every aspect of buying, removing cost and delivering speed of execution to thrill our customers using the best analytic tools Amazon has to offer. The impact of your work will be global, material and remarkable. Our organization is hugely ambitious, curious and impatient to deliver a better experience for our customers. You will have as much freedom to create, experiment and deliver as you can handle. Crazy, disruptive ideas are what we seek. As a Data Scientist in IPC, you will hold a highly visible analytical role that requires interaction and partnership with leaders across the rest of SCOT and Amazon’s Retail and Operations teams. You will be a key point of contact for analyzing and explaining the performance of our systems to Amazon’s leaders in all our worldwide marketplaces, building leaders’ trust in our systems and identifying where better inventory management decisions can serve our customers better. You will work on aspects of inventory management starting from demand forecasting, through product purchase and placement to end-of-life management. Strong English communications skills are a must as you will work with various levels and teams across the US, the EU, the Far East and India. A successful candidate will be a person who enjoys diving deep into data analysis, discovering root causes, and designing long-term solutions. You will help customize business policies and processes while developing new metrics and functionality. The goal is to improve the Amazon customer experience through better selection, product availability, and fulfillment reliability, while enhancing profitability. Key Responsibilities: - Analyze performance of system decisions to identify and correct problems (system or business) that result in incorrect or suboptimal inventory management decisions - Explain system performance and capabilities to business leaders - Ensure that the system is producing the expected inventory management outcomes, and explain variances to plan - Identify opportunities to optimize inventory management decision-making - Develop analytic capabilities, platforms, and pipelines - Develop key metrics and performance indicators to evaluate overall system performance - Participate in business performance reviews to understand trends, develop action plans, and share business plans and performance - 3+ combined years of applied mathematics, machine learning, data science or engineering, or software development - Degree in Engineering, Mathematics, or Computer Science - Very strong analytical and problem-solving skills - Ability to integrate the work of research scientists, software developers, and product managers to innovate and to deliver against complex questions - Excellent English written and verbal communication skills - Experience or good knowledge/understanding of system and software engineering processes - Strong track record in converting data analysis into tangible and significant real-world changes - Experienced working in a fast-paced, high-tech environment (preferably software development) and comfortable navigating conflicting priorities and ambiguous problems - Demonstrated experience incubating new technology while working closely with research scientists, software developers, and technical professionals from product conception to implementation - Proven written and verbal communication skills to convey complicated processes and systems to business partner AMZR Req ID: 524719

           The nonprofit Urban Institute is dedicated to elevating the debate on social and economic policy. For nearly five decades, urban scholars have conducted research to deliver evidence-based solutions to improve lives and strengthen communities across a rapidly urbanizing world. Our objective research helps expand opportunities for all, reduce hardship among the most vulnerable, and strengthen the effectiveness of the public sector.
           Urban seeks strategic, intellectual and entrepreneurial leaders and implementers who can contribute to research and policy analysis of lasting impact; invest in signature initiatives that address issues of national import; and leverage both traditional and new sources of data to deepen capabilities in the areas of advanced analytics, data management, and data visualization.
           Leaders with strong expertise and experience in one of the following research or issue areas will be looked upon to work with Urban experts and partner organizations to achieve outcomes as defined. &#160;Interested individuals are encouraged to submit a cover letter and resume for employment consideration.
             Social Impact Investment
           Broaden engagement of the research community to accelerate social impact investment in evidence-based practices, improving the definition and application of metrics for social impact investment, and documenting and analyzing emerging issues in the field.
             Data Science
           Expand on the traditional statistical tools of policy research to exploit emerging methods that can offer additional insights from data. This often includes machine learning for prediction, natural language processing for text data, clustering for finding hidden structure within data, and parallelized methods for working with big data.
             Immigrants and Immigration
           Advance research in three interconnected domains: the role immigrants play in the economy, the challenges immigrants face in different communities, and immigrants&#8217; access to the social safety net. &#160;Urban wants to develop and assess strategies for better connecting &#160;low-income immigrant families to vital supports such as human services programs and nonprofit providers, and learn how to make those systems work better for immigrant families, particularly those with language barriers or undocumented status.
             Longevity
           Increase public understanding of longevity&#8217;s role in inequality, highlight evidence-based reforms that address disparities in work at older ages, and ensure that debates on social insurance policy address the needs of vulnerable workers across the age span. &#160;Advocate for seniors, workers, children and other groups with a stake in improving preparedness for longevity across the age span.
             Jobs, Wages, and Economic Growth
           Promote more inclusive economic growth at the community, regional, and national levels. &#160;Key issues include the changing nature of work, sectoral and geographic shifts in job creation, workers left behind by the economic recovery, the benefits, costs, and efficacy of policies to increase demand for workers, and broader implications for healthy economic growth, economic mobility, and shared prosperity.

            Understanding of and experience applying sophisticated empirical and methodological research in one of the issue areas described above.
            Desired candidates need not be a researcher but should have an ability to meaningfully engage with highly technical research and create connections between it and other relevant research, social and economic trends, and policymaking.

Job Description

Whole Foods Market is looking for a highly motivated and decisive Manager of Micro Space / Floor Planning to join our Space Planning team. You will be part of a team responsible for delivering data-driven, space planning tools and processes that contribute to the overall productivity of our stores. Above all, you will help shape the strategic direction of space planning by ensuring the best practices, tools and techniques are used.

The Manager of Micro Space / Space Planning is responsible for managing a team to deliver these micro space practices, tools and techniques. This role with drive sales through providing tools for micro space knowledge and execution tools for operational efficiencies.

Responsibilities:

  • Leads the development of planograms for category management and ensures adherence to strategy tactics and visual standards.
  • Works with cross functional project teams including Purchasing, Purchasing Operations, Regional Operations, Inventory Planning, Data Science and Information Technology, serving as the subject matter expert for planograms and space planning.
  • Creates an environment of detailed accuracy in planogram planning and execution.
  • Leads team and partners in ongoing improvement of practices and tools.
  • Works collaboratively to ensure planograms are in alignment with strategic objectives, has a consistent lens of the customer and takes into account operational efficiencies in stores.
  • Maintains a thorough understanding of purchasing and merchandising processes to effectively support the space planning process. Makes smart, confident decisions while working within ambiguity.
  • Manages relationships and budgets with third party vendor(s) for micro space software and assortment optimizations.
  • Manages and oversees deliverables of team members.
  • Exemplifies conscious leadership to mentor, coach and develop Space Planning team members.
  • Consistently communicates and models Whole Foods Market's Core Values and goals.

Qualifications:

  • A self-directed, innovative thinker with a voice and a vision for operational and financial improvement.
  • Bachelor’s degree or equivalent experience at least 5-7 year’s relevant experience in space planning, field retail, merchandising, inventory management, or related role.
  • Minimum of 2 years directly supervising a team.
  • Advanced level skills in Excel, Word, Adobe Acrobat, PowerPoint, product movement software, report writing and query software. Experience or aptitude to learn space management software:  Intactix Space Planning, ProSpace, Apollo, Space Man, Pegman, or Intercept.
  • Knowledge of the space planning process, with the ability to analyze and decipher performance data.
  • Exceptional, proven team leadership and development skills.
  • Excellent communication including written, verbal and listening skills.
  • Excellent visual, analytical and problem solving skills required.
  • Ability to define problems, collect data, establish facts, and draw valid conclusions.  Ability to solve practical problems and deal with a variety of variables in situations where only limited standardization exists.
  • Ability to maintain confidentiality.

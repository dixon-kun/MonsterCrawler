Director of Data Services at GuideStar

Washington, DC 20005

Report this job

About the Job

GuideStar is the world’s leading source of information about nonprofits. The Director of Data Services will lead GuideStar’s efforts to support data partnerships; write, edit and publish reports; drive, strengthen and create data-based products – including custom data sets, productized data sets, GuideStar’s industry-leading Compensation Report, and others; as well as contribute to and conduct strategic and ad hoc quantitative analysis on GuideStar’s extensive data and information to support GuideStar’s sector-wide efforts and internal operations, management and mission accomplishment. This position is central to providing innovative, high-quality data analysis to the nonprofit sector and is a preeminent position in the field today.

Key Responsibilities:

· Drive Research and Actionable Intelligence on Nonprofits and Philanthropy: Using GuideStar’s 2.5B pieces of data, deep institutional knowledge, powerful brand and extensive relationships, develop and execute on a research agenda to help reveal actionable intelligence that drives improvements and insights across the nonprofit sector.

· Manage the Data Services Team (currently two FTE’s), internal cross-department collaborations and external partner relationships to deliver timely and valuable intelligence and strengthen GuideStar’s role as a thought leader in the nonprofit sector.

· Strengthen, Develop & Support Feature, Product and Service Offerings: Work closely with the Sales and Products teams to strengthen current offerings and create new reports and datasets that demonstrate the power of GuideStar’s data and analytics to meet market demands.

· Manage the Production and Evolution of GuideStar’s Industry Leading Compensation Report: GuideStar’s Annual Compensation Report is the premiere offering in the sector. Work to strengthen the offering in order to solidify GuideStar’s leadership in the sector.

· Serve as a Thought Leader & Nonprofit Industry Expert: Write (and work with partners to develop) blog posts, white papers, and other content pieces showing important context, trends, relationships, or other findings from data exploration. Represent GuideStar’s deep and unparalleled expertise in the various forms 990 and the nonprofit sector within government (IRS, etc.), Media, Foundations and Cross-Sector communications, relationships, forums and convenings.

· Help Strengthen GuideStar’s Data Architecture [ : Work with internal teams to inform and support decisions on data acquisition, digitization, and which systems to adopt for powerful analysis.

The successful candidate will have extensive consulting services, client-relationship and research experience; an advanced degree in a social science, business, data science, statistics, or similar field; proven analytical ability; excellent writing, verbal communication, interpersonal and managerial skills; a working knowledge of SQL and software such as Microsoft Access, Salesforce, OpenOffice Base, or R; and, a deep interest in the US nonprofit sector.

The successful candidate will not be expected to perform these duties on their first day, but will have an extended period of training and education with the incumbent in the position. More importantly, the successful candidate must have both the aptitude and the interest to be educated and to educate themselves about these topics.

Five Reasons You’ll Love Working at GuideStar

1. Our People— You’ll work alongside innovative, friendly, and collaborative colleagues who recognize that our combined individual successes make GuideStar the great organization it is today.

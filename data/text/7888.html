Job Description:

3M is seeking a Data Officer for the Health Information Systems Division located in Murray, UT; Silver Spring, MD; Austin, TX; Atlanta, GA; Troy, NY; or Wallingford, CT.

As the Data officer you will lead the effort to identify gaps in our data landscape and lead the effort to close gaps required to provide the most comprehensive view of a patient. You will work with teams throughout HIS to identify existing data resources, establish and manage the data inventory and work to drive product innovation necessary to increase the quality of this asset and its application to solving some of the most important problems in healthcare.

Primary Responsibilities include but are not limited to the following:

  • Lead 3M HIS’ strategic use of data to create new products delivering value to our healthcare customers.
  • Continuously improve how 3M HIS collects, uses, manages, and publishes data.
  • Identify data gaps and work with teams on prioritizing data acquisition activities.
  • Lead efforts to track data collections, databases, physical data models, and linkages between datasets.
  • Define and lead programs to decrease the cost of managing data and increasing the value of the data we have available
  • Improve data quality and establish metrics for how we measure data quality
  • Ensure data is available, reliable, consistent, accessible, secure, and timely to maximize its value to internal and external users.
  • Manage the tradeoffs between simplifying the collection and integration of data for data science to encourage data sharing, and aligning and standardizing data models / schemas
  • Working closely with security and compliance teams, minimize the risks of working with healthcare data while facilitating innovative data science and new application development

Basic Qualifications:

  • Bachelor's degree or higher from an accredited university
  • Minimum of five (5) years in a leadership role (technical or managerial) overseeing large-scale data management in support of strategic business goals
  • Minimum of three (3) years of combined experience supporting or operating big data, data science, and machine learning applications and teams via data delivery and/or data storage systems
  • Minimum of three (3) years of experience defining and implementing security and privacy infrastructure for sensitive data

Preferred Qualifications:

  • Ph.D. or M.S. in Database Systems or related science or data management area
  • Minimum of five (5) years of strong and demonstrated leadership experience and communication skills across executive and engineering leadership
  • Minimum of five (5) years of experience in leading major information management programs in key business areas
  • Minimum of three (3) years of combined experience in big data solutions such as SPARK, Hadoop, MapReduce and/or HBase
  • Experience implementing relational transactional databases, relational data warehouses, and NoSQL databases
  • Experience implementing unstructured text indexing / search against large-scale data systems
  • Minimum of five (5) years of experience in operationalizing data governance and data Quality;
  • Minimum of five (5) years of experience with the major big data solutions and products available in the market
  • Minimum of three (3) years of experience using Scrum, including acting in the role of product owner
  • Expertise in creating and deploying best practices and methodologies
  • Minimum of three (3) years of experience managing and leading technical data teams across multiple departments
  • Minimum of five (5) experience building and supporting big data teams across an enterprise;
  • Minimum of three (3) years of experience with data lake / big data solutions
  • Minimum of three (3) years of experience in healthcare data applications space
  • Minimum of three (3) years of leading large enterprise-wide data governance program
  • Minimum of three (3) years of experience developing business cases for technical projects with a lot of uncertainties

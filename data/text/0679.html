BlackRock's Global Compliance Department protects BlackRock's reputation and provides advice to the business focused on ensuring the protection of our client's best interests. The department is a dynamic environment with the team focused on understanding evolving business strategy, challenging and advising on the impact of regulation in our business, with the objective of:

* Actively participating in business decisions to champion our clients' interests and assist the business in translating Compliance into their daily work and decision making;

* Putting client interests at the center of everything we do;

* Cultivating and enhancing BlackRock's compliance culture;

* Providing thought leadership on the impact and implementation of regulation to our business and to the market; and

* Developing and fostering excellence within the Compliance team.

Job Purpose:

The firm is seeking an individual to be part of the Americas Financial Crime Team based in New York, undertaking investigation of items identified by the firm's transaction surveillance. The successful candidate will need to demonstrate extensive knowledge of, and experience in dealing with, suspicious activity and surveillance systems, and an ability to work as part of a larger team in multiple locations. The role would report into the Global Lead for AML who will be based in New York.

Key Responsibilities:

* Be able to build out the procedures and systems used by the firm to undertake transaction surveillance since the automated surveillance is in its infancy.

* Assist in identifying additional surveillance which should be created and advice on modifying the existing surveillance where relevant.

* Undertake the daily reviews and investigation of alerts generated by the surveillance system and document the outcomes in a manner that would be consistent with regulatory expectations and create a clear audit trail.

* Escalate the items, once identified, for review to the relevant AML Officer as appropriate for the legal entity.

* Produce reporting for inclusion in Board and Committee reporting and more regular reporting to regional stakeholders in the Financial Crime team.

* Work within a global team and communicate effectively to members of that team.

Knowledge/Experience:

* The successful candidate should have 3 years of experience in Financial Crime areas at other financial institutions including experience of undertaking similar surveillance.

* Experience of suspicious activity review and investigation.

* Strong analytical skills.

* An ability to work with data. Data science experience would be an advantage.

* Proven ability to work as part of a team and independently in a fast-paced environment.

* Excellent communication, judgment and business partnership skills.

* Proactive, self-motivated and with excellent organizational skills with ability to prioritize workloads and adhere to deadlines.

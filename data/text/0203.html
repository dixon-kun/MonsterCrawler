Background

Established in 1959, the Inter-American Development Bank ("IDB" or "Bank") is the main source of financing for economic, social and institutional development in Latin America and the Caribbean. It provides loans, grants, guarantees, policy advice and technical assistance to the public and private sectors of its borrowing countries.

The Bank's Knowledge and Learning Sector (KNL) part of the Vice-Presidency of Sectors and Knowledge (VPS) is currently looking for an Open Data and Publication Products Support Contractual.

KNL is responsible for disseminating knowledge, both internally and externally; for establishing the institutional learning agenda; and for delivering activities for staff and counterparts. KNL promotes an institutional knowledge and learning strategy to improve conditions for the acquisition, creation, dissemination, and use of knowledge needed for the effectiveness of Bank operations.

Within KNL, the Felipe Herrera Library (FHL) designs and delivers client-oriented information products for its internal audience of IDB researchers and technical specialists. Additionally, FHL plays a fundamental role in supporting the visibility and dissemination of Bank's created sectoral data and knowledge publications by maintaining the open data portal "Numbers for Development" and the institutional repository of knowledge products (back end of the Bank's Publication website). This position contributes advanced metadata expertise and knowledge on publications processes that is critical for library business. The ideal candidate will have a combination of interpersonal and technical skills that will contribute to the library stewardship on the curation and dissemination of IDB publications

Consultancy objective(s)

* Develop, enhance and maintain the IDB Publications taxonomy of subjects and other authority lists

* Provide support in the effective implementation of current Bank policies for the publication of knowledge products

* Ensure that metadata mapping and interoperability follows standard of effectiveness.

Main activities

The selected candidate will:

* Support the development and maintenance of the IDB's Publications Taxonomy, continuously updating, merging and weeding terms as needed

* In coordination with the Embedded Librarians team, ensure the integration and continuous update of quality metadata in the IDB's Publication Repository required to increase the publications' visibility in the semantic web.

* In collaboration with other IDB technicians and the ITE department, support the effective integration of the Publications Repository to other institutional platforms such as BID365 components, eZshare (IDB's Document Management System), and Convergence (Bank Operations platform).

* In collaboration with the Embedded Librarian team and other publication's processes players, support the application of current Bank policies on the publication of knowledge products, ensuring a consistent approach to policy interpretation and application

* Collaborate with the Publications Librarian in the development and enhancement of the IDB's Publications Repository and the Publications website, in aspects such as improving search performance, user experience, semantic web techniques, and search engine optimization.

* Communicate Bank policies in Publication processes with clients and shareholders via presentations, one-on-one meetings, and other communication efforts

* Keep abreast of technical developments in Open Access to scholarly communications; maintains awareness of trends in institutional repositories and new technologies

* Other duties as required

* Education: Technical studies of associate degree in Library and/or Information Studies.

* Experience: Two years of relevant professional experience.

* Languages: fluent English and Spanish; Portuguese is an asset.

* Areas of Expertise: information management, library sciences, database management, programming, data science, big data analytics, visualizations.

* Skills:

* Client oriented, team player, problem solver

* Highly analytical, proactive, and results driven.

* Passion for innovation and improvement of technologies and practices - not only those that have been part of previous or current projects but also those that are new and/or emerging.

* Ability to adapt and learn quickly in a context of high accountability and policy implementation.

* Knowledge of digital resources and taxonomy management applications (DSpace, PoolParty) and library applications (Aleph, OCLC, SFX, ezProxy, Libguides).

* Working knowledge of publishing and preservation metadata standards such as Dublin Core, MARC, MARCXML, and METS.

* Knowledge of semantic web technologies such as linked data

* Knowledge of Microsoft Office 365 and Microsoft Sharepoint.

* Experience interpreting and applying institutional policies to specific cases

* Experience in preparing and delivering presentations that aim at influencing audiences

* Eager to learn new technologies and experiment with new approaches

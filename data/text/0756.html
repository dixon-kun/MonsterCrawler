Position is focused on geochemical, geostatistical and spatial data analysis for the purpose of conducting environmental forensic evaluations for contaminated sites. This position will encompass allphasesof contaminatedsite management, involving site investigation and assessment, sampling design and sample collection techniques (including specification of analytical methodologies) for riverine, lacustrine and estuarine surface waters and sediments, as well as for upland sites. The position will involve evaluating contaminant sources and origins via statistical pattern analysis, trend analysis, and other statistical techniques, applying customized empirical and mechanistic models to predict the environmental fate and transport of common and emerging contaminants in sediment, surface water, and groundwater; developing conceptual site models used to assess human health and ecological risks; and writing technical reports describing the results of these analyses. This position will include field work and overnight travel.



Required Skills


Required Experience

The successful candidate will have the following:

    • Must hold a master’s degree in environmental science or engineering, statistics (related to environmental science or geology), oceanography, geology, chemistry, or a related discipline.
    • Must have a demonstrated understanding of environmental chemistry ((primarily organic chemistry, persistent organic pollutants, and environmental contaminant fate and transport).
    • Must be adept in the coordinated use of MS Excel and/or MS Access®, ArcGIS, and statistical analysis software (e.g., JMP) to create, modify, and statistically evaluate environmental contaminant databases with an understanding of the mathematical underpinnings of the statistical methods.
    • Must have one to three years of experience consistent with the responsibilities of the position as described above.
    • Must possess excellent verbal and written communication skills.
    • Must be adept with Microsoft Office® programs; rapid and precise data management skills using databases in MS Excel and/or MS Access® is required.
    • Must be experienced in the application of mapping/spatial analysis software, specifically ArcGIS®.
    • Must be physically able and willing to perform field work associated with site investigations and site assessments.
    • Must have a valid driver’s license and be available with reasonable notice for field work on evenings or weekends as project requirements arise.

Preferred qualifications:

  • The following attributes beyond the minimum requirements will be given preference:

    • Experience in isotope geochemistry, radiogeochemistry, and sediment geochemistry, as well as experience in an environmental analytical laboratory with GC or GC/MS organic pollutant analyses.
    • Experience using modeling, and statistical and geostatistical software such as Matlab ® , R ® , JMP ® , and Kaleidagraph ® ; and experience with three-dimensional spatial modeling/analysis tools / advanced statistical tools such as Principal Component Analysis (PCA).
    Current OSHA 40-hour HAZWOPER training and refresher certification and current medical clearance for hazardous waste site work.

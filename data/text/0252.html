KeyLogic is recruiting for a Junior-Mid Level level Data Analyst/Engineer/Scientist to support the Fuel Cell Technologies Office (FCTO). The candidate will work onsite at the Department of Energy Forrestal Building.

The central mission of the Hydrogen Program is to research, develop, validate, and demonstrate hydrogen production, delivery, storage, and fuel cell technologies. Producing and using hydrogen from diverse domestic resources as part of our nation's energy portfolio will ensure that the United States has an abundant, reliable, and affordable supply of clean energy.

Position Description:

The Data Analyst/Engineer/Scientist will assist with formulating, developing and implementing budgetary policies. Provide detailed budget formulation guidance to program managers and officials, both at Headquarters and Field Offices, to include issues involving content, format, and implementation strategies. Assist in identifying, researching, and presenting salient budget issues resulting from independent analysis of program resource requirements, to include the accuracy of program cost estimates and the consistency of program objectives with prior Departmental budget decisions concerning budgets of assigned programs. Review, analyze, edit and consolidates budget submissions and requests for allotments submitted by managers of assigned program areas. Input budget data into the automated budget table system, other systems, presentations and spreadsheets. Assist in designing and preparing budgetary tables and presentations in connection with special analyses for summary presentation of technical budgetary data. Assist in monitoring and preparing analyses of program activities based on Departmental accounting systems, other Departmental records, Treasury reports, and private sector data sources.

Position Requirements:

* Bachelor's Degree in engineering (civil, chemical, electrical, or mechanical), science or other technical fields such as physics, biology, chemistry, earth sciences, mathematics and/or business administration, statistics or computer science

* 2+ years of experience in an engineering or 4+ year of experience in a scientific or other technical field

* 3+ years of experience in documentation disciplines, including technical writing, editing, layout, documentation development and production, and desktop publishing

* Experience with the Department of Energy and/or the Fuel Cell Technologies Office

* Experience with Federal Government contracting and/or experience within renewable technology

* Familiar with the relationships between the Department of Energy, Industry Partners and the National Laboratories

* Familiar with Department of Energy financial systems (STRIPES, etc.)

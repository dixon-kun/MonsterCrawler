About the Job

Wheeler Staffing Partners, Inc. (WSP) is currently seeking a Financial Planning Analyst for an opportunity in Downtown Dallas. If you or anyone you know would be interested, please e-mail your resume to Brandon Price at bprice@wheelersp.com

Job Summary

Financial Planning Analyst will be responsible for analytical support of the Company’s strategic initiatives, capital markets activities, budgeting and forecasting, asset management, corporate finance, and investor relations functions.  Additionally, as a member of the FPQA team, the Analyst will be responsible for supporting other functional areas of the Company’s business, including Operations, Accounting, and Investment Management.

Essential Job Duties and Responsibilities

  • Analytical support of all financing-related activities, including Excel modeling, report generation, and coordinating with the Operations and Accounting teams to review, analyze, confirm, and report on property-level and corporate financial results and operating performance metrics.
  • Participate in, and contribute to, annual budgeting and forecasting processes. Support the creation and refinement of various quantitative and predictive analyses developed in the course of FPQA’s budgeting and forecasting activities.
  • Work with senior members of the FPQA group to ensure compliance with lender and/or servicer requirements and debt covenants, including creation of all necessary reporting.
  • Review, evaluate, maintain, and improve financial models used for capital markets, asset management, managerial, operational, and financial reporting.
  • Track macroeconomic and market-specific performance indicators within the real estate market. Assist with collection, synthesis, and modeling of data in support of analysis of strategic initiatives and asset management decisions.
  • Evaluate potential entity-level and portfolio acquisition opportunities, as well as potential portfolio disposition opportunities.
  • Assist in the preparation of presentation materials for the Company’s Board of Directors, current and/or potential investors, research analysts, and current and/or potential lenders.
  • Assist in the preparation of presentation materials and memoranda presented to the Company’s Executive Committee, Investment Committee and senior leadership team.
  • Other duties as assigned

Education and/or Experience

  • Bachelor’s Degree and strong academic record. Advanced degree (MBA, JD, etc.) a plus, but not required.
  • Minimum 1-3 years’ experience; financial modeling and/or real estate focus preferred.
  • FP&A, investment banking, private equity, corporate banking, and/or accounting experience a strong plus.

Skills/Specialized Knowledge

  • High degree of proficiency with Excel required, including comfort and familiarity with advanced Excel functions. Familiarity with data science tools, including R and Python, is beneficial, but not required.
  • Proficiency with all Microsoft Office applications, including Word and PowerPoint, required.
  • Extremely well-developed analytical abilities.
  • High degree of intellectual curiosity.
  • Excellent organizational skills; ability to manage multiple competing priorities and deadlines.
  • Well-developed written and verbal communication skills.
  • Outstanding interpersonal skills; team-oriented.
  • Self-starter with a strong sense of ownership.
  • Ability to perform advanced mathematical calculations.
  • Comfort and familiarity with basic finance and accounting theory.
  • Ability to read and comprehend financial statements.
  • Ability to maintain confidentiality.

Physical Demands

  • Ability to sit for extended periods of time.
  • Frequent use of fingers, handling, feeling, talking, and hearing.
  • Moderate standing, reaching, walking, stooping, and lifting.
  • Sustains substantially recurring movement to fingers, hands, and wrists.
  • Ability to lift and/or move up to 20 pounds.

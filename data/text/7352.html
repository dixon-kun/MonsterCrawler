TOP LEVEL SECURITY CLEARANCE REQUIRED

The Customer is looking for an energetic, creative IT Training Educator Subject Matter Expert to lead the development of a new curriculum. The objective is to develop and provide the knowledge, skills, and abilities (KSAs) needed to ensure data authorities have the ability to broadly share data holdings within their purview while also being able to appropriately protect sensitive data holdings. Theres no single point on this spectrum that balances these needs for all data sets. Therefore, it is imperative that the right people have the competency to identify the most appropriate balance between data sharing and data protection for every data set. The audience for these KSAs ranges from senior executives making governance decisions, data stewards, data scientists, data ingestion professionals, information technology professionals, risk and compliance officers, and even multiple federal agencies. The Educator will be responsible for laying out a plan to define and scope the full curriculum, define and baseline learning objectives, and develop a high level schedule for developing the curriculum. The Educator will primarily focus on developing and refining the foundational course content. The Educator will work closely with other team members to create learning exercises to reinforce the practical application of skills. The Educator will also participate in classroom delivery of the curriculum along with Subject Matter Experts. The customer is primarily focused on the development and initial classroom delivery of a high quality curriculum. Computer based training is not in the current scope of this position. The Educator will be part of a team of existing IT professionals who have subject matter expertise in the core curriculum. For instructional design, the Educator will set instructional goals, tailor instructional systems design processes, create compelling content, and measure progress toward achieving these goals. The Educator will also partner with customers to ensure program quality and relevance, design exercises and activities that enhance the learning process, and decide on criteria used to judge a learners performance. For instructional planning, the Educator will coordinate training classes, including the scheduling of instructors and facilities, and the preparation of course materials. For instructional delivery, the Educator will apply a variety of presentation and elicitation techniques to deliver a course, or segments of a course; process classroom experiences to ensure that learning points are made and students are able to apply the learning to the job. For instructional revision, the Educator will update course materials to reflect current developments in relevant subject matter, consult with instructors to determine appropriate teaching techniques and documentation, and ensure content updates are consistently applied throughout the course materials.

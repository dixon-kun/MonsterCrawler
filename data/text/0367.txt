        Job Description:
        Introduction
        The Watson Institute for Systems Excellence (WISE), an Institute for Advanced Studies at Binghamton University (State University of New York at Binghamton), is a research-intensive multidisciplinary organization that works in a collaborative manner with several industrial partners including IBM, Google, Innovation Associates, Analog Devices, United Health Services, Mount Sinai Health System, Smart Modular, Sanmina, LiveOnNY, and Montefiore Medical Center. With over 80 graduate students (MS and PhD) and several research faculty and staff members, WISE is a dynamic customer-driven student-centered organization. On average, WISE is awarded $2.5-3.0 million in research grants a year. The Research Scientist we are looking to hire interacts with faculty, staff, students, industry, campus entities, and variety of stakeholders in our research mission.
        Duties &amp; Responsibilities
        Reporting to and working closely with the WISE Director, Associate Director, Assistant Directors, and faculty, the Research Scientist primarily assists faculty on sponsored research projects in applied operations research/optimization and/or data science/mining/analytics with special focus in domains such as health systems and manufacturing/enterprise systems. The Research Scientist is also expected to:
        * Write proposals to seek grants from federal and state agencies, and to industry to expand the breadth and depth of current research activities.
        * Mentor and advise graduate research associates.
        * Publish research findings in leading peer-reviewed journals and at high-impact conferences.
        * Work effectively with faculty, staff and graduate research associates, as well as with diverse teams.
        * Conceptualize, plan, and execute projects.
        * Make independent judgments, not only in project conceptualization and planning, but also in proposal submission, project design, identifying time lines, milestones, and due dates.
        * Prepare and present research-project related findings to healthcare and other industry sponsors, faculty, and staff. Systematically document progress in research projects through technical reports and presentations.
        * Identify new areas of inquiry which would be in-line with WISE's mission and could garner external support.
        * Work on other related projects as assigned.
        Requirements:
        * Ph.D. in Industrial Engineering or a related field by the appointment date; a Bachelor's degree in Engineering is required.
        * Possess in-depth knowledge of domains such as health systems engineering and manufacturing and enterprise systems, with special focus on methodologies such as applied operations research/optimization, simulation and modeling, and data science.
        * Experience in academia, and/or in healthcare systems, and/or in an industrial/manufacturing setting having planned, executed, and have completed various research projects.
        * Demonstrate excellent programming, verbal, and written communication skills.
        * Possess the ability to concurrently work on multiple projects with minimal oversight.

        The challenge
        Adobe Document Cloud is revolutionizing the way the world works with documents. It's the newest cloud offering at Adobe, and a very exciting place to be. The Document Cloud combines a collection of online services integrated with Adobe Reader and Adobe Acrobat. Our subscription base is growing rapidly and we are continually rolling out new features and services. We work in small, agile teams with considerable autonomy and we value engineers with technical competence, creativity, flexibility, strong customer focus and an eagerness for learning and collaboration.
        The Adobe Document Cloud Ideation team is looking for a recent college graduate to join the Adobe Document Cloud's Ideation team, located in its Newton office. We are working on a number of problems relating to document structure, usage, and ultimately new business models and customer opportunities.
        Students with a variety of skills should consider applying: interest and experience in machine learning, data science with experience in front end development. Languages that we will be working in include: Python, R, HTML/Javascript. We will also be using AWS services extensively, e.g. lambda, EC2, etc.
        What you'll do
        * Help us build a large corpus of documents for detailed analysis
        * Help us build our analysis pipeline, scaling into the millions of documents
        * Develop algorithms to understand the structure of documents in a variety of formats
        * Help analyze the performance of various experiments and competing algorithms
        * Work with Adobe's research teams to take the state of the art in research and make great products and features
        * We will also be meeting customers to learn more about how they work and what solutions we can offer. Projects will include building prototypes, validating key learnings, and ultimately putting solutions into production as new features or even new offerings.
        * As a developer in an agile organizations, you will also:
        * Embrace continuous improvement
        * Participate in story mapping, backlog grooming, daily standups, retrospectives, and sprint reviews on a two-week cadence
        * Take responsibility for each other's success
        * Look for ways to continually improve the product and process
        * Work effectively in a fast-paced and ever-evolving environment
        * Learn new tools and technologies
        What you need to succeed
        * Experience with Machine Learning algorithms, programming in R, Pandas or similar
        * JavaScript, HTML, and CSS, JavaScript frameworks
        * Python/Django or a similar framework
        * BS Degree in Computer Science or equivalent
        Making our technology accessible to all
        We believe everyone should have equal access to technology and its benefits. To help achieve this, Adobe is focused on making software more accessible for everyone. When we hire for engineering, development and design roles, we look for the following accessibility skills:
        * Base knowledge of the W3C's Web Content Accessibility Guidelines v2.0
        * Exposure to current tools used by people with disabilities (screen readers and other assistive technologies)
        * Experience with accessibility evaluation and validation software
        * Understanding of input and output modes used by people with disabilities
        * Familiarity with compliance requirements - state, Federal, international

Anyone who has redeemed a coupon, filled a prescription or returned a product, has touched Inmar. We apply technology and data science to improve outcomes for consumers and those who serve them. As a trusted intermediary for over 35 years, we have unmatched access to billions of consumer and business transactions in real time. Our analytics, platforms and services enable engagement with shoppers and patients, and optimize results.


The Inmar legal team is seeking an experienced, multi-disciplinary attorney to provide sound advice regarding a broad range of legal risks and opportunities.  At the direction of the General Counsel and Deputy General Counsel, the Corporate Counsel will advise the business and develop, draft, review, edit and manage contracts in and across a variety of subject matter areas, which may include: general corporate; customer and vendor agreements; advertising, marketing and promotions (including agency agreements, contests, and sweepstakes); services; software and intellectual property licensing;  mergers, acquisitions, joint ventures (including deal negotiation, drafting, and due diligence review); employment; and regulatory compliance (including HIPAA and data rights).  The Corporate Counsel may also assist with litigation efforts, including discovery.

This role will require an ability to thrive at a fast-paced and growing technology company, where strategic thinking and problem-solving is imperative. This attorney will have meaningful opportunities to interact with all levels of management and be responsible for complex projects.


  • A minimum of 3-5 years' experience within a corporate legal department or comparable relevant legal experience.
  • Substantial exposure to various legal issues including commercial contracts, litigation, regulatory compliance, intellectual property, data rights, antitrust, and mergers & acquisitions.
  • Money Transmitter knowledge a plus.
  • Ability to provide legal advice in a straightforward manner in order to help business units appropriately manage risk.
  • Strong customer focus and enthusiastic support of sales efforts.
  • Bar : Licensed to practice law in at least one U.S. jurisdiction; the North Carolina bar preferred.

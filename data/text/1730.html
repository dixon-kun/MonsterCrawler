Implementation Specialist

This position will support the Implementation Sciences Manager to ensure that the integration of our evaluation data findings.

YMCA of the USA

Chicago, IL

The Implementation Specialist (IS) will support the Implementation Sciences Manager to ensure that the integration of our evaluation data findings and other such evidence improve our ability to strengthen, enhance and continuously improve the Evidence-Based Youth Development Division’s suite of programs and practices.

The IS will be responsible for working with Y-USA Technical Assistance providers who consult to individual Ys and groups of Ys that are utilizing Y-USA tools and resources to put into place specific programs or practices.  The IS will focus on applying analyzed data and evaluation results to improve the fidelity and quality of these programs and practices through technical assistance. The IS must identify and communicate to TAs the key features and principles of assigned programs and practices, common pitfalls in their implementation, and solutions to address fidelity, compliance and overall efficacy of implementation.

This role will help ensure that effective implementation of identified practices and programs occurs across EBYD by bridging the gaps between collected data, science and practices.  The IS will support the broader team so that our work continues to improve, refine and develop over time to produce long-term effects and sustainability.

This position will work closely with all members of the Evidence-Based Youth Development Division as well as the Research Evaluation and Data Sciences team and other departments so that effective planning and execution take place.

This position is grant funded, and contingent upon future funding.

This position is located in our Chicago, Illinois office.

EFFECT ON END RESULTS/STRATEGIC IMPACT

The Implementation Specialist will advance the overall strategic direction of YMCA of the USA by enhancing the implementation of program and practices through the use of data and sciences as well as support and evidence-based youth development programs, therebyensuring Ys are effectively prepared to be impactful and sustainable.

  • Bachelor’s degree is required. Master’s Degree strongly preferred in data sciences, implementation sciences or youth development preferred.

  • Minimum two years of experience working collaboratively and building partnerships with staff of local YMCAs to elevate programs and practices, with a proven track record of using collected data to strengthen implementation across a considerable scale.

  • Minimum of 3 - 5 years of experience building strong relationships effectively among a wide variety of stakeholders, including frontline staff and executive leaders.

  • Understands and can translate the implementation and innovation (formal and practice knowledge) cycles of program development

  • Demonstrated ability designing and rolling effective continuous improvement cycles

  • Demonstrated ability working cross-functionally to integrate efforts and produce strong implementation results.

  • At least two of the preferred work experiences and/or preferred education are required

  • Ability to train on how to use collected data is required

  • Ability to hold self to very strict deadlines is required

  • Demonstrated ability to multi-task and manage complex systems with superior technical and project management skills is required

  • Possess excellent analytical, problem-solving skills, and be able to follow all administrative processes

  • Excellent verbal, written and interpersonal communication and presentation skills are required

  • Requires a commitment to the YMCA character development values of caring, honesty, respect and responsibility

  • Review data and work with team members to make data-driven decisions

  • Support technical advisors so they can interpret the data and understand how local Ys are doing with implementation and impact.

  • Support the process of using data collected from local Ys to support the sustainable uptake, adoption, and implementation of evidence-based preventions and interventions

  • Work effectively with the Program Launch; Delivery, Fidelity, and Impact; Sustainability; and Program Development teams to ensure adequate and timely support for Ys throughout all phases of program testing, adoption, or implementation

  • Support coordinated efforts with other Y-USA staff and departments (e.g., Resource Delivery, Government Relations, Marketing, Communications, Research and Evaluation, Training and Leadership Development, Financial Development, Human Resources, Legal, etc.) to advance the integration of implementation sciences and theories into practice for youth development programs.

  • Perform other duties as assigned by supervisor or VP of Division

  • Effectively work to achieve desired outcomes, outputs and overall impact

  • Support the use of data to inform future work with local Ys as well as the scale-up of programs and practices

  • Provide support and technical assistance in the translation of research outputs and outcomes to practice and policy

  • Values. Demonstrates in word and action the Y’s core values of caring, honesty, respect, and responsibility and a commitment to the Y’s mission, in all matters at all times.

  • Inclusion. Values all people for their unique talents, and takes an active role in promoting practices that support diversity, inclusion, and cultural competence.

  • Relationships. Builds authentic relationships in the service of enhancing individual and team performance to support the Y's work.

  • Communication. Listens and expresses self effectively and in a manner that reflects a true understanding of the needs of the audience.

  • Developing Others. Recognizes and acts on the need to continually develop others' capabilities to attain the highest level of performance possible.

  • Project Management. Supports goal attainment by prioritizing activities, assigning responsibilities in accordance with capabilities, monitoring progress, and evaluating impact.

  • Decision Making. Integrates logic, intuition and sound judgment to analyze information to identify greatest opportunities, make sound decisions, and solve problems.

  • Change Capacity. Leads self and others through change by navigating ambiguity appropriately and adapting well to new situations, obstacles, and opportunities.

  • Emotional Maturity. Demonstrates effective interpersonal skills.

  • Quality Results. Demonstrates and fosters a strong commitment to achieving goals in a manner that provides quality experiences.

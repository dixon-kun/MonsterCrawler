          Current Need:
         The Data Scientist will join the Sensitive Data Management team supporting the McKesson Specialty Health organization. This position will lead data projects to capitalize on business opportunities in five key areas: Manufacturer, Provider, and Payor solutions along with Health Economics Outcomes Research and regulatory reporting requirements. This position will support a cross-functional team and provide in-depth advanced analytical insights to solve complex business problems while maintaining compliance with HIPAA and state privacy and security regulations. This role will require analytical skills and tactical responsibilities to provide insights and recommendations for secure, HIPAA compliant solutions.
          Position Description:
         This position receives assignments in the form of objectives and leverages data to create insightful models and tools ultimately used to support business decisions:
           Engages with business leaders and team members including data analysts and data engineers to understand the needs of the business, turns needs into data requirements, and structures data-based solutions as required to support business objectives
           Performs exploratory data analysis, generates and tests working hypotheses, and uncovers noteworthy trends and relationships
           Analyze and model structured data and implement algorithms to support analysis using advanced statistical and mathematical methods from statistics, data mining, econometrics, and operations research
           Perform advanced analytics techniques to mine unstructured data, using methods such as document clustering, topic analysis, named entity recognition, and document classification (machine learning and natural language processing is a plus)
           Communicate results and educate others through reports and presentations
           Able to use sound judgment and prioritize tasks
           Results oriented, enthusiastic, creative thinker
           Self-starter attitude with the ability to excel in dynamic work environments
           Ability to manage multiple priorities
         Qualifications
          Minimum Requirements
         5+ years of related experience. Desired backgrounds may include healthcare, Medicare, pharmaceuticals, biologics manufacturing, or clinical research.
          Critical Skills
           Strong quantitative analysis background and experience in working with large, complex data systems to aggregate, organize, and prepare data for use in business analysis
           Strong mathematical background with knowledge in at least one of the following fields: statistics, data mining, operations research, econometrics, and/or information retrieval (machine learning and natural language processing is a plus)
           Deep experience in extracting, cleaning, preparing and modeling data
           Experience with command-line scripting, data structures, and algorithms
           Proficiency using relevant software packages for statistical analyses and visualization including SAS, SQL, R, Spark, Hadoop (MapReduce, HBASE), Hive, Pig, Java, Trifacta, H2O, Python, Tableau, and Power BI
           Data visualization and presentation skills to effectively communicate data insights
           HIPAA Safe Harbor, HITECH Act regulations and Expert Determination data de-identification regulations
          Education
         MS preferred or 4-year degree or equivalent experience. MS or PhD degree in a quantitative or systems discipline such as Computer Science, Physics, Engineering, Statistics, Mathematics, Econometrics, or related fields is preferred.

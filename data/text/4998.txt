           UX Designer
          Summary: ADP User Experience is looking for a highly motivated UX Designer. You'll become an integral part a new team that creates next generation payroll applications with a focus on disruptive innovation and ease of use. This is a great opportunity for someone who's passionate about collaborative, user centric design and leveraging design thinking to create the next generation of enterprise application experiences.
          You will work with research professionals and product owners to uncover user and business goals and pain points. Guide Product and developers to ideate, design, and synthesize user feedback. Working with the product design team and larger ADT design community, you'll contribute to the overall UX vision and design patterns, then help socialize them across the product team. We're an agile organization that works iteratively and is committed to getting solutions out fast, collecting feedback, and continually improving solutions.
           Responsibilities:
           Identify and capture relevant business and user goals and objectives
           Interview users, stakeholders, and SMEs as needed to inform and confirm design choices
           Create the appropriate UX artifacts to share insights with the product team (e.g. story boards, use cases, video, scenarios, and more)
           Lead collaborative design sessions
           Create sketches and other artifacts that capture designs and help ensure everyone, from stakeholders to development, are on the same page
           Participate in UX team community review sessions to socialize and evolve designs and improve your skills
           Contribute to product consistency by adhering to and contributing new design patterns
           Create prototypes for use in usability testing
           Help determine metrics and other techniques to measure success
           Keep up-to-date on design trends, tools, and techniques
           Be an advocate for users and passionate about the experience

           Basic understanding of enterprise environments (processes and security concerns)
           Understanding of effective page layout and navigation schemes (so you can ensure that products of collaborative design sessions conform to best practices and ADP standards)
           Ability to take complex information and distill it into simple components and interactions
           Experience with Agile and lean UX processes
           Ability to multi-task on various projects effectively
           Ability to think big picture (about standards and vision) while maintaining acute attention to process details

          Adjunct Lecturer: Experimental Statistics I
          Southern Methodist University
          Office of the Provost
          MSDS 6372 Experimental Statistics I
          Position Summary:
         The Office of the Provost at Southern Methodist University welcomes applications for adjunct faculty to teach courses in our new online Master of Science in Data Science program (DataScience@SMU). Responsibilitiesinclude teaching live online sections of statistical methods, data analysis, and statistical sampling at the Master's level.
          We are hiring instructors for the following course:
          MSDS 6371 - Experimental Statistics I
         :
         The focus of this course is on the experimental design and statistical methods. Methods taught in MSDS 6371 include exploratory data analysis, two-sample t-tests, analysis of variance, and linear regression. Emphasis is on appropriate application of these methods and interpretation of the results in observational studies and experiments.
          Responsibilities:
         Classes are all taught online via the Internet therefore, no relocation is necessary. Instructors will need Internet access (preferable a wired connection). Courses are taught in the evening to accommodate students work schedules. Each live 90-minute class session involves instruction, answering questions, working problems with students, grading homework assignments, preparing and grading assignments, guiding students in course projects and hosting online office hours for sections consisting of up to 17 students. Lecturers will also be expected to grade homework assignments, host online office hours, review student applications for admission, and participate in other program-sponsored activities.
          At The Conclusion of the Course Students Will:
          Gain a better understanding of basic statistical methods covered in introductory statistics courses and problems that arise when these analytic methods are applied to real-life research problems
          Demonstrate understanding of the advantages and disadvantages of a given experimental design, particularly with respect to the type of conclusions that can be made
          Appropriately apply the methods discussed in the course to numerical and categorical data
          Communicate the findings of a statistical analysis in a clear, concise, and scientific manner
          Course Start Dates:

         August 28, 2017 to December 15, 2017 - Preparation begins August 21, 2017

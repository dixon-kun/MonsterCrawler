Data Developer

Location: Culver City, CA Division: Ipsos Science Center R&D

The Ipsos Science Center is a uniquely positioned group within Ipsos, tasked with work for external clients, developing software tools encapsulating best practice, advanced analytic requests from internal teams, and expanding what is possible in market research with data science.

We’re looking for a highly capable, data-literate person. We need someone who’s a better statistician than most programmers and a better programmer than most statisticians. We need someone who can suggest analysis appropriate for a problem, develop a prototype, polish it if it works, stand up an API if it’s worth distributing, build visuals on top if needed, and work through the challenges along the way. We’re looking for a fearless developer, willing to slice into any of the layers and tackle the problems they find; a developer who may be a specialist in one step, but has the flexibility, capability, and curiosity to collaboratively work across the process. In short, we’re looking for a full-stack developer for the analytic stack.

Position Responsibilities

  • Enhance and maintain existing tools used by the larger organization
  • Design and develop new tools for internal users and external clients
  • Conduct or assist with research (e.g. designing/implementing/testing new algorithms and approaches)
  • Conduct ad hoc projects for internal clients

Skills:

Required Skills

  • Bachelor’s in Computer Science, Mathematics, Statistics, or related
  • Highly Capable R and/or Python developer - you feel comfortable developing your own packages and maintaining others
  • High level familiarity with statistics and analytics

Preferred Skills

  • Academic or professional experience with analytics or machine learning using e.g. caret, scikit-learn.
  • Experience designing and deploying web applications using e.g. flask, web2py.
  • Experience designing and developing static and dynamic visualizations using e.g. bokeh, ggplot2, d3.js, dc.js
  • Experience designing or implementing new analytic methods

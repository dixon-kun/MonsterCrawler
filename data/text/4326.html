THE POSITION

The Brattle Group is seeking an experienced engineer to work alongside our consulting teams to provide software development and data analytics support. In the course of our work, we regularly receive large amounts of data and/or documents in a wide variety of formats. The Data Engineer will be located in The Brattle Group’s Cambridge, Massachusetts office, but will interface with internal and external clientele regardless of location in order to determine how to best process these materials into a form supporting manipulation and analysis, as well as implementing agreed upon systems and processes. The Data Engineer will also oversee and maintain internally-developed applications. Additionally, The Brattle Group’s consultants develop and apply reproducible scripts and advanced machine learning algorithms (e.g. topic modeling, cluster analysis) to data sets on a case-by-case basis. Thus, the Data Engineer’s work and projects will be wide-ranging, with numerous opportunities for professional development, innovation, and growth.

The core duties of this position include:

Developing processes for cleaning, validating, and uploading data to Excel-readable formats, SQL server, or our internal Hadoop cluster (depending on data size and project needs) in collaboration with IT and project teams

Developing and testing data processing algorithms in R and re-evaluating on an on-going basis to identify opportunity for increased efficiency

Maintaining and building features for a web-based document search tool built in Django and relying on an Apache Solr backend

Writing reproducible scripts in Python that will classify, clean, and visualize unstructured text data

THE CANDIDATE

In addition to the qualifications listed below, the successful Data Engineer will have the ability to self-teach and to guide colleagues employing a consultative approach to developing solutions. The individual must be able to solve problems creatively and with relative autonomy. The Data Engineer should bring to the role a background in data science coupled with experience administering big-data tools. The ideal candidate will possess:

Bachelor’s degree in Computer Science, Computer Engineering, or a related field

3+ years of professional experience, of which at least 2 years include work in data science

Demonstrated experience communicating with and training non-developers

Deep fluency with Python and R

Proficiency with a compiled language, such as Java, C, or C++

Data science skills include:

Strong proficiency with R and python-pandas

Strong proficiency in SQL

Working knowledge of visualization platforms – e.g., D3, ggplot2, or matplotlib

Distributed computing skills a plus – e.g., Apache Hadoop and Apache Spark

Experience with cloud computing services also a plus (Google Cloud Platform or AWS)

THE PACKAGE

We offer a generous compensation and benefits package, including medical, dental, 401K/profit sharing, life and disability insurance, paid time off, and internal professional development and training programs, designed to reward success at all levels. Details regarding compensation will be based on the successful candidates’ knowledge, skills, and experience.

In order to be considered for this position, please submit a cover letter with salary history and resume.

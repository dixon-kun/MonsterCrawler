Departmental Overview

As the primary academic support service on campus, the Student Learning Center (SLC) helps the institution fulfill its commitment of providing educational access and excellence for a broad spectrum of students. SLC services help undergraduate students transition to the academic culture of Berkeley, translate institutional expectations and meet the academic demands of faculty. The Center's cross-disciplinary learning and teaching models engage students in the practice, application and critique of disciplinary theories, concepts and methodologies. The Center addresses the following areas for the campus:

* Addresses uneven K-12 preparation of students.;

* Assists students in identifying and developing their identity as scholars;

* Bridges the cultural demands and expectations of the University.;

* Supports faculty in their knowledge and understanding of the undergraduate educational experience.

* Mentors and trains aspiring and future teachers and professors.;

* Provides innovative and strategic teaching, learning and training models that are research based.;

* Provides a learning space for students to engage in their learning process.

The Student Learning Center employs 20 career employees and over 250 represented, academic student employees, (e.g.individual tutors and study group leaders) and adjunct instructors. The Center's annual budget is $2.8 million and it serves 9,000+ students per year (about one-third of Berkeley's undergraduate population).

The SLC seeks a passionate educator committed to serving undergraduates in meeting their intellectual and professional aspirations. The incumbent possesses a strong working knowledge of economics and/or data science, delivers a responsive pedagogy, and is dynamic in modeling a recursive learning process that moves students toward proficiency in the subject matter.

This is a 2-year contract position.

Responsibilities

* Delivers established academic support program to students that enhance academic success through a specific course or format of service for a narrow field of study in an academic discipline (e.g., first-year course in economics, writing; introductory chemistry in the sciences; pre-college algebra or calculus in math). Teaches course, meets individually with students and holds office hours. Supports and conducts peer tutor training seminars

* Administers established assessment instruments to assess students' academic preparation and progress. Administers established evaluation tools to enhance course and teaching effectiveness. Evaluates effectiveness of tutor training seminars.

* Collaborates with designated faculty and/or higher level Student Academic Specialist to implement established curriculum for a course/format.

* Maintains knowledge of campus partners (e.g. Disabled Students' Program, advising units, teaching libraries, Counseling and Psychological Services) and refers students appropriately.

* Selects from, modifies, and applies existing academic resource materials (print, web, video-based) in course/format.

Required Qualifications

* Intermediate knowledge of theory, research and pedagogy in the areas of teaching learning and academic support, including methodologies to enhance student success.

* Intermediate knowledge of K-18 contexts as they pertain to the needs of incoming student populations.

* Intermediate knowledge of a specific academic discipline.

* Knowledge of multiple learning styles, affective, socio-cultural and linguistic issues as they apply to providing academic support.

* Knowledge of academic and curricular issues as they apply to providing academic support.

* Experience implementing learning strategies for a specific course or format of academic support.

* Knowledge of relevant campus, state and federal policies, rules and regulations.

* Strong interpersonal skills that facilitate communication across a broad range of people.

* Good judgment and decision-making skills. Keen political acumen.

* Developed multicultural competencies: knowledge and experience working with diverse group of students, staff and faculty

* Strong oral and written communication skills.

* Proficient computer skills.

* Bachelors degree in related area and/or equivalent experience/training

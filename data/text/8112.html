Adjunct Lecturer: Experimental Statistics II

Southern Methodist University

Office of the Provost

MSDS 6372 Experimental Statistics II

Position Summary:

The Office of the Provost at Southern Methodist University welcomes applications for adjunct faculty to teach courses in our new online Master of Science in Data Science program (DataScience@SMU). Responsibilities include teaching live online sections of statistical methods, data analysis, and statistical sampling at the Master's level.

We are hiring instructors for the following course:

MSDS 6372– Experimental Statistics II:

This course examines more advanced methods, such as multivariate ANOVA, linear discriminant analysis, logistic regression, machine learning, and bootstrap.

Responsibilities:

Classes are all taught online via the Internet therefore, no relocation is necessary. Instructors will need Internet access (preferable a wired connection). Courses are taught in the evening to accommodate students work schedules. Each live 90-minute class session involves instruction, answering questions, working problems with students, grading homework assignments, preparing and grading assignments, guiding students in course projects and hosting online office hours for sections consisting of up to 17 students. Lecturers will also be expected to grade homework assignments, host online office hours, review student applications for admission, and participate in other program-sponsored activities.

At The Conclusion of the Course Students Will:

* Gain a better understanding of basic statistical methods covered in introductory statistics courses and problems that arise when these analytic methods are applied to real-life research problem

* Demonstrate understanding of the advantages and disadvantages of a given experimental design, particularly with respect to the type of conclusions that can be made

* Appropriately apply the methods discussed in the course to numerical and categorical data

* Communicate the findings of a statistical analysis in a clear, concise, and scientific manner

Course Start Dates:

May 15, 2017 to August 17, 2017 – Preparation begins May 8, 2017

Training and Technical Requirements:

Because this is a virtual teaching role over an Adobe platform, hired faculty will be assigned 3 mandatory training sessions 3-6 weeks prior to the start of each semester. There are also certain technical requirements that all candidates must meet to be considered for the role. They are listed below:

* Wired high-speed internet connection (8Mbps download & 3Mbps upload minimum speeds)

* Full-powered laptop or desktop computer that is less than 3 years old (not a netbook or tablet)

* Up-to-date operating system (old operating systems such as Windows XP/Vista or Mac OS X 10.6 and 10.7 are NOT supported)

* Webcam (laptops with integrated webcams usually work very well)

* Phone (need excellent reception where you plan to teach if using a mobile phone)

* Headset for your phone (be sure the headset includes a microphone)

* Technological aptitude: The ability to learn technical concepts and adapt them to a teaching environment

Compensation:

The salary for this adjunct position is $7,500 for every two live sessions taught ($3,750 for one live session) and is a renewable per semester each year. We are recruiting applicants now for all terms.

Qualifications:

* A PhD or equivalent is required preferably in statistics or biostatistics.

* Candidates who are all but dissertation (ABD) will also be considered

* Proficient in SAS software, particularly for advanced linear models, such as multivariate analysis of variance, principle components analysis, and discriminant analysis

* Ability to use SAS on a Macintosh via a server

* Familiarity with the R language

* A superior academic performance record

* The ability to be self-directed with broadly-defined limits on assignments

* Excellent oral and written communication skills

* Demonstrated ability to interact efficiently with diverse people in a highly multidisciplinary environment

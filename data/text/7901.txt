         The Center for Strategic and International Studies (CSIS) is a non-profit, bipartisan public policy organization established in 1962 to provide strategic insights and practical policy solutions to decision makers concerned with global security and prosperity. Over the years, it has grown to be one of the largest organizations of its kind, with a staff of some 200 employees, including more than 120 analysts working to address the changing dynamics of international security across the globe.
          JOB SUMMARY:
         The Defense-Industrial Initiatives Group (DIIG) at CSIS provides timely, in-depth analysis on the critical issues at the cross-section of the Department of Defense and its industrial base. DIIG focuses on defense acquisition trends, technological innovation, ongoing reform efforts, and the budgetary environment.
         This opening is for a full-time, paid internship. We are seeking a candidate with a background in public policy and data science, as well as a strong interest in international security.
         Currently the analytics team focuses on a variety of topics including the following:
          Future Years Defense Program
          Major Defense Acquisition Programs
          Navy Shipbuilding
          S. Force Structure
          DoD Procurement and RDT&amp;E
          Small Business Policy
          ESSENTIAL DUTIES AND RESPONSIBILITIES:
         Interns at DIIG bolster the team with research, writing, program development, and coding. Essential functions may include, but are not limited to the following:
          Assisting with research and organizing relevant information for the various DIIG projects
          Assisting with data collection and management
          Improving the analytic capabilities of the broader International Security Program
          Assisting with event planning and staffing
          Exploring the application of data science to public policy
          KNOWLEDGE, EDUCATION, AND EXPERIENCE:
          Completed bachelor&#8217;s or master&#8217;s degree. Or in pursuit of a bachelor&#8217;s degree (preferably Junior or Senior).
          Proficiency with Microsoft Excel and SQL databases highly desirable
          Familiar with programming languages for data analysis (Python, R, etc.)
          Extensive experience with R preferred, including the tidyverse, ggplot, and shiny
          Must have at least a 3.0 GPA (on a 4.0 scale) from an accredited U.S. institution or equivalent from a non-U.S. institution
          Strong communication and interpersonal skills
          Experience with quantitative analysis
          Ability to work independently
          Interest in defense and national security issues

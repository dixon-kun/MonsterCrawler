        Project Objective: Collaborative Problem Solving and Embedded Assessment
        The "Collaborative Problem Solving and Embedded Assessment" project will explore collaborative problem solving as it relates to the ACT's Holistic framework and team based assessment. Collaborative problem solving is a key area of interest for ACTNext and a skill needed to succeed in school and work. Embedded assessment is critical to ACT being able to better integrate assessment and learning into lives of individuals. The testing industry is struggling to accommodate a number of initiatives to decrease the amount of testing (e.g., OptOut, laws and regulations limiting testing time) while simultaneously being asked to provide a wider range of information about students. Embedded assessment can help with this.
        A principled assessment design approach was followed to develop clear construct descriptions and rubrics for coding student behavior observed in video clips of middle school students working together to solve problems in Physics Playground. In addition to out-of-game behaviors, in-game indicators and other assessments may also be used as part of the analyses. Video clips of teams of three students have already been collected and are ready for analyses. The intern may also be involved in other relevant projects that involve text analytics with machine learning and natural language processing. As part of this project, the intern will gain hands on experience working with complex real world data.
        Typical work-related activities include:
        * Conduct literature reviews on relevant topics
        * Assist with analyses of student team video clip data
        * Assist with data analysis
        * Interpret analyses and write results
        * Assist with text analytics projects involving machine learning and natural language processing

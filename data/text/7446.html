Job Description

Chief Information Officer (CIO), reporting to the President, to provide leadership and direction in implementing and advancing all of the Firm's CDS (Client Delivery Services) and IS (Information Systems) services including all technology operations, practice support, and security. Essential Job Duties: Develop, track, and control the CDS and IS annual operating and capital budgets including business plan justification of the costs/identification of the benefits for spending and initiatives. Develop and implement CDS and IS governance practices, policies, objectives, and activities to ensure reliability and scalability of operations while maximizing return on investments (ROIs) and increasing overall productivity. Analyze operations to evaluate the performance of the CDS and IS departments, and their staff, in meeting objectives. Analysis will also help determine areas of potential cost reduction, increased productivity, program improvement or policy change. Maintain knowledge of developments in the area of systems and hardware to assure that new developments (i.e.: encryption at rest) are incorporated in future systems of the Firm and offer creative and innovative ideas for how to best use information technology to meet changing business demands and simplify operating practices. Plan and control staffing and development, organization, hardware acquisitions and facilities for the information systems department to assure that they are consistent with and supportive of the business plans of the Firm. Evaluate external advancements in the IS industry to improve client/user satisfaction, reduce operating expenses and maximize revenues. (i.e.: Cloud, SAAS) Manage strong operating practices for vendor management, contracting, and ongoing support and maintenance of system applications. Approve the development and execution of an enterprise-wide disaster recovery and business continuity plan. Act as IT Security Specialist to ensure the client is always protected from internal and external security threats. This includes the oversight of overall IT disaste



NOTEThe ideal candidate will have experience managing vendors (outward facing), managing systems (internal). We need someone who has dealt with big data mining, data filtering and has a focus on next gen architecture. Someone familiar with litigation technology, data science, and analytics, staff development. We imagine the role will be 30% strategic, 40% tactical and 30% operations.

The ideal candidate also gets litigation support software development, cloud computing, data mining.

Data Engineer – All Levels

Are you interested in using your data and analysis skills to make a difference? Do you want to make healthcare work the way it should?

At athenahealth, our mission is to be caregivers’ most trusted service, helping them do well doing the right thing. We do this by delivering cloud-based services for EHR, practice management and care coordination on a single instance, multitenant, open platform spanning over 14 years and over 50,000 providers. Because of that, we have a very large, unique data asset.  The Data Science group at athenahealth is dedicated to gaining insight from our data and driving those insights across our company in order to help deliver on our mission. If you like the challenge of finding needles in data haystacks, enjoy unravelling mysteries, are willing to challenge conventional wisdom, and want to help shepherd your insights to others, then you should consider a role in one of the Data Science teams here at athenahealth.

While data science is often only associated with big data and machine learning, at athenahealth it is much more than that. Teams of business analysts, data engineers and data analysts all work together to mine valuable insight and change the direction and capabilities of our products and services.

A little more about the roles:

Business Analyst – The business analyst analyzes corporate, product, payer, and healthcare provider performance data, in order to identify and implement performance improvement opportunities.  They develop a deep understanding of the health care industry and our business, and use this knowledge combined with outstanding analytic, communication, and project management skills to get the job done.  Our business analysts tend to have more technical skills than in other industries, and internally we refer to them as performance analysts.

Data Analyst – The data analyst combines a mix of analytical skills, data modeling techniques, statistics and predictive algorithms in order to help athenahealth gain a competitive advantage in the marketplace. The data analyst creates and maintains the data assets of the company, working with teams across the organization to help them improve their operation or answer key business questions.

Data Engineer – The data engineer is in many ways the foundation of the Data Science team, ensuring that data is available in the right environment for everyone. It is not uncommon for data engineers to be building tools and infrastructure, or trying out the latest analytic dbms.  This is the most technically demanding role within Data Science.
Current team members have come from widely varying educational backgrounds – English, Public Policy, Psychology, Economics, Biology, Mathematics, Computer Science, Physics – with different levels of experience.  What’s important is a passion for using data to make a difference.

Qualifications

  • Ability to manipulate data, formulate hypotheses, draw conclusions, and deliver results
  • Bachelor’s degree required.  Some positions favor MS/Ph.D./ScD and /or several years of experience.
  • Sense of humor and effective interpersonal communication skills
  • Health experience is not required; you will learn from the best – your colleagues!

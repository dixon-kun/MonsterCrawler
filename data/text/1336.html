Data Scientist (336-124) at Launchpoint

Itasca, IL 60143

Report this job

About the Job

The role of Data Scientist is to develop models and algorithms for the identification and extraction of information both for the optimization and growth of our existing product lines as well for the creation of new and exciting, analytic-oriented products. As a key player, you will master some of the complex business problems our clients, our healthcare system, as well as our own data challenges face, and deploy your mastery in developing solutions for these problems.

ROLES AND RESPONSIBILITIES:

  • This position is responsible for the design, development, implementation, and maintenance of value-add analytics for LaunchPoint's product portfolio, primarily by leveraging large healthcare data-sets to produce valid, replicable, and defensible analytic content.
  • This position is responsible for monitoring and tuning data models continuously to ensure they remain accurate as additional data is analyzed.
  • This position will also research, evaluate, recommend, and utilize appropriate data mining, predictive modeling, and/or statistical analysis tools and methodologies to perform relevant analyses.

This position supports ad hoc analysis requests.

Required Skills:

ESSENTIAL DUTIES:

  • Development, maintenance, and updating of analytic/statistical models.
  • Maintain current knowledge of established and emerging analytic methodologies, software, or tools.
  • Adhere to and support enterprise standards for the design and implementation of analytics to support and enhance new and existing products.
  • Support the execution of analytics strategy for the Data Services and Analytics organization.
  • Support product leadership with the design, development, and delivery of new analytic/information-centric products.
  • Present analytic results and recommendations to peers and leadership team
  • Train and educate internal staff and management on data mining methodology and business issues unique to healthcare products.
  • Anticipate future trends and potential impact, and communicate accordingly.
  • Maintain effective working relationships within team and among internal customers.
  • Effectively and comfortably interact with upper management.
Required Experience:

POSITION REQUIREMENTS:

  • Graduate-level degree with concentration in a quantitative discipline such as statistics computer science, mathematics, economics, or operations research.
  • Strong verbal and written communications skills, listening and teamwork skills, and effective presentation skills. This is absolutely essential since you will have a lot of exposure to different internal groups (data, IT, product and sales)
  • 3+ years of relevant work experience including 1 year of statistical modeling and data mining on healthcare claims, using large and complex datasets.
  • Experience with Subrogation and/or Eligibility Services preferred.
  • Expertise in statistical modeling techniques such as linear regression, logistic regression, GLM, decision tree models, cluster analysis, and principal components, as well as feature engineering and model validation.
  • Expertise in building and applying machine learning/predictive modeling in real-world use cases
  • Programming experience with R. Other statistical software (CART, Emblem, SPSS, Matlab). Greenplum and UNIX experience is a plus.
  • Programming experience with SQL.
  • Detail-oriented technical aptitude with strong logical, problem solving, and decision-making skills.
  • Excellent organization/planning skills and talent for managing many large and complex datasets.
  • Aptitude in performing multiple tasks and dealing with changing deadline requirements. This includes knowing when to escalate issues.
  • Maintains a focused, flexible, organized, and proactive manner

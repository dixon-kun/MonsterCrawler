        Description:
        The Public Health Division of the Leidos Health Group has an immediate need for a Survey Specialist in Atlanta, GA.
        The Survey Specialist will support a contract for the Centers for Disease Control or Prevention. The candidate will work closely with Public Health and IT professionals to define, design, and develop sample surveys and will execute virtually all aspects of the survey data collection lifecycle using a variety of methods, tools, programming languages. The candidate will capture and document requirements and business rules from various CDC programs, and translate them into electronic, paper, and telephone-based surveys. The survey specialist will produce documentation for technical analysis, system design, security and training materials as needed. In addition, the Survey Specialist will provide basic data management support for survey data and will also help program electronic survey instruments so the applicants must possess the following technical skills:
        * A good working knowledge of database systems such and Microsoft SQL Server, oracle, MySQL, etc. and be able to execute basic queries using Structured Query Language (SQL);
        * Basic Proficiency with HTML and very basic understanding of web-page design,
        * Beginner's knowledge of web technologies such as jQuery, JavaScript, java, XML, XSLT, Cascading Style sheets (CSS), Flash, ASP, ASPN.NET, etc;
        * Proficiency with a Visual Basic variant such VBA, VB6, VBScript, or VB.NET;
        * Solid understanding of how to format and manipulate data and datsets using programs such as Excel, SAS, R, Python, SPSS, SUDAAN, JMP, S-PLUS, or SQL-based programs.
        Many other skills will be learned through on-the-job training, so a willingness to learn is a must, along with being self-motivated, detail-oriented. We prefer candidates who ask questions and are not hesitant to suggest alternative methods to senior staff to improve processes and outcomes. The Survey Specialist will work in the exciting National Center for Zoonotic and Emerging Infectious Diseases. He or she will work to support longitudinal studies as well as to support exciting and time-sensitive outbreak investigations alongside disease detectives. As a result, as a survey specialist you must remain flexible and willing to adapt and perform various roles as needed. Some of the other technical skills that we look for as a plus, but are not required are:
        * Ability to draw representative samples for complex sample surveys
        * Unicom Intelligence (f/k/a IBM SPSS Data Collection or mrInterview)
        * Blaise survey language
        * Familiarity with web-based survey systems such as SurveyMonkey, Qualtrics, Zoomerang, SurveyGizmo, LimeSurvey, RedCap
        * Ability to perform statistical analyses and have any working knowledge of epidemiology
        * Knowledge of good research study design;
        * Knowledge of survey best practices and survey methodology best practices
        The roles and responsibilities of this job may include some, but not necessarily all, of the following. We encourage you to apply if you have only a subset of the skills below and not necessarily all of them:
        1. Program electronic surveys using a visual-basic like programming language
        2. Assist in planning study design for various epidemiological studies
        3. Conduct data analyses including but not limited to descriptive statistics, factor analyses, generalized linear models, random effects models, analysis of survey data, and other statistical methods
        4. Manipulate and extract data from various data sources
        5. Prepare reports in accordance with the analytical results, including providing support for the development of manuscripts.
        6. Draw inferences from data and disseminate scientific information in the form of presentations, reports, slides, and co-authored peer reviewed journal manuscripts.
        Qualifications:
        In addition to the desired skills listed previous, we require all of the following:
        * Bachelor's degree in Biostatistics, Statistics, Mathematics, Economics, Epidemiology, Data Science, Decision Sciences, or related field with 5 years' experience
        * Excellent interpersonal, verbal, and written communication skills
        * Willing and Eager to learn
        * Ability to work well under pressure
        * Ability to adapt and look for new ways to do work to improve efficiencies and improve processes
        * Ability to work well both in isolation and in teams
        * Work independently, and self-direct work as needed

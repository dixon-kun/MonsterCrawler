Position Description Title/PD#: Research Health Science Specialist (Sr. Data Scientist) / 6743-A

* RELOCATION INCENTIVE/RECRUITMENT INCENTIVE IS NOT AUTHORIZED.*

This position is within the Program Evaluation and Resource Center (PERC), a component of the Office of Mental Health Operations (OMHO) in Patient Care Services within the Veterans Health Administration (VHA). PERC is responsible for providing strategic guidance and program evaluation to improve the quality and availability of treatment for Veterans with substance use disorders and related concerns including mental health disorders, behavioral health disorders, Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder, chronic pain, and homelessness. This program evaluation includes attention to special populations such as returning service members from Operation Iraqi Freedom and Operation Enduring Freedom (OIF/OEF), women, and aging Veterans, as well as new treatment paradigms such as primary care-mental health integration and Veteran-centered and recovery focused care.

The incumbent of this position will serve as a senior Data Scientist within PERC and provides support to the Director. He/she is responsible to the Director of PERC for general conduct of evaluation studies and makes major contributions to the design, formulation, and implementation of these projects.

The major duties and responsibilities include, but are not limited to:

* Performing Extract, Transform, Load (ETL) activities with a deep understanding of relational databases; including metadata and formal descriptions of the tables, columns, domains, and constraints;

* Developing and maintaining data products (dashboards, databases, data tables, and files) which can be shared and accessed by a variety of customers, including senior administration and clinical staff;

* Developing, conducting and implementing program evaluation projects across the VHA mental health system;

* Educating mental health program leads and VA Central (VACO) program office staff about the access, uses and benefits of dashboards and data products that improve patient outcomes and the efficacy of clinical programs (for example, dashboards that address smoking cessation, delivery of evidence-based psychotherapy, best practices for opioid prescribing, consults between primary care and mental health, and mental health staffing);

* Ensuring compliance with all data security requirements and regulations and facilitating the protection of large volumes of highly sensitive patient information.

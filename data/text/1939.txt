        Description:
        The Biological Life Sciences Operation of the Health Group is seeking a Biomedical Data Scientist who will perform as a productive member of a team that supports analytical, program management, and research proposal funding processes for the Army in Frederick, MD.
        This is NOT a laboratory research position.
        JOB DESCRIPTION: Candidate will apply analytical and scientific expertise to several medical research programs. The job entails, but is not limited to, the detailed analysis, integration, categorization, and application of a broad spectrum of biomedical and health-related data and information derived from a diversity of customer-generated requirements (e.g., response to inquiries from the Army, other government agencies and Congress, and private sector). Other aspects of the job include practical knowledge of networks and servers. Responsiveness, as well as open and frequent coordination and discussions with a multidisciplinary and goal-oriented group, are inherent elements of the day-to-day operations. Candidate must be capable of independent action and initiative.
        Candidate will use expertise in the analysis of one or more of the following areas: biochemistry, immunology, cell biology, molecular biology, medicinal chemistry, clinical and experimental therapeutics, pharmacology, military operational medicine, etc. This position is located in Frederick, Maryland. Some travel required.
        Qualifications:
        Qualifications
        REQUIRED EDUCATION/SKILLS: A Master's degree from an accredited college or university appropriate to research is required, with minimally one year related experience. Excellent analytical, writing, and verbal-communication skills are required and experience in data analysis/manipulation software (e.g., SQL, SAS, Access, Workbench) and MS Office software (Word, Excel, PowerPoint, Outlook) in a PC environment is essential. Training and experience in databases relevant to biological sciences, particularly cancer or trauma research, is essential. Candidate must be able to interact in a positive and competent manner with individuals at all levels of responsibility, and willing to address a wide range of rapidly changing and divergent issues. Candidate must be capable of applying and integrating knowledge from multiple sources toward solving problems of a research nature. Candidate must be capable of working alone or with a technical team of researchers. Strong interpersonal skills including the ability to set and meet goals are necessary.
        This position requires a US citizen in order to obtain US DOD security clearance.
        DESIRED SKILLS: (1) Experience in communication of complex data schema and analyses to the layman and technical writing is preferred. (2) Organizing and implementing operational procedures and (3) analysis, review, and evaluation of interdisciplinary scientific research.

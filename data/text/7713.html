Duties

This position is located in the Operations Analytics (OA) section of the Office of the Deputy Under Secretary for Field Operations (OFO), within the Veterans Benefits Administration (VBA). OA is a small team of operations research analysts/data scientists and business analysts that assesses workload requirements and conducts resource planning while also providing timely information to VBA and executive leadership to drive data-informed operational, tactical and strategic decisions. The incumbent in this position will be responsible for the development of predictive modeling capabilities for operational issues as well as optimization models to meet VBA's mission objectives. There is a need for the incumbent to have a strong background in mathematics and statistics in order to perform the functions assigned.

MAJOR DUTIES:

* Choosing from a descriptive, diagnostic, predictive and prescriptive analytic framework in to define, diagnose, predict or influence decision-making in order to create desired program outcomes for veterans and survivors.

* Developing studies and models using quantitative analytical techniques and methodologies, such as regression analysis, mathematical programming, and modeling simulations to solve real-world challenges.

* Evaluating and conducting independent business case analyses and analysis of alternatives to program and process improvement initiatives that involve complex technical parameters across VBA functional and business line boundaries.

* Performing cost estimation and/or analysis of operational programs, facilities, and information systems or programs using approved processes and tools, to include mathematical modeling and statistical analysis.

* Developing cost estimate sources, methods, controls and projections using a variety of techniques including modeling, parametric analysis, engineering estimates, learning curve, risk analysis, and cost estimating relationships.

* Tracking and monitoring changes and developments in analytic research and technology .

* Communicating key analytic advances to management officials and supporting analytic projects that require advanced analytics such as predictive analysis (forecast) and decision analytics.

Work Schedule: Standard five day, eight hours per day, full time (40 hours per week) schedule; actual times to be determined. This position may require occasional travel for training. Dates and length of training will be negotiated with the candidate. Telework may be authorized.

Promotion potential: The selectee may be promoted to the full performance level without further competition when all regulatory, qualification, and performance requirements are met. Selection at a lower grade level does not guarantee promotion to the full performance level.

Physical Requirements: The work is sedentary in nature, involving some walking, standing, sitting, bending, and carrying of light items, such as paper, books, and computer printouts. The Department of Veterans Affairs provides reasonable accommodation for persons with disabilities.

The Director of a Product Line guides the future direction, business performance and life cycle of one or more product lines including enabling software, services, solution expertise and partnerships. The candidate leads cross-functional business teams to develop product line extensions, assumes accountability for the business performance of the product line roadmap, secures partnerships and conveys the vision to internal and external stakeholders. The Director of a Product Line reports to a General Manager of a product category and collaborates closely with product owners, product managers, marketing, sales, services and support. This leadership role is responsible for managing the product line strategy, release trains, commitments and business plans. The primary focus of the Director of a Product Line is delivering multi-generational product plans and improving Axway’s overall ability to win in the market.


  • Developing product line extensions. Beyond everyday priorities, the effective Director of a Product Line has an eye toward the future and invests significant time in defining product concepts, business cases and business plans for product line extensions. The Director of a Product Line oversees the development of market and product requirements for new and existing products. This role is responsible for effective communication, verbally, visually, and in writing, with cross-functional business teams to determine the optimal scope of enabling software, services, solution expertise and partnerships. The Director of a Product Line relies on and empowers Product Owner(s), Product Manager(s), Solution enablement managers, Service Line Directors, Product/Solution Marketers, Presales and Operations to determine specific product features, services packages, business processes, go to market programs, pricing, launch plans and sales methodologies for each product.
  • Assuming accountability for the execution and business performance of the product line roadmap. The Director of a Product Line drives the key performance indicators (KPI) for their product line, including pipeline, revenue, market share, customer retention and profits. The qualified Director of a Product Line develops intimate relationships at all levels of the customers’ organizations to regularly capture buyer and user persona feedback into the product line direction and strategy. He or she proactively identifies areas of opportunity and improvement in all technical and operational aspects of the product line, initiates action plans to address those areas, contributes hands-on to executing the plans and follows through on the results to ensure business cases for the products in the product line are met.
  • Securing solution partnerships. The Director of a Product Line ensures effective creation of build versus buy analysis, sales channel effectiveness and market access to determine solution partner requirements. He or she collaborates with marketing, sales and partnership teams to define and pursue solution partnerships to complete products or create new routes to market.
  • Conveying the solution vision to internal and external stakeholders. The Director of a Product Line invests significant time externally articulating the vision of their product line to employees, customers, partners, analysts, industry bodies and other influencers. The effective Product Line Manager collaborates closely with the senior leadership and executive teams to develop overall Axway business strategy and execute the company’s multi-year business plans.
  • Safeguard Axway and Axway customer data through compliance with Axway policies and the appropriate use of Axway equipment and facilities.

  • An imaginative and decisive business technologist who possesses deep domain knowledge of customer problems, market trends and competitor capabilities. Orientation is towards the definition of solutions spanning one or more business cases using strong visual, verbal and written communication.
  • Has earned credibility in identifying key market trends that have been instrumental for selecting the right path of product line extension and direction.
  • Champions “whole product” thinking, ensuring sufficient consideration of non-functional requirements and implications for how product is promoted, sold, delivered, and monetized.
  • Has built credibility and a reputation for guiding cross-functional teams to execute on quality deliverables, i.e. product requirements document (PRD), product roadmaps, product concepts, business cases, services statements of work (SOW), pricing models, sales program plans, marketing plans, sales presentations, etc.
  • Lives and breathes the voice of the customer, capable of translating market trends, requirements and competitive dynamics into comprehensive products, fostering healthy debate and ambitious thinking within a team environment to arrive at a suitable solution.
  • Collaborates without boundaries to ensure transparency into timelines, quality of delivery, exposure to risk, barriers to execution, business viability, and remediation plans.
  • Strong aptitude for hands-on creation of compelling visual and persuasive written content for internal and external consumption.
  • Takes an inclusive and listen-first approach to appropriately influence release contents, schedules, and technical decisions, forming a highly interactive tight-knit relationship built on trust.
  • Continues learning and adapting to change by being receptive to coaching and addressing gaps by proactively developing desired proficiencies. Seeks mentoring and feedback.

Required Education/Experience

  • Bachelor degree or comparable academic training in User-Centered Design, Computer Science, Engineering, Data Science, Management Information Systems, Computer Information Systems, or closely related discipline.
  • Master of Science or comparable post-graduate academic training, a plus.
  • 8+ years of progressive leadership owning product management and/or product marketing with a reputation of delighting customers, meeting tight deadlines, and fostering cross-functional collaborative working relationships.
  • Management level experience developing people at different stages of their careers; managing performance and talent development.
  • Experience in the creation of more than one product from inception to release with meaningful results measured in terms of customers, revenue and/or market share.
  • Background with direct responsibility as a product owner, business analyst, presales consultant, or technical support engineer, a plus.

If you are a driven, hands-on individual with a successful track record in sales and territory management, excel in customer service and are looking for an opportunity to grow professionally, this Field Account Manager position may be a fit for you.

Position Summary:

The Field Account Executive is responsible for providing on-site service and sales to pharmacy operations in an assigned territory. The individual in this role will be required to perform a wide variety of duties including controlled substance inventories, product shipment preparation, new account sales and product and service presentations. This position is a part of the Inmar team specializing in expired pharmaceutical product returns processing, professional waste disposal and customized reporting services. This team serves hospital, government, independent retail, chain-store, clinic, long-term care, veterinarian and other pharmacy segments including wholesalers and manufacturers.

The territory for this position will manage accounts in and around Toledo, Ohio.

Major Responsibilities/Essential Functions:

  • Interface with customers on a professional level at all times, handling concerns and providing education on all services and regulatory compliance issues. Establish client needs.
  • Safely travel to scheduled locations throughout assigned territory, servicing the pharmacy by handling all pharmaceutical returns. Ensure the client has all tools needed for proper return of products.
  • Responsible for inventorying all controlled pharmaceuticals and completing proper documentation as required by law.
  • Prepare and package the returns shipment consistent with the company procedures and the requirements of the commercial freight forwarder.
  • Accurately prepare all internal documentation such as itineraries, supply requisitions and expense reports and submit according to established procedures.
  • Schedule meaningful pharmaceutical returns sales appointments; effectively present and close hospital and independent retail pharmacies.
  • Schedule meaningful product sales appointments; effectively present and close all Strategic Business Partners (RASMAS, Capital inventory, EXPID, Cactus Smart Sink, floor mats).
  • Perform miscellaneous administrative duties such as faxing, photocopying and sending ‘Thank You’ notes as needed. Maintain current customer database.
  • Ensure compliance with company Injury and Illness Prevention program.


Education / Qualifications / Experience

  • Associate’s Degree with 2 - 5 years of experience in sales and territory management or any equivalent combination of experience and training that provides the required knowledge, skills, and abilities needed to complete the job responsibilities.
  • Intermediate experience in all Microsoft applications.
  • Ability to travel daily by car with overnight travel.
  • Ability to establish personal credibility and trusting relationships.
  • 60% service and 40% sales duties, or as previously determined by Regional Manager.
  • Ability to operate a motor vehicle safely and obey all traffic laws.

While performing the duties of this job, the associate is:

  • Regularly required to use hands to finger, handle or feel objects, tools or controls, and reach with hands or arms.
  • Frequently required to operate a motor vehicle safely and obey all traffic laws.
  • Regularly required to talk or hear and read instructions on a computer monitor and/or printed on paper.
  • Frequently required to stand, kneel or stoop, and lift and/or move up to 50 pounds.
  • Frequently required to view items at an extremely close range and must be able to adjust and readjust focus

Safety:

Support a safe work environment by following safety rules and regulations and reporting all safety hazards.

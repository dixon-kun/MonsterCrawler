General Summary/Overview

Partners Healthcare Systems’ (PHS) Division of Research IS & Computing, Enterprise Research Infrastructure and Services (ERIS http://rc.partners.org), in collaboration with the BWH Department of Cardiology and the One Brave Idea, is immediately seeking a Data/Database Engineer. This position works within multi-disciplinary teams that build data processing pipelines and Hadoop/Spark-based analytics jobs. Using your knowledge of distributed computing systems and sys/ops development, you will specialize in data tools and data methods to facilitate biomedical research and personalized medicine. Centered at the interface between the ERIS’ Scientific Computing and Big-Data Analytics service and our scientific communities, the role includes both implementation and consultation aspects.

We are seeking someone with the proven ability to help teams and projects successfully accomplish their technology and data objectives. Key characteristics are an eagerness to learn and apply new technologies, versatility and breadth in technical skills, and communication and customer service skills. Candidates are not expected to be deeply knowledgeable in all areas, but must demonstrate the ability and desire to learn and to support a large and diverse environment in its use of technology. Ideal candidates thrive on variety and innovation in their daily work, on interaction with customers who are world-renowned leaders in their scientific field, and on working with a wide range of technologies in a decentralized non-standard environment (academic).

ERIS enables and supports the highly successful and innovative research programs of the largest teaching hospitals in the nation--Massachusetts General (MGH), Brigham and Women’s (BWH), McLean and Spaulding Rehabilitation Hospitals--with their more than 3200 grant-sponsored programs in the biomedical sciences, from basic to clinical and applied research.

Principal Duties andResponsibilities



• Participate in a multi-disciplinary team to design and build next generation analytics and collaboration platform solutions for research and clinical departments across the Partners Healthcare System.
• Serve as an internal consultant who can bridge the technology (HPC, Big Data tools and systems) to scientific groups and their data problems.
• Provide expertise in data management, analytics and optimum data structuring for very large or diverse datasets using a Data Science approach.
• Configure and provision systems (HDFS/Hive, RDBMS and NoSQL) for the data problems and provide data to scientists for their analytical models and applications.
• Work closely with research clinicians and bioinformaticians to implement their advanced processing pipelines on Hadoop/HPC/Cloud environments
• Understand performance characteristics of underlying server, network and storage hardware
• Audit and document workflows, systems and processes for compliance with security, regulatory and data integrity requirements
• Analyze and improve performance of existing system code/pipelines/algorithms
• Support Technical Customers: Together with the HPC team that includes a scientific applications support specialist, provide support and consulting to scientific researchers who use a broad spectrum of applications from diverse fields (genetics, epidemiology, drug development, natural language processing, and high resolution imaging).
• Analyze and resolve customer and technical problems: Troubleshoot analytical problems when issues arise. Proposes, maintains and enforces polices, practices and security procedures.
• Create and close tasks within the service tracking tool using established standards
• Develop and maintain system documentation as well as training and reference materials for the community using knowledge management tools.
• Use the Partners HealthCare values to govern decisions, actions and behaviors. These values guide how we get our work done: Patients, Affordability, Accountability & Service Commitment, Decisiveness, Innovation & Thoughtful Risk; and how we treat each other: Diversity & Inclusion, Integrity & Respect, Learning, Continuous Improvement & Personal Growth, Teamwork & Collaboration.
• Perform other duties as required by the situation and circumstances

Qualifications

• BA/BS/engineering degree required or equivalent combination of skills/experience. Advanced degree in a computational science or analytical discipline preferred.
• 10+ years minimum experience in working with systems, databases or programming environments in Linux environments for administrative computing. Expert Linux user experience required.
• 8+ years of active experience in support/administration lifecycle of data tools (RDBMS’, NoSQL, Hadoop ecosystem) particularly in open-source platforms and languages
• 4+ years working in a programming environment (at least one of: Java, Python, Ruby, Scala).
• 6+ years administering data management systems of 2 or more of the following: MySQL, Postgres, Hadoop, Mongo.
• 4+ years designing/developing relational database schemas.
• Hands-on experience working with very large datasets on distributed computing platforms
such as Hadoop/HPC/Cloud.
• Demonstrated written communication skills and interpersonal skills required.
• Ability to deeply understand application functionality and technical documentation required.
• Highly desired:
o Experience with at least one additional database technology (SQL or NoSQL—Hbase, Hive, Pig, Cassandra, Mongo)
o Experience with Puppet
o More than 1 year experience with one or more Hadoop-related technologies (Map-reduce, Spark, Hive, Kafka).

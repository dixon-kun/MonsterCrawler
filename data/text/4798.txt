        We are seeking a Postdoctoral Appointee to support the development of turbulence modeling by applying data science analysis to experimental data, primarily Particle Image Velocimetry (PIV), and computational results from Direct Numerical Simulations (DNS). Data-driven machine learning will be applied to the analysis and creation of turbulence models. This includes collaboration with experimentalists and modelers to develop, validate, and apply turbulence modeling strategies to solve complex problems. Opportunities exist to support research spanning multiple disciplines, including heat transport, multi-material mechanical deformation, microscale/mesoscale modeling, and aerosciences.
        Responsibilities include, but are not limited to:
        * Research, development, of methods to analyze experimental and DNS data to extract quantities of interest to turbulence model development
        * Development and application of methodologies from Data Science and Machine learning areas for analyzing performance of turbulence models relative to measurements and DNS
        * Use of Machine learning and Data Science methods to identify potential improvements to turbulence modeling approaches
        Qualifications We Require
        * PhD, conferred within 5 years prior to employment, in Mechanical Engineering, Aerospace Engineering, or other related engineering or natural science field
        * Experience with fluid dynamics and turbulence modeling for RANS, Hybrid RANS-LES and/or LES
        * Proficiency with the Linux operating system and experience with a high-level programming language such as FORTRAN, C, or C++
        * Ability to obtain and maintain a DoE Q clearance
        Qualifications We Desire
        * Experience with Matlab, Python, scripting languages and grid generation software
        * Experience with Machine Learning Algorithms
        * Experience with /working knowledge of Particle Image Velocimetry &#8211; relevant to data analysis.
        * Experience Signal/image processing techniques, numerical methods for compressible flows, reduced order modeling techniques
        * Experience/familiarity with Verification and Validation and Uncertainty Quantification.
        * Thorough knowledge of and applied experience with scientific and engineering methods and with discipline's standards for the ethical conduct of research
        * Strong verbal and written communication skills and an ability to interact well with fellow technical workers with diverse technical backgrounds
        Position Information
        This postdoctoral position is a temporary position for up to one year, which may be renewed at Sandia's discretion up to five additional years. The PhD must have been conferred within five years prior to employment.
        Individuals in postdoctoral positions may bid on regular Sandia positions as internal candidates, and in some cases may be converted to regular career positions during their term if warranted by ongoing operational needs, continuing availability of funds, and satisfactory job performance.
        About Our Team
        The Aerosciences Department offers challenging and important work relating to national security in R&amp;D and technology applications in aerodynamics, aerothermodynamics, compressible fluid mechanics, and flight dynamics. Our primary mission supports U.S. Department of Energy Defense Programs, and aerosciences projects funded through the U.S. Department of Defense, DARPA, NASA, and industry.
        Our projects span the Mach number range from subsonic through hypersonic and involve systems ranging from aircraft released ordinance to reentry systems and rocket systems. Technical activities include experimental, analytical, and computational efforts plus support of flight test activities, both pre-flight/post-flight analyses and field test operations. The Aerosciences Department maintains a strong balance of research and development activities and works synergistically with other organizations at Sandia to meet current and future customer needs.

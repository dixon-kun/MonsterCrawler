The Hampton University School of Engineering and Technology invites nominations and applications for the position of Assistant Dean of the School of Engineering and Technology. Founded in 1868, Hampton University is a leading historically black university (HBCU) located on the Virginia Peninsula in the City of Hampton. It is a privately endowed, co-educational, non-sectarian institution of higher learning with accreditation by the Southern Association of Colleges and Schools, and the State Council of Higher Education of Virginia. The School of Engineering and Technology includes the Department of Engineering, Department of Architecture, and Department of Aviation. The programs are ABET, NAAB, and AABI accredited.

Duties and Responsibilities

The successful candidate for Assistant Dean of the School of Engineering and Technology is expected to assist the Dean in leading the School's efforts to support Hampton University's transformation from its current Carnegie Foundation classification to a research-intensive university. Accordingly, the candidate will be expected to:

* Help inspire and lead the School's faculty in the pursuit of excellence in teaching, scholarship, and service

* Review and update the curriculum

* Write and initiate proposals for external funding and support national and international collaborations that are essential to the growth and marketing of the School

* Help ensure the effective operation of the School

* Respond to University directives and

* Monitor the quality of instruction within the School.

The candidate will also help provide leadership and coordination to all academic programs offered by the School, including strategic planning, resource allocation, implementation, evaluation and continuous improvement; operational function of the academic mission; collaboration with the Dean, chairs and administration to develop and support the enhancement of the School; accreditation and external stakeholders; and representation at events and activities.

The successful candidate will assist the Dean in overseeing the recruitment, development, and performance of faculty; achieving enrollment and retention increases with new program development and successful implementation of recent initiatives; ensuring high standards in the School's programs; addressing the needs of a changing globally-oriented engineering and technology community; aligning the School's priorities with the University Strategic Plan; fundraising; developing and maintaining strong partnerships with alumni, industry, and donors; management of the School's administrative and financial affairs; and other responsibilities as assigned.

Qualifications

The Assistant Dean of the School serves as both an administrator and faculty member, and is expected to provide effective leadership in both roles. An earned doctorate in engineering, data science or cyber security, or a terminal degree in architecture and aviation, in addition to college-level teaching experience are required.

Candidates with experience in the following areas are preferred:

* College administration

* Proposal development and submission to funding agencies for support of educational programs and research

* Government contracting

* Contract management and

* Financial and personnel management

The successful candidate must have excellent written and oral communication skills, a history of scholarly achievement, and the ability to organize effectively in a multidisciplinary environment. He/she must value diversity, especially as it pertains to the merits of historically black colleges and universities (HBCUs).

Position summary:

Celgene's IKU (Information, Knowledge, Utilization) organization was formed to manage healthcare data as a core asset and harness its derived insights to drive decision-making across the entire organization; from research through to commercialization. Data Management within IKU is the proactive alignment of Celgene's data science needs, data governance standards and our growing network of strategic data partners. As the complexity and sources of healthcare data rapidly evolves, Data Management ensures that IKU's systems and capabilities evolve with it; allowing us to meet our changing data science needs and fuel the development of innovative medicines for patients.

The Director of Data Management is a core member of the Data Sciences team. S/he works closely with both the data governance and alliance management teams to ensure the acquisition and maintenance of the data necessary to support Celgene goals. As a member of the Data Sciences team, s/he will also be responsible for the execution of observational research projects, as needed.

The ideal candidate will be a seasoned researcher with significant experience in the design, execution and analysis of large-scale observational healthcare studies. This experience should include the execution of studies through multiple partner organizations, requiring the collection and integration of independently generated clinical and molecular datasets. Along these lines, s/he will also be very familiar with the taxonomies, ontologies and vocabularies commonly employed in healthcare research. S/he will be experienced working in environments demanding diverse data sources, such as clinical trial data, electronic health record data, insurance claims data, and disease/patient registry data. The candidate must be hands-on and action oriented with a demonstrated ability to actively listen, integrate input from diverse sources, and collaboratively translate medical or business questions into data requirements and a clear path forward.

This role will be required to maintain a deep scientific and analytical understanding of healthcare data, from data generation, collection, integration to its analysis and interpretation. This role will also be empowered to actively improve and innovate upon each of those areas.

Overall responsibilities include:

* Directly participate in multiple data science projects

* Work directly with business development and alliance management team to grow our network of data and technology partners

* Work directly with the data governance team to ensure the adoption and evolution of data standards (internally and across our partners) necessary to leverage data assets throughout the organization

* Collaboratively develop analysis plans based on clinical or business questions.

* Represent the IKU Data Sciences group and its capabilities in meetings with nontechnical peers

* Work in a fast-paced multidisciplinary dynamic environment, and as necessary create examples, prototypes, demonstrations to help the business better understand innovative solutions

* Collaborate with team members to meet project checkpoints and accomplish team objectives

* LI-EX

Qualifications

Education and Experience Guidelines:

* 8+ years of experience in pharmaceutical / biotechnology / healthcare / health IT industry

* 8+ years of hands-on experience managing observational healthcare research studies

* 8+ years of hands-on experience working with large real world databases (EMR, Claims, etc)

* Advanced degree (MSc., MPH, PhD) in Biostatistics, Epidemiology, Computer Science, Informatics, Mathematics, or related field with a substantial statistical component; or postdoctoral fellowship and/or accredited training in data-related sciences with a substantial statistical component

Technical Experience Guidelines:

* Hands-on knowledge of R, Python, and/or SAS

* Working knowledge of SQL and relational databases

* Experience analyzing data from traditional healthcare databases (electronic medical records, claims, etc) is preferred

* Experience with Hadoop platforms (ex Cloudera, Hortonworks) and related "big data" technologies is preferred

Experience working in Cloud Computing environments (ex AWS, Azure, etc) is preferred

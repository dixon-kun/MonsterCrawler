             External Job Description
            As a member of the LNO Student Success team, the Executive Director, Student Science is responsible for establishing the data science vision, strategy, and best practices for the LNO Student Success team. This leader will consult on practical applications for improving customer outcomes across the network, working closely with partner institutions in adoption of evidence-based best practices.
              Lead and facilitate discussion and needs analysis globally and regionally with senior executives and key stakeholders&#160;for student success efforts and initiatives for each Laureate International University.
              Drive revenue, reputation and thought leadership for LNO and the Laureate network of universities through the application of cognitive-behavioral insights.
              Manage a group of external advisors in relevant cognitive and behavioral science areas
              Lead the conceptual design and deployment of a Laureate proprietary student behavioral cognitive profile model supporting retention efforts, service optimization, and personalization.
              Lead, collaborate and work with local universities and key contacts in assembling, updating, and reporting student success initiatives for Laureate universities
              Design and execute research interventions towards improving Retention, Student Engagement and Employability outcomes for Laureate.
              Contribute to the productization of Retention products with focus on scaleability, institutional fit and efficiency across the network. (e.g., Customer Effort Score, Customer Profile, Early Success Toolkit)
              Drive cross-functional collaboration with global institution stakeholders to ensure clarity in project goals and alignment of priorities, timelines and objectives.
              Develop the process for systematically testing new innovations towards improving the customer experience.
              Collaborate with institutions and influence leaders and stakeholders to develop their retention strategy, based on maturity and modality
              Maintain close awareness of and contact with leading edge research, products, and services relative to student success;
              Collaborate with LNO operations in the areas of MBI (Predictive Analytics) to deliver product to clients effectively and on a timely manner
              Responsible for cross-functional management of global project teams dedicated to the execution of retention strategy within research pilots
              Drive student success findings in to key areas beyond student services such as enrollment, curriculum, faculty development and management.
              Responsible for collecting, consolidating and reporting key pilot data to LNO &amp; LIU management, &#8211; applying&#160; behavioral science insights to assist network decision making

            PROBLEM SOLVING
            The focus of this role is to apply cutting-edge knowledge and doctoral-level skills in behavior and cognitive science to solve key problems in student success &#8211;&#160; retention, graduation, and career outcomes &#8211; and to lead the incorporation of the solutions into products that will serve Laureate&#8217;s global network of institutions and students.&#160; Specifically, the incumbent will need to apply the following problem solving skills:
              Identifying and characterizing opportunities to improve student success
              Synthesizing Laureate and outside research to develop simple and powerful models of student success that will drive both product development and change management across the network
              Defining and conducting programs of research to understand Student Success issues more deeply, and to characterize potential solutions in terms of practicality, cost, impact, and change management requirements
              Working with senior leadership to identify and seize opportunities for Student Success thought leadership for Laureate

Western Union is changing. Yes, we're the company that moves money around the world quickly and reliably in 200 countries and territories. We are also present in over 510,000 locations bringing immense distribution convenience to our consumers. But we're on the cusp of becoming much more than that. Our vision is to be the preferred financial services provider for the underserved – billions of people and businesses with unmet financial service needs. That means aggressively pursuing digital, mobile, ATM/kiosk and stored value card strategies that meet the needs of today's global consumers and businesses. So, if you're looking for a company on the move – and truly one of the most diverse work environments on the planet – then Western Union could be for you.

We are looking for an Intern - Processing and Analytics to be based at our Global Headquarters in Englewood, CO.

As an intern, you will support the List Processing Systems and Analytics group within Global Compliance and engage with stakeholders to investigate, identify and resolve issues in list processing matters. Using statistics and data science, you will support the enhancement of the list processing algorithms. Located at Western Union's Global Headquarters in Englewood, Colorado (south Denver metro area), this internship will provide an opportunity to review Western Union's functions, core processes and business units.

Every intern candidate must meet the following requirements:

* Enrolled in a current undergraduate or graduate program tracking to a Bachelors or Master's degree

* Planned graduation between December 2017 and/or Spring 2018

* Ability to work full time and on-site at Western Union during the 10-week program, from June 5th to August 11th 2017

Qualifications

* Studies toward a Master's degree in Business Analytics, Data Science, Statistics, Mathematics, or Computer Science

* Must be enrolled as a MBA or Masters student when the Internship Program begins in June of 2017 (i.e. students who have already graduated when the program begins are not eligible for consideration)

* Data management skills with experience working with large data sets

* Strong interpersonal, analytical, problem solving, prioritization, decision making and conflict resolution skills.

* Experience working RDBMS architectures such SQL, DB2, MySQL or other relational databases and relational database programming.

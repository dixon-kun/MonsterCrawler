Noblis is building a behavioral analytics team to support a wide variety of client needs focused on detecting fraudulent electronic transactions/usage.  Cyber Fraud Forensic Analysis team members will work collaboratively with the Noblis Lead Data Analytics architects and data scientists to detect fraud through analytics capability and visualization in support of Federal Agencies. The Cyber Fraud Forensic Analyst will assist the customer in strengthening their security posture against fraudulent transactions, through detailed and accurate forensic cyber analysis and reporting.  Analysts will work closely with government personnel and other Noblis professionals in a team environment.

Responsibilities include:

  • Traige and interpret system logs
  • Detect fraud through analytic methodologies
  • Identify new trends for report development and analysis
  • Collaborate with Tool and Report Developers
  • Recommend improvements for the detection of fraudulent behavior through the analysis of data from many disparate sources
  • Author findings summary reports to enable partner organization awareness and response
  • Understand business processes and synthesize potential fraud scenarios and risk indicators
  • Interact directly with client project team members and operational staff

  • Summarize findings in concise and coherent manner for delivery to client senior leadership

  • THIS POSITION WILL BE SHIFT WORK. CURRENT NEED IS FOR 2 nd and 3 rd SHIFT


Required Qualifications

  • Must be able to obtain a Public Trust Clearance
  • Bachelor’s degree in a technical or quantitative discipline, or equivalent combination of education and/or work experience
  • A minimum of 8 years of related experience in Cyber Fraud Analysis
  • Understand network infrastructure; knowledge and understanding of Internet web transactions and associated traffic flows
  • Advanced Microsoft Office Suite skills with a focus on Microsoft Excel
  • Experience with Linux command line and editor
  • Ability to make informed analytic judgements and accurately and thoroughly document findings in a written and/or presentation format
  • Desire to help the customer make better business decisions through the analysis of cyber threats, system development and big data
  • Strong Analytic and creative problem solving abilities
  • Ability to perform pattern recognition and analysis
  • Strong communication, written, and organizational skills

Desired Qualifications

  • Experience in forensic log analysis
  • Progressive experience in data analytics; focus on internet web transactions is preferred
  • Programming experience
  • Ability to easily communicate complex ideas to senior executives
  • Experience within or directly supporting 24/7 watch function; familiarity with visualization tools, response procedures and key challenges in a watch environment
  • Highly inquisitive and ability to work both independently and in a collaborative setting
  • Strong teamwork, communication and interpersonal skills
  • Understanding of the current cyber, insider threat and cyber fraud landscape preferred
  • Applicants selected will be subject to a government security investigation and must meet eligibility requirements for access to classified information

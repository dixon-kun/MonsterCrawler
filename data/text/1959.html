Title: Behavioral Pharmacology Research Scientist / PACE (WRAIR Scientific Services)*
Date Posted: 04/06/2017
Job Location: Silver Springs Maryland

Job Description:

PRIMARY FUNCTION(S)

Serve as Behavioral Pharmacology Research Scientist at Walter Reed Army Institute of Research (WRAIR), in support of its biomedical research mission for the Center for Military Psychiatry and Neurosciences (CMPNS) mission to conduct basic and applied research to develop evidence-based strategies to diagnose, prevent, and mitigate the effects of psychological demands, continuous operations, and brain trauma on the health and well-being of Service Members. This position will conduct biomedical research that is responsive to Department of Defense and U.S. Army requirements and delivers lifesaving products including knowledge, technology, and medical materiel that sustain the combat effectiveness of the warfighter. Develop, implement, and utilize animal models that rely on general and cognitive performance to address specific problems of military relevance.

RESPONSIBILITIES AND DUTIES

Specific Responsibilities include but are not limited to :

  • Provide scientific expertise in behavioral pharmacology, animal models of stress, PTSD, experimental psychology of learning and memory, cognitive and general performance testing and assessment in animals, neuropharmacology, neurophysiology and advanced statistical analyses.
  • Prepare and obtain approvals for scientific animal-use protocols for the conduct of research.
  • Design and conduct animal studies.
  • Collect and analyze data from research studies.
  • Present preliminary findings at military and national scientific conferences.
  • Serve as author or coauthor on manuscripts resulting from data collection efforts.
  • Perform standard descriptive and inferential statistical analyses, such as advanced mixed-model ANOVA, using computerized tools such as SAS.
  • Produce graphical representations of collected data and summary results using PC-based computerized tools such as SigmaPlot, etc
  • May lead and direct the work of others.
  • A wide degree of creativity and latitude is expected.
  • Typically reports to head of a unit/department

MINIMUM QUALIFICATIONS:

  • Requires a Ph.D. in Experimental Psychology, Neuropharmacology / Pharmacology, Neuroscience or related field
  • Two years of research experience in an animal laboratory setting.
  • Knowledge and skills in Behavioral Pharmacology, Experimental Psychology, Neuroscience and animal models
  • Familiar with a variety of the field's concepts, practices, and procedures.
  • Demonstrated ability to conduct creative and productive research and implement in vivo behavioral techniques
  • Ability to utilize office automation equipment, including computers, photocopiers, telephones, and telefax machines.

PREFERRED QUALIFICATIONS

Desired experience 5 years.

WORKING LOCATIONS & ADDITIONAL INFO

WRAIR 503 Robert Grant Avenue, Silver Spring, MD 20910

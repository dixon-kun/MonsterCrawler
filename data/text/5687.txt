         About Citi
         Citi, the leading global bank, has approximately 200 million customer accounts and does business in more than 160 countries and jurisdictions. Citi provides consumers, corporations, governments and institutions with a broad range of financial products and services, including consumer banking and credit, corporate and investment banking, securities brokerage, transaction services, and wealth management. Our core activities are safeguarding assets, lending money, making payments and accessing the capital markets on behalf of our clients.
         Citi&#8217;s Mission and Value Proposition&#160; explains what we do and Citi Leadership Standards explain how we do it. Our mission is to serve as a trusted partner to our clients by responsibly providing financial services that enable growth and economic progress. We strive to earn and maintain our clients&#8217; and the public&#8217;s trust by constantly adhering to the highest ethical standards and making a positive impact on the communities we serve. Our Leadership Standards is a common set of skills and expected behaviors that illustrate how our employees should work every day to be successful and strengthens our ability to execute against our strategic priorities.
         Diversity is a key business imperative and a source of strength at Citi. We serve clients from every walk of life, every background and every origin. Our goal is to have our workforce reflect this same diversity at all levels. Citi has made it a priority to foster a culture where the best people want to work, where individuals are promoted based on merit, where we value and demand respect for others and where opportunities to develop to are widely available to all.
         The Sr. TPM Intelligence Officer is a key role within the TPM Intelligence team, a part of the larger Chief Third Party Management Office.&#160; The Chief Third Party Management Office has the responsibility to oversee the end to end management of Third Parties both External to Citi and Inter-affiliates owned by Citi.
         The role actively drives efforts to advance CTPMO&#8217;s assessment of internal and external third-party data and deliver meaningful information to the broader TP Risk &amp; Control teams, as well as to the executive and business management groups. A key focus area for this role is the identification of effective ways to monitor third-party landscape and control environment through risk modeling, development/evaluation, reporting tools and analysis on data sets representing approximately 17,000 external Third Party relationships and over 400 Inter-affiliate services spread across nearly 200 countries globally.
         This effort calls for close partnership with several other groups around the globe; most notably Enterprise Supply Chain, Finance (Inter Affiliate Risk Management Services) and sector and regional TPM teams. Through such collaborative efforts, the Senior TPM Intelligence Officer contributes to the thought leadership of the group by leveraging existing information, identifying new channels of data, and developing new and enhanced insights that can be helpful to Citi businesses&#8217; use and management of third parties as well as their strategic and tactical decisions.
         Given the growing importance of TPM intelligence, this role is also critical in providing in-depth and sophisticated analysis with interpretive thinking to address emerging problems and develop innovative solutions. Such efforts are also expected to drive CTPMO&#8217;s development of risk modeling capabilities and operationalizing their application.

         The Center for Excellence in Survey Research (CESR) of NORC at the University of Chicago seeks highly motivated candidates at all levels who are eager to join our vibrant and growing group to bring their data scientific and statistical expertise.
         Senior Statisticians oversee and offer expertise in statistical analysis activities such as small area estimation, causal inference, hierarchical linear modeling, experimental and quasi-experimental design, clinical trials, and biostatistical analyses. This position requires strong project and business development leadership skills. Senior statisticians should have a Ph.D. in Statistics or a related field plus five years of work experience in statistics including at least 3 years in a similar work environment (a combination of equivalent education and work experience may be substituted for the above requirements).
         Statisticians offer a wide range of statistical expertise, including statistical analysis, modeling, matching, small area estimation, and Bayesian analysis. Candidates should hold a Ph.D. in Statistics or Biostatistics, or an equivalent combination of education and experience. The positions require documented experience with statistical software such as SAS, R, or Stata.
         Senior Data Scientists employ their knowledge of predictive analytics methods and data mining to lead the company&#8217;s development of standards in data science. They will design, develop, and deploy data-driven predictive models to solve problems using technologies in data mining, statistical modeling, analytics platforms, and business intelligence. Other responsibilities include advising management on gaps in the company&#8217;s current technical capabilities, recommending plans for growth, and mentoring the professional development of new and existing staff in the field of data science. Senior Data scientists should have a strong statistical background; 5-7 years of experience in data mining, predictive modeling, or another data intensive analytical role; a Master&#8217;s or higher degree in Computer Science, Information Services, Mathematics, Statistics, Economics, Public Policy, Operations Research, or a related discipline; experience working with large-scale databases and big data frameworks; and experience with SAS, R, SQL, and business intelligence tools. Experience in analytical visualization and knowledge other modern programming languages, such as Java, C, or Perl, is highly desirable.
         Data Scientists apply data mining, statistical modeling, analytics platforms, and business intelligence to solve problems and answer questions for clients. They will employ a wide array of advanced data mining techniques in support of research and operational analytic projects. Other responsibilities include contributing to methodological standards and supporting business development activities for the company&#8217;s data mining and predictive analytics capabilities. Candidates should have a strong statistical background; 3 years of experience in applied data mining, predictive modeling, or another data intensive analytical role; a Bachelor&#8217;s or higher degree in Computer Science, Information Services, Mathematics, Statistics, Economics, Public Policy, Operations Research, or a related discipline; and experience with SAS, R, SQL, and business intelligence tools. Experience in analytical visualization and knowledge other modern programming languages, such as Java, C, or Perl, is highly desirable.
         Statistical Analysts contribute to a variety of statistical activities, including sampling, survey analysis, regression, statistical modeling, experimental design, Bayesian methods, data mining, and predictive modeling. Analysts should have knowledge of statistical methods; a Master&#8217;s degree in a quantitative discipline, such as Statistics, Biostatistics, Mathematics, Survey Methodology, Economics, Information Systems, Data Analytics, or related field OR a Bachelor&#8217;s degree in one of these fields with four years of relevant experience; and experience in SAS, R, or Stata.
         Statistical Assistants provide programming, research, and project management support to survey sampling and statistical research projects. Programming activities include writing SAS programs to select samples, calculate weights, and impute missing data, performing data analyses using SAS, Stata, R, SUDAAN, or other statistical packages, and producing descriptive statistics and tables. Research duties may include assisting with writing reports and memos and performing literature reviews. Project management tasks involve tracking the financial progress of projects using Excel, preparing reports for and attending monthly project reviews, and assisting with budgeting and contract proposals. Statistical Assistants should have a Bachelor's degree with an excellent academic record, including courses in Statistics, Mathematics, Computer Science, and the social sciences, and academic or work experience performing data analysis using a statistical computer package.

        SUMMARY
        The primary purpose of the Program Coordinator position is to perform complex coordinator/ administrative support duties requiring judgment within general guidelines. Provides high-level departmental/faculty senior administrative clerical support encompassing a broad skill sets including: Administrative support for Chair, Genomic Medicine/Director of the Center for Survivorship Genomics/Robert A. Welch Distinguished University Chair.
        KEY FUNCTIONS
        1. Departmental Project Coordination
        * Coordinates the administrative operations of various projects and programs related to Genomic Medicine and/or the Therapeutic Discovery Platform; acts as a liaison and communicates with internal and external customers to arrange business meetings (via WebEx or in-person) and/or business travels arrangements.
        * Coordinates all aspects of the recruiting process for potential candidates including scheduling interviews, coordinating schedules, organizing and preparing interview itineraries, escorting candidates to and from interviews, arranging departmental lab tours, and compiling overall interview evaluations.
        * Manages the HR process for departmental contractors, consultants, and employees; institutional vetting, badges, security access, and office locations.
        * Coordinates the bi-monthly research seminars for Genomic Medicine; organizes speakers, coordinates travel arrangements, creates seminar and speaker itineraries, process honorariums and reimbursements, disseminates seminar information, and correspondences.
        * Coordinates with other departments to assist with the preparation of the Annual Research MDACC Symposium.
        * Maintains current scientific, technical, and regulatory environment information concerning relevant developments related to various projects and/or programs.
        * Participates in conferences and serves on committees as requested by Chair and/or Department Administrator.
        * Provides specialized administrative support to faculty, department administrator, and research laboratory staff.
        2. Faculty Support
        * Coordinates and schedules a large volume of daily appointments in Outlook for a complex calendar for the Chair, Genomic Medicine/Director of the Center for Survivorship Genomics/Robert A. Welch Distinguished University Chair, exercising judgment and understanding of work environment, priorities, and preferences.
        * Possesses knowledge of institutional and external forms and contacts to facilitate completion of projects and making trustworthy judgment calls on behalf of the Director of the Center for Survivorship Genomics &#8211; Moonshots Program
        * Facilitates the operation of office functions for assigned faculty, including the coordination of schedules and itineraries for visitors.
        * Maintains the most current information pertaining to faculty CV, periodical publications, and biographical sketch.
        * Processes faculty extramural leave and PTO leave requests.
        3. Support for Committees/Councils
        * Plans and facilitates standing meetings of several institutional committees and councils (Moonshots Programs - BIG Data, Research &amp; Education) on behalf of the Director of the Center for Survivorship Genomics. Tasks include securing venue, notifying and reminding attendees, communicating with guests, arranging catering, providing logistical support if necessary, recording attendance and coordinating travel.
        * Provides support for Moonshots Projects (Big Data &#8211; OEA as well as CGL and IMT). The Director of the Center for Survivorship Genomics instituted the creation of unified Cancer Genomics Laboratory for the Moonshots Program, merging Genomic Medicine and IPCT initiatives, serving as Scientific Director. Leading the genomics aspect of the Institutional Big Data platform for Moonshot initiative.
        * Provides support for all program and project meetings and sub-meetings supporting projects led by the Director (e.g., BIG Data Science Project Initiatives).
        * Makes trustworthy decisions on Director's behalf, whenever needed pertaining to communication and deadlines.
        * Coordinates meetings and presentations for the purpose of providing progress, updates, and goals and objectives of each project.
        * Coordinates meetings/interviews with Executive Candidates monthly. Director serves as active member of Executive Recruitment Committee.
        4. Research Laboratory Support
        * Provides administrative support to Faculty and Research Laboratory Staff.
        * Manages and coordinates all aspects of lab candidates: Utilize the appropriate MDACC systems to make all necessary travel arrangements (flight, lodging, and meals); organize and prepare meeting itineraries and lab seminars; organize lab luncheons; escort lab candidates to and from interviews/meetings; and arrange departmental lab tours.
        5. Financial Management: Expense Review &amp; Reconciliation
        * Compares statistical sample report expenses with expense documents received for purchases made or services performed. Completes Statistical Sample Reports in SharePoint monthly.
        * Receives and files expense/supporting documents and statistical sample reports to insure that they are accessible, when needed.
        * Notifies department administration of overdrafts and potential problems with accounts.
        * Maintains knowledge of institutional accounting policies and procedures.
        6. Travel and Reimbursement Management
        * Using Concur - arranges and manages heavy and often complex domestic and international travel for Chair. Ensures that all reservations are made timely for early registration.
        * Completes all documentation related to travel, such as travel requests, itinerary, documentations folders, etc.
        * Responds quickly and accurately to updates and changes to travel plans. Confirms all reservations (air, hotel, event logistics and itinerary, etc.) prior to attendance.
        * Maintains knowledge of travel and reimbursement policies and procedures.
        * Prepares and submits expense reports and travel reimbursement requests accurately.
        7. Communication
        * Provides telephone support for the Futreal Lab - answering and managing all phone calls, with the understanding of and sensitivity to current topics, situations and relationships. This also includes taking a complete message with accurate date, time, name, number and information.
        * Makes trustworthy decisions on behalf of Chair in his absence.
        * Provides clear communication, whether over the phone or face-to-face must be understandable and handled with courtesy, accuracy, professionally and with respect for confidentiality.
        * Composes, proofreads and edits correspondence, tracking routine forms and correspondence.
        * Demonstrates grammatical proficiency in English in written communication and has knowledge of standard professional practices in writing emails, memorandums, letters, invitations, etc., as well as knowledge of institutional branding standards and correspondence practices.
        * Arranges meetings/conferences according to department specifications, including booking rooms, catering and equipment.
        * Prepares agendas for routine meetings and Moonshots Program related meetings.

         The Marketing Analytics Sr. Associate is a senior-level business and data analyst role specializing in business requirements definition, data specification, and business intelligence design for cross-platform and cross-LOB marketing analytics projects.   The analyst coordinates subject matter experts across multiple teams to identify, understand, and articulate analytical and reporting requirements that enable JPMC to understand the performance of various marketing initiatives.   The analyst uses these requirements to ensure data designs can meet the analytical business requirements.   The analyst ensures development teams understand the requirements and associated data and validates that resulting solutions function properly.  The analyst works closely with the business intelligence team to understand how data will support reporting and visualization.
          Become a subject matter expert on marketing analytics and the operational systems used to build and execute marketing campaigns.
          Lead the business and data requirements collection process for analytical projects that support new marketing capabilities.
          Clearly articulate business and data requirements through written and verbal communication.
          Describe how data is used to deliver reporting and analytics that will be used to measure the effectiveness of new marketing systems, capabilities, and campaigns.  Ensure reporting will be delivered that measures the strategic intent of marketing projects.
          Partner with the Business Intelligence team to document and communicate the design of reports and dashboards, the data inputs required to generate them, and the resulting outputs.
          Work closely with vendors to understand what data they can provide and how that data should be provided to Chase and integrated with internal data assets to deliver analytics.
          Obtain approval from all stakeholders and impacted teams that requirements and data designs will deliver the required analytics.
          Provide business analysis and analytics expertise to the Marketing Roadmap / Personalization @ Scale program that seeks to standardize data, process, and technology across the various marketing execution teams within Chase.  Partner closely with Marketing Services personnel to drive data standardization decisions and business requirements collection for analytics and reporting.
          Assist with writing business requirements documents and performing user acceptance testing for all Marketing Analytics Platform (MAP) releases.
          Construct data preparation algorithms using tools like Alteryx to prepare data for analytics.
          Construct reports and dashboards using Tableau.
          Discover new possibilities for business analytics resulting from the introduction of Hadoop and its related technologies.
          Qualifications
          A minimum of five years of related business, data, and/or analytics experience.
          Bachelors degree in Science, Business or Arts is required.  A degree MIS, computer science, data science, or a related data/analytics subject area is a plus.
          Strong command of business requirements collection and documentation is required.
          Understanding of data, analytics, and reporting is required.
          Experience working with business intelligence tools such as Tableau.
          Excellent command of the SQL language and experience querying relational databases.
          Excellent planning, interpersonal, problem solving skills are required.
          Excellent communication skills, including verbal, written and presentation necessary. Active listening skills are required.
          Excellent people and collaboration skills. Ability to bring people together to accomplish shared objectives.
          Tenacity with digging into data, systems, and documentation to understand how things really work in an effort to deliver the best requirements and data design.
          Ability to navigate the political dynamics inherent in a large organization.
          Effectively facilitate meetings and cross-functional teams.
          Understanding of Big Data technologies such as Hadoop is a plus.  Willingness to learn these new technologies is essential.

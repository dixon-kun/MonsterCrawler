Assistant Teaching Professor– MS in Information Systems Multidisciplinary Graduate Engineering—College of Engineering Boston, MA campus The Northeastern University College of Engineering Multidisciplinary Graduate Engineering group offers 6 different Master of Science degrees in engineering fields that are interdisciplinary, draw on industry experience, and are professionally relevant
The enrollment in these programs are growing rapidly with the number of new students joining Northeastern each fall more than tripling over the last 5 years
To support the teaching and advising involved with these programs and their continued growth, the Northeastern University College of Engineering seeks to fill a non-tenure-track teaching faculty position to create a community of students in the M
S
in Information systems program at the Boston, Massachusetts campus
MS in Information Systems focuses on the intersection of cutting-edge technology and business complexity with an emphasis on the art and science of designing intricate software applications especially in the areas of healthcare, security and privacy, auditing and compliance, business improvement and intelligence, as well as financial accountability
The MSIS program offers key courses in the following areas: Application Engineering and Development, Web-methods and Tools, Smartphone/Mobile Web Development, Engineering Big-Data Systems, Data Sciences, Optimizing Map-Reduce Platforms, Big-data Architecture and Governance, Network Structure and Cloud Computing, Big-Data System Engineering using Scala, Advanced User Experience Design and Testing, Big-Data Systems and Intelligence Analytics, Foundations of Parallel, Concurrent, and Multithreaded Programming, and Blockchain and Cryptocurrency Engineering
MSIS students take managerial classes to strengthen their organizational and communication skills as well as their real-world knowledge
These courses include Business Analysis, Agile Development, Quality Assurance and Automation, Healthcare Service Delivery, Business Process Engineering, Drug Development Processes and Information Systems Compliance, Organizational Change and IT, IT Auditing, Enterprise Architecture, Business Intelligence and Data Warehousing Engineering, as well as Financial Accounting Systems
The main responsibilities for this teaching faculty position includes high-quality teaching, student advising, and service at the college and university levels
The position also requires scholarship, in particular related to pedagogical or industry inspired research/evaluation
For this position, service relates to identifying opportunities for program growth including partnerships with industry, faculty research programs within the college, development of new degree or certificate programs, new course development, and involvement across the various Northeastern’s Professional Advancement Network
National and International professional service activities are possible at the discretion of the faculty member and the College
This position is collaborative with the program director of the MSIS Program and other members of the growing Multidisciplinary Graduate Engineering group
Teaching assignments will focus on courses specific to the focus of the MSIS Program in Boston with a typical teaching load of 6 courses for the academic year

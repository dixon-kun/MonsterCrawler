        Job Summary
        The Department of Surgery at Denver Health is looking for a Biostatistician for Trauma and Acute Care Surgery.
        This full time statistician/ big data analyst will support our broad ranging work across multiple clinical studies examining drivers and outcome after severe traumatic injury and surgical disease.
        The ideal candidate will posess a broad skillset in data science including experience and expertise in performing statistical analyses, managing and merging large complex and diverse datasets and assisting investigators in statistical design and conduct of clinical studies.
        Additionally this position will be responsible for coordinating data compliance for multiple funded studies as well as generating reports of complex clinical data for compliance and quality improvement (QI), research, and operations.
        Essential skills include facility in broad statistical analyses using software platforms such as SAS, SPSS and/or R.
        This position will provide both formal and ad hoc analyses and report development in response to requests from end users as well as participate in manuscript, grant and presentation, preparation. Evaluates, writes, presents, and provides recommendations regarding clinical operations.
        Actively participates with Department of Surgery staff to prepare analyses and tables for QI and research. May provide guidance or expertise on clinical data.
        Minimum Qualifications
        Education:
        Master's degree in Biostatistics, Statistics or related field.
        Experience:
        Dependent on education. Previous statistics experience required. Typically at least 2 years conducting healthcare data analyses.
        Knowledge, Skills &amp; Abilities:
        Statistical analysis skills.
        Knowledge of SAS or SPSS. Knowledge of
        SQL, including the ability to write queries, create stored procedures, and develop complex joins and
        tables.
        Ability to create reports within preferably Microsoft SQL Server Report Services (SSRS), or a
        comparable program such as Crystal Reports. Ability to define the scope of work, develop an analysis plan, implement appropriate statistical methods using statistical software (SAS or SPSS), generate reports, and interpret results.
        Certificate/License/Registration: None All Certifications and Licenses required for this job must be
        kept current as a condition of continued employment.
        Denver Health is an integrated, efficient, high-quality academic health care system that is considered a model for the nation. The Denver Health system includes the Rocky Mountain Regional Level I Trauma Center, a 525-bed acute care medical center, Denver's 911 emergency medical response system, 8 family health centers, 15 school-based health centers, the Rocky Mountain Poison and Drug Center, the Denver Public Health Department, an HMO, and The Denver Health Foundation.
        As Colorado's primary safety net institution, Denver Health is a mission-driven organization that has provided more than $3.3 billion in care for the uninsured in the last ten years. Denver Health is a leader in performance and quality improvements and remains financially secure, in part, due to its nationally recognized implementation of lean principles in healthcare. Denver Health is a major resource to the community, serving approximately 185,000 individuals and 67,000 children a year.

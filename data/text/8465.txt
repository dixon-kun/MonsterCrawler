1)('Jobs.com', 1)('original', 1)    Load Forecast Analyst - NRG Energy, Inc. | Jobs.com
        NRG is leading a customer-driven change in the U.S. energy industry by delivering cleaner and smarter energy choices, while building on the strength of the nation's largest and most diverse competitive power portfolio. A Fortune 200 company, we create value through reliable and efficient conventional generation while driving innovation in solar and renewable power, electric vehicle ecosystems, carbon capture technology and customer-centric energy solutions. Our retail electricity providers serve almost 3 million residential and commercial customers throughout the country. More information is available at www.nrg.com. Connect with NRG Energy on Facebook and follow us on Twitter @nrgenergy.
        Education:
        Bachelor's degree in Analytics, Statistics, Economics, Data/Computer Science, or math/programming related field, Master's degree is preferred.
        Experience:
        The ideal candidate will have at least 1-2 years of energy experience in programming, statistical modeling, data science and/or forecasting. The candidate will also have other skills and proficiency in various areas, particularly in analytics, data science, or other math, economic or related disciplines, in order to show a pursuit of breadth and depth in experience and knowledge. The Analyst will have education or experience in data science and the skills to work with large datasets to generate insight and work products in support of the business. In addition, the Analyst should have some knowledge and proficiency in systems architecture as it pertains to customer data, risk and forecasting systems from a retail perspective. Lastly, the Analyst should have the ability to exhibit ownership in tasks and functions and the ability to take leadership in achieving tasks or projects.
        Responsibility:
        The primary responsibility is the operation of load forecasting processes and products for various NRG retail brands, including the development, maintenance, operation and review of load forecasting models. In addition, he / she will manage the effective communication of results, drivers, and feedback to stakeholders and cooperative groups regarding the forecast output, settlement analysis, and performance analytics in conjunction with their team. He / she will also be responsible for managing various projects both internal to the group as well as in conjunction with other teams.
        Secondary responsibilities include the development of new techniques, models, and/or processes to utilize the growing granularity and availability of market, utility/ISO and contract/load data to generate analytical insight on our customers. The candidate will be expected to leverage their experience, knowledge and tools to have a direct impact on the growth and development of advanced analytical capabilities and impact commercial decisions related to the business.
        Examples of knowledge and techniques:
        * Regression modeling, including neural-net, simple regression, and hybrid modeling
        * Thorough understanding of load and modeling constituents, including weather and transform functions
        * Statistical analysis to construct performance metrics (MAD, MAPE, etc)
        * Moderate to advanced programming skills (SQL, scripting / object-oriented coding including Python, Matlab, R, SAS, VBA)
        * Comprehension of deregulated power markets, portfolio management and optimization, energy supply stack, customer profiling
        * Thorough comprehension of load, including ISO-level, regional-, zonal-, congestion-level, customer profiles, C&amp;I and mass customers
        * Proficient in trending analysis, correlation to weather, attrition, conservation
        * Proficient in short- and long-term forecast (time scales from hours to decades)
        * Conduct original research and publish and/or present work in leading energy publications/ conferences
        * Knowledge of electricity-market hedge instruments, including settlement mechanisms, volatility attributes, and liquidity constraints
        * Knowledge of market load and deviation settlement mechanisms, capable of shadow-settlement calculations
        * Provide valuable insights to marketing to advance cross selling initiatives such as providing solar to retail customers and vice-versa
        * Ability to represent the Company in external forums such as PUC and ISO meetings
        * Leadership skills to manage, delegate work, and provide skill development to their subordinates
        * Ability to manage projects and meet / exceed deadlines
        The Analyst is expected to work in a fast-paced environment, along with a strong ability to multi task. Often, the Analyst will have advanced computer skills to be able to develop applications to streamline processes, extract and mine large datasets, develop front-end applications for visualization, work alongside IT for bug-fixes, code enhancements, and user-testing. The Analyst is expected to possess a fundamental knowledge of weather and energy businesses (portfolio optimization, applying fundamental analysis to enhance load forecasts). The individual will also be able to convey not just the forecast output, but provide risk to the forecast (upside / downside) based on fundamental information. Furthermore, the Analyst is expected to help ensure the success of the broad group in accordance with company standards and policies.

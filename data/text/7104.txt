            Who we are:
           At The Urban Institute, we believe in the power of evidence to improve lives and strengthen communities.&#160; Our employees are empowered to influence policy, open minds, shape decisions and offer solutions.&#160; We encourage intellectual honesty, innovation, diversity, and mutual respect, and our analysis elevates debate, wherever it takes place.
           The Urban Institute Summer Internship is a PAID 8 &#8211; 10 Week program designed to provide students who are interested in research and public policy issues with &#8220;rewarding and substantive&#8221; work assignments that are valuable to our organizational mission.
           Our Data Technology and Innovation Team is currently looking for a Data Science Intern to work in a cutting edge, big and unstructured data environment. The department is made up of polyglot programmers who use tools such as C, C++, Fortran, Python, R, and SAS to translate public policy and proposals into algorithms and code.
            How you'll contribute:
            Apply modern data science methods to public policy data and extract new data sources and insights.
            Create automated data collection systems using webscraping, database, and cloud computing technology to collect datasets to answer policy questions.
            Produce an automated data collection system (with assistance) and two blog posts, or similar brief analytical documents, including statistical analysis and/or visualizations of public policy data.
            Contribute to ongoing projects within the Data Technology and Innovation team based on your interests. Potential projects could include:&#160; translating new unstructured data into structured formats for researchers.
            What you'll bring:
            Students pursuing a Bachelor&#8217;s or Master&#8217;s Degree in Computer Engineering, Computer Science, Mathematics, Political Science, Public Policy or related fields
            Competence performing data analysis, or programming in general, in R, Python, or Ruby
            Experience in one of the following: Data visualization, cloud computing, SQL, Docker and/or Web Scraping, however the intern will be given training and support to learn these systems and tools
            Interest in domestic economics and/or social public policy issues
            Ability to clearly communicate and write for a non-technical policy audience
            What you'll experience:
            Participation in our &#8220;Welcome&#8221; Orientation
            Opportunity to network with Researchers, Senior Professionals and others Interested in your professional growth and interests
            Professional Development such as workshops and brown bags in addition to attending Public Urban Institute events
            Informal feedback and check ins with your intern manager
            Opportunity to present the value of your contribution and what you learned during our End of Summer Presentations

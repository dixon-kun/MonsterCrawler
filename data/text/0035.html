TheU.S. Department of Justice (DOJ), Office of the Inspector General (OIG), AuditDivision, is looking for graduate students currently enrolled in graduate degreeprograms for Operations Research, Data Science, Data Analytics, or ComputerScience within the Washington DC commuting area who are interested indeveloping their skills in these areas.

TheOIG is a statutorily created independent entity whose mission is to deter anddetect waste, fraud and abuse and misconduct in DOJ programs and personnel, andto promote economy and efficiency in those programs. The Audit Division alsomonitors DOJ contracts, grants and other agreements. The Inspector General(IG) is appointed by the President and subject to Senate confirmation, andreports to the Attorney General and Congress. The OIG investigates allegedviolations of criminal laws by DOJ employees and also audits and inspects DOJprograms.

Thisexcepted service position is being filled under the provisions of the PathwaysInternship Program which is designed to provide students enrolled in a widevariety of educational institutions, from high school to graduate level, withpaid work opportunities. The program allows students to be exposed to thework of the Government through Federal internships while pursuing their degree.The internship provides federal managers a ready source of studentemployees and potential permanent employees upon completion of the programrequirements. It also provides the student with work experience directlyrelated to the curriculum studies or career goals. This position is located inWashington, DC in the Office of Advanced Audit Techniques within theAudit Division. This job requires physical presence at the position location. If this sounds like the perfect fit for you,we invite you to join our team as a Pathways Intern!

Uponsuccessful completion of the internship program, you may be eligible fornon-competitive conversion to a full-time permanent position.

Additional selections may be made if additional vacancies occur within 90 days of certificate issuance.

This announcement will remain open until the applicant pool reaches 150 applications or until the closing date.

Duties

Thisposition is for an Operations Research/Data Analytics trainee in the Office ofAdvanced Audit Techniques and Data Analytics located at the Audit Divisionheadquarters in Washington DC. Theintern will work with experienced staff on a variety of projects that will increasethe intern's knowledge, skills, and abilities in the areas of data analytics,operations research and statistics. Assignmentswill increase in complexity and difficulty as the intern's experienceincreases. The intern will be assignedcollaborative tasks in technical areas required to develop as a dataanalyst/operations research analyst/statistician. Specific assignments and responsibilities mayinclude:

* Assistingwith planning and carrying-out clearly defined studies and projects, under theguidance of an experienced Operations Research Analyst, who has overallresponsibility for the study or project. Applying data analytics,operations research and statistical theories, concepts and principles to work.Using SAS, SPSS Statistics, R and Tableau software to analyze relevant data.

* Discussingscope and objectives of research with supervisor, locating and reviewingrelevant research literature to help develop methodologies.

* Discussingappropriate methods, techniques, and procedures with supervisor and applyingagreed upon methodologies to obtain and analyze data and information on a broadrange of issues. Discussing possible adaptions to accepted practices withsupervisor, and implementing as necessary.

* Performingwork to exacting and clearly stated standards.

* Preparingpreliminary reports, or sections of reports according to establishedprocedures. Documenting study results and writing accurate reports forreview by supervisor. Helping to interpret data and prepare data in finalform for publication.

* Describingmethods and procedures used, and summarizing data obtained and descriptions ofproblems or unusual situations encountered in carrying out studies.

The position requiresthe intern to work a specific schedule determined by the supervisor andcandidate, based on the needs of the organization and the schedule of thestudent.

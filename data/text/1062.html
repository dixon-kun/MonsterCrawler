A Day in the Life of a Business Intelligence Analyst:

In Business Intelligence, You Need to Be...

  1. Knowledgeable in the tools and techniques needed to transform raw data into information.
  2. Capable of empowering other departments in the company to leverage advanced technology and industry techniques.
  3. Proactive in turning this information into new business strategies.
  4. Insightful in providing historical, current, and predictive views of business key performance indicators.
  5. Skilled in verbally and graphically communicating data findings.

Here's More About What You'll Do

  • Identify intelligence needs of each department
  • Empower other departments in the business to leverage technology
  • Research and analyze areas of interest to the company which will allow the company to gain a competitive advantage
  • Increase the efficiency of old business processes and aid in the development of new business processes
  • Perform market research and analysis
  • Participate in management meetings related to intelligence reporting
  • Implement the recommendations of intelligence reporting and management
  • Develop an understanding of each department's role within the company; maintain an advanced knowledge of their needs to remain competitive

We look for all kinds of backgrounds and skills for Business Intelligence Department. Most of all we want people who fit our high-performance culture and are able to add continuous value to our company.


Education: Bachelor’s degree in Business Management, Economics, Finance, Accounting, Analytics, Data Science, Math or a related field required.

Experience:

  • Experience working with large data sets and turning them into insight.  Prior experience working  in ecommerce or marketing environment preferred
  • Experience with cost flow and financial data (including margins). Appreciated but not required. We do a lot more than finance
  • Experience managing cross-functional projects

Computer Skills:

  • Expert level Microsoft Excel. Your life will be much better if you know Excel
  • Familiarity with relational databases (SQL) a plus. If you don’t know it you will learn it quickly
  • Familiarity with some BI software

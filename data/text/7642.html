About the Job

NETE providesstrategic management consulting, business systems development and integration, Web design and technology solutions, and managed outsourcing services to commercial companies and federal government agencies.
NETE supports the major federal agency that funds biomedical research. We seek a highly motivated, flexible, organized, and detail oriented Mid-level Data Architect to join our accomplished team and play a key role in a new exciting project. What you do at NETE will have a significant impact on the biomedical research community. If you want to learn, grow, and contribute to exciting projects then this is the job for you.
The ideal candidate would be a Data Architect with expertise in Open Source technologies as well as knowledge and experience in research data, scientific publications, and other documents. Good communication and interpersonal skills are essential. Qualified candidates will have a strong academic background in computer science and ideally data science/statistics.
Responsibilities:
  • Work closely with a scientific lead, and data engineers to design, and implement an evolving vision for a novel data warehouse solution centered on linkages between funding data, research artifacts, scientific outputs such as publications and patents, and scientific impact. This data warehouse is based in the Azure cloud and uses custom Python code for ETL, PostgreSQL, and Apache Spark.
  • Apply and enforce best practices for extracting, transforming and integrating new data sources and developing workflows for curation of these data.
    • Location: Rockville, MD


  • Bachelor’s degree in Computer Science, Information Systems or related field. Master's degree in Computer Science, Information Systems or related field is a plus.
  • 3-5 years or more of experience with Databases, ETL processes, XML and related technologies.
  • 3-5 years or more of experience with solution architecture across multiple domains such as business, data, integration, and application.
  • Strong experience in conceptual and physical data models, data security and API control.
  • Experience with tuning performance, scaling, and caching is a must.
  • Proven experience with advanced system design techniques.
  • Excellent understanding of data modeling across relational and graph databases.
  • Experience reviewing designs for robustness and quality.
  • Good understanding of architecture concepts, design patterns, tools, techniques and best practices.
  • Ability to communicate across diverse audiences and work in a team environment; strong organization and interpersonal skills.
  • Excellent knowledge in application of design techniques, methodologies, and languages.
  • Excellent knowledge of Software Development Lifecycle (SDLC).
  • Experience working with Open Source software, e.g. CentOS/RHEL, PostGreSQL, Python, Spark, required.
  • Experience working with research data, scientific publication data, and text documents; a plus.
  • Technology experience in building solutions using Spark, Cassandra, HBase, ElasticSearch is a plus.
  • Must be competent in SQL, Python, and Linux shell scripting.
  • Research experience, a plus.
  • Experience with system administration of cloud-based Linux servers, a plus.
  • Competency in any or all of R, Java, Scala, PySpark, and SparkR, a plus
  • Professional certification(s) desired.
  • Applicants selected will be subject to a Public Trust background security investigation and may need to meet eligibility requirements for access to sensitive information. US Citizens or Permanent Residents preferred.

        The Propulsion Engineering Data Analyst/ Scientist will be responsible for providing expertise and technicalleadership in applying numerical methods and algorithms to solve and analyzeproblems affecting aviation gas turbine engine performance. The Propulsion EngineeringData Analyst / Scientist will also act as a liaison between CAMP Systems andour engine OEM partner(s) to facilitate program execution and supportsuccessful completion of established short-term and long-term business objectives.
        This position will reportdirectly to the Vice President, Engine Health Management and will requiretravel to strategic engine OEM partners' facilities on an as needed basis.
        Responsibilities
        Primary interface providingtechnical expertise and leadership in applying numerical methods and algorithmsto solve and analyze problems involving gas turbine engine performance.
        Collaborate with cross-functionalbusiness units (both at CAMP Systems and OEM partners) to oversee all datamining, analytical analysis, quantitative problem definition, and solutiongeneration and optimization efforts.
        Establish and maintain effective OEMpartner relationships ensuring partner satisfaction through excellent serviceand communication.
        Stay abreast of advances in technologyand services, especially as they relate to reporting, analytical processing,analytics, data mining, process mining, complex event processing, benchmarking,predictive analytics and prescriptive analytics.
        Communicate project status andperformance to stakeholders and multiple levels of management, as required.
        Other responsibilities asassigned.
        Requirements
        Bachelor's degree in thediscipline of engineering, applied mathematics or physics; advanced degree preferred.
        Minimum of 3-years relevant workexperience in data analysis, statistical analysis and modeling, preferablywithin the aerospace industry and with a working knowledge of gas turbine engineperformance.

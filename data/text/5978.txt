            . In these roundtable discussions, two students engage in an academic discussion with a top-tier university professor on specific topics pertaining to a given course. These discussions are filmed and will be used as part of a larger online classroom.
             These are one day, contractual positions.
            This is a paid project. Depending on the amount of prep work required, participants can expect to be paid $300 for a half day of filming on set and $600 for a full day. Filming will take place at our studio in Los Angeles, CA. While we typically ask graduate students to be a part of these discussions, there are some discussions that will require undergraduate students or recent graduates. The successful applicant will possess a positive attitude in addition to being bright and articulate!
            If chosen for roundtable discussions, you will be required to be on set at a scheduled time and day for filming with proper wardrobe. Information regarding schedule and wardrobe will be sent ahead of filming. In addition, the professor is likely to assign prep work for you to complete prior to your arrival on set. This usually consists of readings and videos. In most cases, the materials will be provided for you electronically. Prep work must be completed prior to your arrival at the studio. If you fail to do your prep work, you will not be paid in full!
            On the day of filming, you and another student will be asked to participate actively in the filmed discussion. This discussion will be led by the professor, referencing the prep work as needed.

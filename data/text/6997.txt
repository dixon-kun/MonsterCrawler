        Overview:     The Systems Analyst IV is responsible for supporting the analytic needs of the health system through the application of high level statistics and data science. This position requires experience in healthcare data and operations, coupled with an extensive knowledge of large data set development and quantitative data analysis methodology and analytical tools for reporting. The Systems Analyst IV routinely supports business decisions and operational processes with frequent interaction with other key team members and business owners across the organization. Design of complex analyses to support a variety of functions. Utilization of R or Python for statistical analysis of large data sets with an understanding of machine learning or data mining algorithms is required.
        - Create and maintain programs to perform analyses and generate both routine and ad hoc reports. - Engage in special programs such as Dashboard Metrics and Quality Improvement Programs to improve data gathering and reporting processes. - Identify areas of process improvement in analytical reporting and conduct data quality review. - Proven ability to conduct and interpret quantitative and qualitative analyses. - Develop exception reporting and conduct drill down analysis to proactively identify specific variances in medical costs and utilization. - Ability to generate a quality work product in a timely manner while maintaining a strong attention to detail. - Work autonomously and collaboratively with report requestors to define desired report requirements and validate results; communicate effectively with others. - Identify and communicate operational impact of analytical findings/decision support. Work collaboratively across departments to understand the needs of the organization in relation to analytic needs. - Research and recommend areas for improvement of data quality and reporting. Carry out other projects, activities, etc. as assigned. Analytical Skills: Must be able to work with large amounts of data including facts, reports, figures, and number crunching. Must be able to extract data and analyze it for accuracy, trends, or exceptions.

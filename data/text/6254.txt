        Monsanto is a sustainable agriculture company focused on enabling farmers around the world to produce more from their farms, both large and small. As our climate changes and population grows, we're striving to feed additional billions safely, affordably, and with minimal environmental impact.
        To meet this need, Monsanto has launched the world's largest effort to develop naturally-sourced microbial enhancers of plant health, productivity, and fertility. We believe microbes offer tremendous potential to deliver ecologically responsible and cost-effective benefits to crops around the world.
        The BioAg Alliance was established by Monsanto and Novozymes, with a goal to discover, develop and sell microbial solutions that enable farmers worldwide to increase crop yields with less input. As part of the BioAg alliance, Monsanto is investigating how soil microbes and the culturable and unculturable fractions of the microbiome interact to increase soil health and enhance the yield of row crops.
        Understanding the functional capacity of microbiomes in a culture-independent manner depends on our ability to extract information about genes from metagenomic datasets.

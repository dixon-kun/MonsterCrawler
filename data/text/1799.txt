          Job Description:
         The Security Operations Center Analyst is a member of the Cyber Hunt and Threat Analysis (CHTA) team with a focus on security intelligence, anomaly hunting, and incident response. You must leverage your intuition, expert security knowledge, and an array of tools to uncover malicious activity. You must maintain familiarity with multiple operating systems and attacker tactics, techniques, and procedures in order to triage and perform rapid analysis of real-time data feeds, looking for events of interest. You will perform preliminary malware analysis to help determine initial infection vectors, behavior, or the extent of an issue in support of a timely and successful incident response process. You will design and drive the implementation of original detection rules for various monitoring systems based on current threats, vulnerabilities, or your discoveries.
          Primary Duties/Responsibilities:
          Analyze SIEM event, other data feeds, intelligence, malware samples, and incident data for evidence of malicious or anomalous activity, and to better understand adversary TTPs.
          Design and drive the implementation of original detection rules and indicators of attack (IOCs) for various CSSP (monitoring) systems and subscriber systems on the basis of current threats, vulnerabilities, or other specifics pertaining to the CSSP subscriber environments.
          Develop custom tools or platforms to detect malicious activity at the host and network levels
          Conduct threat hunting, triage and analysis of (near) real-time and trended data feeds for potential intrusions or deviations from policies or organization baselines.
          Fuse locally derived and externally sourced cyber threat intelligence into signatures, detection techniques, and analytics intended to detect and track the advanced threat.
          Review and act as an escalation point for ongoing event analysis and malware investigations.
          Contribute to the development of operational processes &amp; procedures within the CSSP to support improvement of intrusion detection and response processes.
         Required Skills
          Expert level understanding in one core area of Information Security (network, host, application).
          Knowledge of Data Science techniques such as anomaly detection and machine learning
          Experience with McAfee EPO (or general host based detection and prevention suites such as OSSEC, Yara, MIR, CarbonBlack, Tanium, etc.)
          Experience with Network Security Monitoring (NSM) tools (Snort, Suricata, PCAP, tcpdump, etc.) and analysis techniques (alert, flow/session and PCAP analysis)
          Experience with IR, SIEM, Threat Intelligence, and Forensics tooling.
          Experience with Assured Compliance Assessment Solution (ACAS) (or general vulnerabilty scanning and network discovery tool such as Nessus, OpenVAS, or Core Impact)
          Experience with reverse engineering and related tools (e.g. IDA Pro/OllyDBG).
          Experience working in a multi-tenant/service provider environment.
          Understanding of common detection methods along Cyber Kill Chain and Post-Compromise TTPs.
          Experience with DoD IA/CND certification and accreditation programs
         Required Experience
          5 years&#8217; experience with a minimum of 2 years in one or more of the following: incident response, application security, network security, security operations, security monitoring, or security focused system&#8217;s engineering.
          Minimum one year experience with Splunk/Splunk Query Language (or general data analysis skills using SQL, big data technologies, or scripting).
          Minimum of one year scripting or programming experience in PowerShell, Ruby, Python, Shell/BASH scripting, Java, C/C++, C#, Perl, or other related languages.
          BS degree in Computer Science, MIS, Computer Engineering, Electrical Engineering, or equivalent work experience (work experience may be substituted for formal education).

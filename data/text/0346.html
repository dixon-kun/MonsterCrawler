Position Summary:

We are seeking a talented Clinical Laboratory Technician/Technologist – Data Analyst/Certifying Scientist Department to join our laboratory team in Huntington, NY. The selected candidate will ensure the legal defensibility of scientific data generated in a technical, high volume, laboratory operation. Duties include but are not limited to: data certification, assay integrity, expert witness, Key Summary reports, LC/MS/MS and GC/MS operation, problem solving, enforcement of and compliance to policies and procedures, process optimization, communication and reporting, record keeping and other laboratory operations in a forensic chain of custody environment.  We are offering flexible hours/days/scheduling.


Essential Functions:

  • Certify  and approve confirmation testing results for reporting
  • Maintain assay integrity and performance to ensure reproducibility and legal defensibility of all results
  • Provide expert witness
  • Review Key Summary reports
  • Promptly report concerns to all appropriate laboratory leadership and/or staff for effective feedback loop, quality improvement and training
  • Operate LC/MS/MS and GC/FID instruments for analysis: Injection scheduling, data processing, data review, advanced troubleshooting, advanced maintenance, instrument setup and validation
  • Review and approve QC validation tests to include: pool tests, subpool tests, internal standard tests, reagent tests
  • Contribute to, review and approve of method development and method validation
  • Assist with new technology and implementation of such
  • Collaborate with co-workers, managers and other supervisors to solve work-related problems within or between departments; answering and resolving questions/issues daily
  • Follow work schedules, assignments, and production sequences to meet production goals, respond to fluctuating workloads, and improve quality and productivity of laboratory operations
  • Participate in employee training for equipment, software and other laboratory operations
  • Read and comply with: SOPs, regulations, best practices, guidelines, standards, policies and procedures
  • Enforce compliance of all policies, procedures, standards, guidelines and best practices for: CLIA, safety, environmental, laboratory, hazardous materials, equipment, accident reporting and security
  • Personal accountability for attendance, productivity, training, and incidents
  • Participate in employee performance appraisals
  • Ensure that all work is complete, accurate and meets our high quality standards
  • Care for and maintain lab work area, furnishings and equipment
  • Participate in safety training, follow laboratory guidelines including hazardous materials and waste handling, life safety, medical equipment safety, accident reporting and security
  • Perform all other assigned duties as instructed or required


Education and Experience:

  • B.S. degree or higher in Chemistry or related scientific field
  • One year experience certifying non-negative (positive) confirmation data
  • High degree of computer literacy
  • Knowledge of Laboratory Information Systems software
  • Knowledge of CLIA and CAP (highly desirable)
  • Medical terminology (highly desirable)
  • Licensed by New York State Education Department (NYSED) as a Clinical Laboratory Technician/Technologist.


Knowledge, Skills, and Abilities:

  • Strong troubleshooting and problem-solving skills, able to work well with all team members
  • Understand and carry out instructions, reason and make judgments; perform arithmetic operations quickly and accurately
  • Strong work ethic, detail oriented, punctual, organized, personable, reliable and dependable
  • High motivation level, personal integrity and accountability, strong communication skills


Physical Demands/Working Conditions:

  • Light to moderate physical effort (lift/carry up to 25 lbs.) -Heavier weights with assistance
  • Standing for long periods of time
  • Repetitive motions and/or prolonged computer use
  • Working at a fast pace, subject to many interruptions and both physical and mental stress
  • Must wear provided appropriate personal protective equipment to include lab coat, gloves and face shield
  • Air-conditioned laboratory environment
  • Exposure to some odorous chemicals and specimens

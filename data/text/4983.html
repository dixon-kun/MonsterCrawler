Altamira brings a commercial mindset to solving the most complex national security problems by delivering mission application development, multi-intelligence analysis, and data science technologies and solutions to the defense, intelligence, and homeland security communities.  Altamira’s culture of innovation and excellence, and mid-market-size, positions us as the premier next generation leader bringing technology solutions to mission.

Altamira is an Equal Opportunity/Affirmative Action employer.  All qualified applicants will receive consideration for employment without regard to race, color, religion, sex, national origin, disability, or protected veteran status.

The successful candidate should have working knowledge of NISPOM security regulation DoD 5220.22-M.  Knowledge of processing personnel clearances, employee briefings, visit requests, ensuring the appropriate storage, marking, safeguarding, and disclosure requirements for the protection of classified information, physical security to include SCIF monitoring and to provide a wide variety of administrative and clerical duties.  Candidate should also have knowledge of the e-FCL submission site developed to submit required facility clearance information to DSS in electronic format.  Cybersecurity; threats and methods of operation, targeted information, countermeasures, and reporting requirements.  Counterintelligence awareness and reporting; Insider Threat awareness; the integration of counterintelligence into security programs; counterintelligence concerns in personnel security and foreign travel; research and technology protection; and threats to defense industry.

This position supports all on site employees and sub-contractors.

•Ensures on site Security Office Management procedures are followed and executed.

•Experience in preparing classified material for courier transport.  Knowledge of wrapping and marking classified materials for couriers.

•Initiates, prepares, processes and oversees the appropriate administrative documentation and correspondence relating to the mission requirements on site.

•Request clearance origination, transfer, reciprocity and termination of personnel security clearance investigations, re-investigations and positions of public trust through the HQs Security Office.

•Maintains access rosters for secure and unsecure areas pertaining to keys, combinations, swipe or pin accesses and badge requirements.

•Maintains onsite personnel security rosters (CPRL & CCIL) and suspense logs.

•Maintains visitor access log and sign in rosters.

•Conducts security briefings, Initial and Annual Training for all assigned personnel on site.

•Updates and maintains the security database, spread sheets or reports.

•Participates in audits and inspections

•Oversees all office administration requirements for support staff on site.

•Additional duties as assigned by the FSO/CSSO

Job Requirements:

• Must possess a current TS/SCI

•Five to seven years’ experience as a security professional, experience using JPAS a must have

•Experience with DSS and their review process and FSO Toolkit a must have

Administrative experience in office management a plus.

•Proficiency in Microsoft office products including Excel

•Must be a self-starter who is organized, detail oriented and can handle multiple tasks at the same time

•Provide input regarding security requirements and controls for systems where information is processed, stored, or transmitted to the Information System Owner.

•Implement personnel security requirements under JAFAN 6/4 and prepare DD 254's as needed.

•Understand and comprehend JAFAN 6/4 personnel security requirements; process program access requests (PARs), request for access (RFA) and cross overs; execute program indoctrination agreements (PIAs); review of SF86s, JPAS, Industry Partner Access Registration (IPAR) and other customer vetting processes to ensure individuals meet program eligibility requirements.

COMSEC experience preferred

•Familiarity with ICD 503 & 705 process.

•Understand how to ensure that the SSP, POA&M and all other security documents are prepared.

•Previous experience verifying the responsibilities of the ISSM/ISSO/ISSE are performed.

•Experience performing Contractor Company PSO Oversight.

•Ability to implement Government security requirements for Government and Industry facilities accreditation and information systems certification and accreditation to include co-use/joint use.

        Responsible for managing data science experiments from initial ideation through completion. The Data Science Project Manager (DSPM) will ensure data science experiments are defined and executed according to the ELC Data Science methodology, that all resources (across all teams, inclusive of business sponsors and SMEs) are appropriately engaged and communicated with, that deliverables are constructed according to standard operating procedure, collected in the appropriate repository and moved through workflow signoff.
        The DSPM will typically manage multiple data science experiments simultaneously for a variety of business sponsors. The DSPM will coordinate resources from across multiple groups including infrastructure, applications, corporate marketing, legal, information security, and Enterprise Architecture, depending on the nature of the science experiment.
        The DSPM will be accountable for developing the plan, securing commitment for all critical tasks from the resources executing them, providing regular (weekly) status updates to stakeholders, sponsors and team members, and providing other key communications.
        The DSPM will also manage risks and issues on a project basis and ensure that the project sponsors are aware of them in a timely fashion so mitigation or corrective action can be taken.
        Data science project management as a discipline
        Data science projects are iterative, with short "sprints" which result in business results to drive subsequent rounds of project direction and definition. In this way Data Science project discipline is similar to iterative software development methodologies such as "Agile."
        A good data science project manager will provide the best working environment for data science team members, removing them from all complexities and administrative work and ensure timely progress.

The Data Science and Advanced Analytics group is seeking an experienced survey sampling statistician to join its growing team. This group plays a key role within Abt SRBI providing survey sample design, data science methods, statistical consulting and analysis to a broad range of government agencies, university researchers, foundations, the media, and corporations. Much of their work is focused on public policy and policy evaluation in such areas as health policy, epidemiology, education, food safety, transportation and urban planning, the environment, financial institutions, and public opinion.

Position Summary:

The Senior Survey Statistician works with clients on sampling designs, communicating the strength and weaknesses of various sampling strategies, and representing Abt SRBI at research conferences (e.g., the Joint Statistical Meetings, the American Association for Public Opinion Research, Federal Committee on Survey Methodology, Total Survey Error, American Public Health Association, Transportation Research Board, and others).

Key Responsibilities:

• Determine appropriate sampling frames (ABS, landline and cell RDD, list samples) and modes (face-to-face, phone, mail, web, mobile apps, text messaging), or mixes of frames and modes, most appropriate for the client needs
• Develop sampling designs for complex surveys
• Identify strategies for sampling rare populations
• Work with large federal public use datasets, such as ACS, NHIS, CPS, QCEW and PDB, to develop optimal sampling strata, and to inform weight calibration procedures
• Conduct power analyses to determine required sample sizes
• Develop and implement weighting protocols for complex surveys (including creation of replicate weights)
• Perform model-based imputation
• Implement quality control procedures, including nonresponse and mode effect analyses, quantification of nonresponse and non-coverage biases
• Calculate response rates, standard errors and design effects
• Prepare methodological reports on sampling and weighting procedures
• Provide thought leadership through methodological research


Required Skills and Knowledge:

• Ph.D., ABD or Master's degree in statistics, quantitative survey methodology, or a quantitative social science field. Master's-level applicants should have extensive prior experience working on complex surveys in a setting of a survey research organization.
• Strong SAS, Stata, R or Python programming skills
• Working knowledge of sampling inference and applied sampling strategies
• Working knowledge of the total survey error paradigm, models of survey response, cognitive and social aspects of survey measurement
• Strong writing, presentation, and interpersonal communication skills

Preferred skills:

• Demonstrated ability to conduct methodological research
• Complementary expertise in additional areas of statistics and data science, including, but not limited to, mixed models, missing data, machine learning, text analytics, econometrics, or other quantitative social science areas

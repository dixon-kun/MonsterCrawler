        University Hospitals (UH) is a community-based healthcare system which serves patients at more than 150 locations throughout Northern Ohio, including seven wholly owned and four affiliated hospitals.
        Committed to advanced care and advanced caring, UH encompasses the regions largest network of primary care physicians, outpatient centers and hospitals. The network also offers specialty care physicians to treat almost every disease and condition, skilled nursing, elder health, rehabilitation and home care services, and occupational health and wellness. University Hospitals is the second largest private sector employer in Northeast Ohio and is within the top five largest private sector employers in the state of Ohio.
        Position Summary &amp; Essential Duties:
        Coordinates the clinical quality and transformation of practice to support the population health and clinical integration network:
        Key responsibilities include:
        * Leadership in the transformation of clinically integrated providers to support the quadruple AIM of improvements in quality, patient and provider satisfaction along with the reduction in the overall cost of care
        * Create, review and evaluate evidence-based guidelines and protocols to address the needs of populations being served. Engages with network physicians in development and utilization of the guidelines
        * In collaboration with quality committee, develops, tracks, trends specialty-specific quality indicators based on national standards and best practices
        * Using data from internal/ external sources aggregates care outcomes for a designated population with a focus on population health interventions including clinical and cost outcomes to improve the safety, effectiveness, efficiency and quality patient-centered care
        * Develops and maintains close working relationship with the data science/ clinical integration architect of UH Quality Care Network
        * Provides professional and technical support to care coordination/ management colleagues to ensure efficient operations across all practices along with system level teams. This includes assisting with processes, protocols, workflows and regulatory compliance
        * Leads development of training material, including distance learning, for providers on multiple aspects of quality and clinical integration. Facilitates the use of learning management systems for content delivery (LMS)
        * Provides reports on quality and outcomes, contract performance and training for multiple stakeholders
        * Thought leader in the evolution of clinical integration, supports academic curiosity in searching for innovative approaches to solving identified problems
        * Strong presentation skills with ability to share information to diverse groups on the focus and work of clinical integration
        * Provides support to provider relations and communications team members on provider recruitment and knowledge of the UH Quality Care Network
        * Performs other duties as assigned
        * This role may encounter Protected Health Information (PHI) as part of regular responsibilities. UH employees must abide by all requirements to safely and securely maintain PHI for our patients. Annual training, the UH Code of Conduct and UH policies and procedures are in place to address appropriate use of PHI in the workplace

About Augsburg College:

Augsburg College offers more than 50 undergraduate majors and nine graduate degrees to approximately 4,000 students of diverse backgrounds. With a student/faculty ratio of 15:1, the college focuses on close personal interaction with faculty and experiential education opportunities on campus and in the community. An Augsburg education is defined by excellence in the liberal arts and professional studies, guided by the faith and values of the Lutheran church, and shaped by its urban and global settings. See more information about Augsburg below.

Job Description:

The Augsburg College Computer Science Department invites applications for full-time tenure-track and non-tenure-track positions beginning August 15, 2017. Computer Science at Augsburg is a growing and dynamic department in an institution committed to liberal education, experiential learning, undergraduate research, and equity and inclusion. Augsburg College is a college of the city, providing an education based on critical analysis, leadership and responsible citizenship of a very diverse student body. We base our Computer Science on the standards of the Association of Computing Machinery.

We seek candidates who can contribute to a department poised for innovation, growth, and interdisciplinary collaboration. The Computer Science Department thus seeks candidates with a broad range of academic expertise, commitment to undergraduate education, and special interest in:

* Data Science and Analytics

* Computer applications to Business

* Complex Software such as compilers

* Computer Languages

Selected candidates will work in a growing department in the new Hagfors Center for Science, Business, and Religion, to be completed January, 2018. We have designed a Computer Science office space with offices surrounding a common area where students study and learn. Faculty members will have intimate contact with hundreds of students in all courses from an Introduction to Computer Science (CS 0) to Languages and Compiler Development.

Responsibilities for the tenure-track positions include the ability to teach a full range of undergraduate Computer Science courses, student advising, and service to the department and the College through committee work. Faculty will be expected to continue their scholarly work and involve undergraduates in their work.

Responsibilities for non-tenure track positions include the ability to teach a full range of undergraduate Computer Science courses, student advising, and service to the department and the College through committee work. Non-tenure track faculty members are not evaluated based on their active scholarly work.

Requirements:

Minimum requirements for Tenure Track positions include a PhD in a relevant field, and teaching experience; those for Non-Tenure Track positions include a Master's in a relevant field and teaching experience. We seek committed and creative teachers who will be responsible for teaching a diverse student body. Candidates should be open to extensive involvement in student activities.

Additional Information:

Among the distinctive features of the College are its deep dedication to civic and community engagement, and a strong belief in the integration of liberal arts and professional studies across all programs. The College's commitment to the local neighborhood and the world is enhanced through the work of four signature centers: the Christensen Center for Vocation, the Center for Global Education, the Sabo Center for Citizenship and Learning, and the Strommen Center for Meaningful Work. At both undergraduate and graduate levels, students actively engage in international travel and intercultural experiences that broaden their view of the world and deepen their engagement in it.

Augsburg's main campus is located in the vibrant Cedar-Riverside neighborhood of Minneapolis, one of the most diverse areas in the Twin Cities. The University of Minnesota West Bank campus and one of the city's largest medical complexes are adjacent to Augsburg; the Mississippi River and the Seven Corners Theatre district are just a few blocks away. In addition to its Minneapolis campus, Augsburg has a vibrant branch campus in Rochester, the fastest growing city in Minnesota and headquarters of the Mayo Health System. In addition to these locations, Augsburg owns and operates international sites in Mexico, Guatemala, and Namibia.

Application Instructions:

To apply for this position, go to http://www.augsburg.edu/hr to upload your cover letter, CV and official transcripts. As part of the online application process, you will be required to provide the names of three professional references. If your application advances in the search, you will be notified prior to Augsburg contacting your references. Application review will begin immediately and continue until the positions are filled. For priority consideration, candidates should apply by March 15, 2017.

Augsburg College is an Affirmative Action/Equal Opportunity Employer. All qualified applicants will receive consideration for employment without regard to race, color, religion, sex, sexual orientation, gender identity, national origin, veteran or disability status. Successful candidates will have demonstrated abilities working in an academic community committed to intentional diversity. Candidates are thus encouraged to highlight skills and experiences that demonstrate these competencies.

COMPANY PROFILE:

CHSi supports the health and well-being of civilian and military workforce partners in austere, remote, and security challenged environments outside the continental United States. With over 40 years of experience providing a full range of medical services, CHSi medical management solutions are comprehensive, responsive, and reliable. Our unique service delivery approach, international recruiting network, and proprietary processes and systems deliver secure and compliant solutions. Through solid performances in the U.S. and its territories, and in Iraq, Afghanistan, Kosovo, and Africa, CHSi’s solutions are built on a foundation of field-tested best practices. The CHSi service delivery approach includes onsite health centers, a national network of highly credentialed board certified medical staff, and medical readiness teams.

SCOPE:

The Data Scientist I shall be proficient at entering, cleaning, and conducting basic data manipulation and analysis. Operating in consultation with Preservation of the Force and Family (POTFF) program staff and the government’s Preservation of the Force and Family (POTFF) biostatistician. The data scientist will build and disseminate databases and spreadsheets designed to record Preservation of the Force and Family (POTFF) related programmatic data. The data scientist shall provide consultation and assistance to supported units and Preservation of the Force and Family (POTFF) staff to identify opportunities and methods for capturing data relating to Preservation of the Force and Family (POTFF) programs and initiatives. The data scientist shall prepare reports and presentations that accurately convey data trends and associated analysis as required.


** This position is notional and contingent upon contract award**

JOB RESPONSIBILITIES:

  • Entering, cleaning, and conducting basic data manipulation and analysis.
  • Operating in consultation with POTFF program staff and the government’s POTFF biostatistician, the data analyst will build and disseminate databases and spreadsheets designed to record POTFF related programmatic data.
  • Provide consultation and assistance to supported units and POTFF staff to identify opportunities and methods for capturing data relating to POTFF programs and initiatives.
  • Shall prepare reports and presentations that accurately convey data trends and associated analysis as required.

BASIC QUALIFICATIONS:

  • Bachelor’s degree in quantitative science, social science or related discipline.
  • Proficient with the suite of Microsoft Office programs, including Word, Excel and Access.
  • Basic proficiency with commonly used statistical software application (e.g. SPSS, SAS, and R)
  • Ability to obtain a TS/SCI or Secret security clearance based on work location.
  • Possess excellent communication skills and shall be highly detail oriented and organized.

OTHER DUTIES AND PREFERRED SKILLS:

  • Ability to work well within a fast–paced, team–oriented environment.
  • Ability to show a track record of solving large, complex problems
  • Experience in working with massive data sets, including structured and unstructured with at least 1 prior engagement involving data gathering, data cleaning, data mining, and data visualization
  • Knowledge of advanced data analytic techniques, including data mining, machine learning, statistical analysis, and Natural Language Processing.

          PROGRAM DESCRIPTION
         The Advanced Biomedical Computing Center (ABCC) is a part of the Data Science and Information Technology Program (DSITP) at Leidos Biomedical Research, Inc. The ABCC provides technology development, analysis, and scientific application support to NCI scientists in the areas of data science, information visualization, database architecture and applications, imaging and visualization, biomarker discovery, proteomics, mathematical and statistical modeling, pattern recognition, nanoinformatics, molecular structure, data integration, and workflow automation. The ABCC also provides dedicated support to NCI labs in genomics and bioinformatics analyses.
          JOB DESCRIPTION/BASIC FUNCTION
         This position description does not represent a current opening
           but may be used
         to identify candidates with skills and experience for positions within Leidos Biomedical Research that frequently become available. Candidates who express an interest may be considered for future positions at Leidos Biomedical Research.
         ABCC seeks a
          data scientist
         with focus in Machine Learning/Deep Learning to take advantage in the advances in machine learning to classify, organize, and derive new insights into the biological processes being explored by the plethora of omics technologies being deployed in the research oncology field.
          Basic function of this position includes:
          Provide domain knowledge in machine learning/deep learning and other areas such as cognitive learning as the field develops
          Aid in analyzing and interpreting multiple data sources, and transforming data to knowledge
          Explore, develop, implement, and deploy algorithms and software using techniques from machine learning/deep learning
          Collaborate with ABCC team members and apply machine learning related techniques, algorithms, and software tools to image analysis and recognition, natural language processing, systems biology, genotype/phenotype/disease correlations, and structure analysis
          Participate in NCI and ABCC sponsored educational and outreach activities to students, interns, and local education institutions
          Provide written and oral updates to peers and maintain best practices for planning and coordination purposes
          Qualifications:
          BASIC QUALIFICATIONS
          To be considered for this position, you must minimally meet the knowledge, skills, and abilities listed below:
          A Ph.D. from an accredited college/university according to the Council for Higher Education Accreditation (CHEA) in computer science or related field is required
          Foreign degrees must be evaluated for U.S. equivalency
          This position is subject to obtaining a Clearance
          Solid experience in machine learning with a focus on deep learning
          Hands on experience in algorithms and software development using techniques from machine learning/deep learning
          Communicate effectively through written and verbal means to NCI scientists, experts in the field, and DSITP/FNL management &#8211; including publishing in scientific journals
          PREFERRED QUALIFICATIONS
          Candidates with these desired skills will be given preferential consideration:
          Ability to manage multiple projects and prioritize workload to make sure established goals are met,
          Ability to work in a diverse environment and apply machine learning techniques to different scientific fields including and not limited to image analysis and recognition, natural language processing, systems biology, and structure analysis
          Hands on problem solving experience in one or more scientific fields mentioned above is desired
          Solid understanding of modern GPGPU programming and cluster based development is desired

        About the Agency
        This position description covers one Foreign Affairs Officer position in the Office of Analysis, Planning, Programs &amp; Learning for Africa, East Asia, and South Central Asia in the Bureau of Conflict and Stabilization Operations (CSO/APPL 2) in the U.S. Department of State (DOS).
        The work of the positions is focused on improving regional teams' analytical skills through the utilization of mixed methodologies, their ability to develop more effective narratives and the capacity to better convey their analyses through the use of data visualization products.
        The incumbent is responsible for providing sound analysis based on empirical evidence that is coupled with the visual depiction of data that will serve to identify conflict prevention and crisis response patterns and trends that better inform CSO policymakers. This position meets the Administrative Exemption.
        This position is eligible for telework; additional criteria will be required (e.g., supervisory approval).
        Duties
        Duties include but are not limited to the following:
        * Provides analytical and professional development support to CSO personnel, particularly the Bureau regional offices, concerning the identification, procurement, management and visualization of conflict-related data. Designs technical tools to improve the effectiveness and efficiency of conflict analysis efforts, and ensures dissemination of conflict and stabilization best practices through the development of customized Web-based opportunities for Bureau staff.
        * Further designs frontend Web-based dashboard applications to support knowledge sharing across CSO, the Department and on a government-wide basis.
        * Works effectively with such software as Hypertext Markup Language (HTML), Cascading Styles Sheets (CCS) and Javascript.
        * Leverages conflict and stabilization knowledge and utilization of advanced technologies to achieve rapid identification of key actors, information gaps and windows of opportunity as needed for effective delivery of the Bureau's conflict prevention and stabilization message.
        * Carries out data-driven strategic developments and structured formal analyses of CSO policies and programs through effective application of advanced data science and analytics.
        * Conducts in depth research and analysis of dynamics of conflict that are focused on such thematic issues as good governance and countering political violence in specific countries and regions.
        * Organizes and structures policy analyses through utilization of data-driven visualization techniques and trend identification processes.
        * Conducts research and analysis of dynamics of conflict in collaboration with analytical counterparts in the intelligence community, multinational organizations, conflict and stabilization subject matter experts in the U.S. and abroad, local constituencies and civil societies.
        * Draw sound conclusions and develop recommendations based on expert technical knowledge and application of qualitative and quantitative analyses.
        * Work effectively with such software as Hypertext Markup Language (HTML), Cascading Style Sheets (CSS)and Javascript .

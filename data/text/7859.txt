         PROGRAM DESCRIPTION
          The mission of the Data Science and Information Technology Program (DSITP) is to develop an enterprise-level, consolidated information technology infrastructure that provides exceptional IT capabilities to the Frederick National Labs for Cancer Research (NCI-Frederick/FNLCR) in support of basic, translational, and clinical cancer and AIDS research.&#160; The Advanced Biomedical Computing Center (ABCC) is a part of the DSITP within Leidos Biomed.&#160; The primary focus of ABCC is to support scientific research at NCI at Frederick, NCI-Bethesda, NIH, and other federal agencies through the Economy Act. The ABCC provides bioinformatics, systems biology, data integration and analysis, mathematical simulation and modeling, image analysis and visualization, nanoinformatics, proteomic analysis expertise, and web-enabled application delivery to these communities.
          The ClinOmics Program, a joint venture between the Genetics Branch and the Laboratory of Pathology, was launched by senior leadership of the Center for Cancer Research (National Cancer Institute) to establish a comprehensive translational genomics platform to enable precision therapy trials for adult and pediatric cancer patients at the NIH Clinical Center.
          JOB DESCRIPTION
           This position description does not represent a current opening but may be used to identify candidates with skills and experience for positions within Leidos Biomedical Research that frequently become available. Candidates who express an interest may be considered for future positions at Leidos Biomedical Research.
          LIMS Developer will: 1) Configure and maintain Clarity LIMS that tracks progress of clinical samples through wet-bench and computational protocols working with wet lab and bioinformatics team, 2) Work with ClinOmics Lead and wet lab personnel to perform basic admin duties to maintain Clarity LIMS, 3) Ensure LIMS system is updated with existing SOPs both wet lab and bioinformatics, 4) Ensure LIMS system launches pipeline, 5) Ensure that LIMS keeps track of reagents, 6) Ensure LIMS communicates with Labmatrix, Soft Path and CRIS system working with CCR IT, CBIIT, and CC Electronics Medical Records department, 7) Ensure BCL to FASTQ conversion is completed by pipeline, 8) Ensure all FASTQ is transferred to long term storage, TGen servers and Biowulf, 9) Ensure that level 3 data is backed up in long term storage, 10) Ensure data integrity, 11) Maintain detailed documentation of pipeline runs and validation/benchmarking projects, in accordance with CLIA standards, 12) Participate in weekly meetings, 13) Monitor Labmatrix, and genomics LIMS entries for all patients, 14) Work collaboratively with team members, and will ensure seamless transition of work when planning to be absent from the office.

          BASIC QUALIFICATIONS
          To be considered for this position, you must minimally meet the knowledge, skills, and abilities listed below:
          A Bachelor&#8217;s degree from an accredited college/university according to the Council for Higher Education Accreditation (CHEA) in Computer Science, Biostatistics, Bioinformatics or a Masters in Life Sciences, with significant quantitative skills, is required or four (4) years of related experience in lieu of education
          Foreign degrees must be evaluated for U.S. equivalency
          A minimum of two (2) years of progressively, responsible relevant experience as designated by the program requirements
          Experience in developing and maintaining Clarity LIMS in a clinical laboratory setting
          Tracking of work through project management programs
          Ability to be organized and keep detailed and accurate documentation
          Excellent oral and written communication skills

          Candidates with these desired skills will be given preferential consideration:
          Experience working in a CLIA/CAP environment
          Experience in working in both a research and production environment
          A solid understanding of biology, cancer and the molecular basis of human disease

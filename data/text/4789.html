OBJECTIVE:

The Associate Director, Content Manager has the primary responsibility of designing, building and managing the Takeda Global Data Hub and related content sources. This role is essential in the establishment of an open, well structures and easy to use knowledge base for Takeda.

ACCOUNTABILITIES:

* The definition, design and delivery of Takeda knowledge management capabilities

* Ongoing creation of new intellectual property to advance the enabling capabilities of the organization.

* to innovate in exciting areas including unstructured data, sentiment, identity resolution, malfeasance, and linguistics

* Drives solution formulation around the sources and uses of data to solve customer and internal challenges

* Collaborates with key internal stakeholders to identify unique opportunities to discover and exploit data assets using traditional and non-traditional methods of data synthesis.

* Design processes to innovate around large, complex data sets, including modeling and experiment design.

* Collaborate globally with key stakeholders and implementation partners on value assessment and use-case evolution.

* Contribute to the development of customer-facing material that communicates complex messages around data in a consumable and compelling way, including data visualization

* Contribute to the ongoing creation of intellectual property that advances the enabling capabilities of the organization, especially related to unstructured data

* Drive internal process change management to optimize data capture and standardization.

* Maintain quality system documents and communication collateral, including message crafting, authoring text, workflow visualization, review and integration of feedback to evangelize the value of data.

* Coordinate cross-functional collaboration with external vendors and with customers to gather information, prioritize projects, and proactively manage issues.

EDUCATION, BEHAVIORAL COMPETENCIES AND SKILLS:

* Demonstrated ability to build relationships across multiple organizations, in a multicultural environment

* Demonstrated strategic thinking and influence

* Must thrive in fast-paced environment with ability to multi-task and prioritize multiple projects with minimal supervision

* Demonstrated subject matter knowledge in data science with practical application experience

* Excellent written communication and presentation skills, multilingual preferred

* Strong understanding of problem formulation and articulating complex ideas

* Ability to quickly develop an understanding of advanced concepts in emerging data science in order to facilitate communication about the work of the group

* Strong skills in problem formulation and empirical process, including non-regressive methods for data analysis

* Familiarity with data analysis software tools (SAS, R, SQL), intelligent OCR/ICR systems, computer programming, statistics, data management, BI visualization tools.

* Advanced degree in engineering, life sciences or library science, PhD preferred

* Certification or extensive practical experience with life science coding systems (e.g. SDTM, ICD-9/10, MeSH, HL7, Sno CPT).

Required Qualifications:

* Bachelor's degree required, master's degree a plus. Degree concentration in a quantitative, analytic or business discipline preferred.

* Has operated successfully in similar roles, especially involving matrixed organizations requiring cross-company collaboration and disciplined delivery of initiatives.

* 5 years experience with data/data science

* Demonstrated experience in a multi-cultural/multinational setting

* Demonstrated critical competencies: problem solving/analysis, communication skills (oral/written), initiative leadership/influencing

* Proven track record of change management including process improvement and evaluation of new technology

* Excellent verbal and written communication skills including ability to constructively interface with all levels of management and technical staff.

* Advanced skill in MS Office, specifically PowerPoint, Excel and Word.

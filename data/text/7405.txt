        Technical Knowledge: Has a broad conceptual understanding of capabilities and direction of technology, coupled with an in-depth knowledge of the technology required and the needs of business environment necessary to support assigned systems and/or projects. Has a thorough understanding of staff responsibilities, abilities and development needs. Business Knowledge &amp; Partnership: Provides direction and effectively leads by example to ensure that all services are meeting the stated business objectives on a timely basis with excellent quality, at a reasonable cost. Keeps all areas informed of developments affecting their function. Demonstrates flexibility, anticipates customer needs, and effectively communicates in a manner consistent with the audience. Educates and influences customers on the most effective use of technology, from both a short-term and long-term perspective. Problem Solving &amp; Decision Making: Demonstrates advanced analytical and diagnostic skills dealing with issues that are often novel and not readily defined, lack known precedent or appear contradictory. Able to develop solutions by applying accepted processes or is able to create new approaches to leverage technology from abstract information. Sees the big picture, and is fully aware of technology and business directions. Has department, corporate and group objectives in focus while identifying and removing barriers. Optimizes the use of all available resources. Team Orientation: Maintains effective partnerships across the organization and is able to influence senior management, peers and subordinates through an inclusive style and recognition of their abilities and knowledge. Balances team and individual responsibilities. Exhibits objectivity and openness to others' views. Gives and welcomes feedback. Puts success of team above own interest. Leadership: Manages cross-functional objectives and/or priorities supporting an assigned business area(s). Responsibilities are assigned with considerable latitude for decision making. Results reviewed periodically according to predefined objectives. Sets level of risk taking, and gathers/synthesizes information to all appropriate levels of the organization. Planning and Project Management: Insures all planning done on the area level is supportive of the business strategic plans. Appropriately delegates while maintaining accountability; sees that projects are assigned by workload and capabilities. Completes projects accurately and on time. Change requires reassessing and adjusting tactical and/or operational direction. Accounts for all fiscal budgeting and expense control for area of responsibility. Demonstrates sound cost-effectiveness. Responsible for information of importance and whose disclosure could harm the overall financial position of the company. Human Resource Management: Maintains a work environment that stimulates the professional growth of all direct/indirect reports. Provides leadership by setting example for subordinates to follow. Responsible for the accurate and timely completion of all staff performance evaluations and development planning. Recruits and maintains effective staff members. Demonstrates a capacity to recognize, value and respect differences across a diverse work force.

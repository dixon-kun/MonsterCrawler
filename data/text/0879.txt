        About Barclays
        Barclays is a transatlantic consumer, corporate and investment bank offering products and services across personal, corporate and investment banking, credit cards and wealth management, with a strong presence in our two home markets of the UK and the US.
        With over 325 years of history and expertise in banking, Barclays operates in over 40 countries and employs approximately 130,000 people. Barclays moves, lends, invests and protects money for customers and clients worldwide. Please visit our website here for more information.
        Chief Security Office
        The Chief Security Office is a Group function which unifies the Cyber Security, Physical Security, Resilience, Investigations and Intelligence functions into a single organisation. CSO is part of the Operations and Technology function at Barclays. Please visit the O&amp;T website here for more information.
        Role Purpose
        The Security Innovation team's goal is to drive the right customer outcomes and protect the integrity of our financial services through innovation. We apply future oriented thinking to the trends in Security, IT and Fintech by:
        * Facilitating an innovation capability and culture within the Chief Security Office
        * Leading innovation initiatives to deliver new security capabilities within Barclays
        * Supporting the broader Open Innovation across Barclays
        Key Accountabilities
        Specific accountabilities
        * Drive change to address gaps and realise innovation opportunities
        * Own and develop innovation domains which could encompass specific stakeholders, verticals, technologies, cyber controls, etc.
        * Enhance CSO ability to identify and deliver next generation strategic control capabilities
        * Support the broader Barclays Open Innovation agenda in the form of Rise, the Barclays Accelerator, and other initiatives. Please visit the ThinkRise website here for more information
        Stakeholder management and leadership
        * Build relationships and contacts internal and external to Barclays with a view to surfacing new ideas and technologies
        * Facilitate an innovation capability and culture within CSO
        * Coach others in problem solving techniques and issues; to reach decisions under conditions of uncertainty or high risk
        * Ability to interact at all levels of the organization
        * Create and develop vendor relationships, influencing product roadmaps and innovation
        Decision making and problem solving
        * Identify and drive requirements and make recommendations for change
        * Deliver "secure by design" services by working with Architecture, Engineering teams &amp; 3rd party supplier management
        * Collaborate with and influence technology roadmaps across Design and Digital in support of developing enterprise security solutions
        * Engage with Barclays wide programmes and projects to ensure security risks are identified and managed in line with Barclays risk and control frameworks and business risk appetite
        * Be responsible for the provision of technical insight and support relating to changes in the threat environment, control viability and risk appetite and to ensure it is being appropriately identified and / or managed
        * Drive a greater understanding of the Group-wide threat environment
        Your Skills and Qualifications will include
        * 5 years of experience in a full time security role
        * 2 years in a Big Data role (e.g. Data Analyst, Data Engineer, Data Scientist, Data Modler, etc.) or AI role (e.g. Machine Learning Engineer)
        Preferred
        * Broad and comprehensive understanding of security concepts and principles across Cyber and Physical security
        * Experience with Big Data platforms and Machine Learning techniques
        * Broad (though not necessarily deep) knowledge of IT technology
        * Understanding of trends in IT / Security and can anticipate future directions
        * Proven ability to be innovative and demonstrate thought leadership in a start-up or enterprise environment
        * Ability to lead the delivery of major projects/programs; e.g. consults on project management issues or methodologies; promotes the use of business cases and upfront benefit realisation; draws upon lessons learned across the company; can provide decision making and leadership in ambiguous circumstances
        * Knowledge of threat technologies, architectures and business operating models
        * Good knowledge of cyber-crime trends and technical solutions, products and methodologies
        * Experience in creating and developing vendor relationships and influencing product roadmaps
        * Proven track record of influencing and challenging technology investments at senior levels
        * Experience of delivering benefits across different time horizons, aligned to an agreed strategy whilst balancing the risk of failure
        * Capability to evaluate complex technical products and weigh competing requirements in evaluation decisions
        * Strategic and commercial mind-set with some experience in commercial negotiations
        * Strong communication skills and the ability to constructively challenge the present way of working
        * Highly capable in managing own performance and that of the team
